Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the security news:

A series of security breaches and incidents have made headlines this week. LastPass, a password manager, has suffered yet another compromise due to a partner breach, resulting in the exposure of customer data including names, phone numbers, and email addresses. The breach occurred at Klue, an AI business intelligence firm, and affected multiple companies, including LastPass. Meanwhile, former Trump adviser John Bolton pleaded guilty to mishandling classified defense information and agreed to pay a fine of $2.25 million.

In other security news, Europol, Microsoft, and other partners have disrupted the infrastructure of two widely used infostealers, Amadey and StealC, which facilitate cybercrime. The operation, known as Operation Endgame, involved seizing and taking down malware infrastructure, including 326 servers and 142 domains, and recovered up to 27 million stolen access credentials. Additionally, Australia's Security and Intelligence Organisation (ASIO) has found nation-state hackers inside critical infrastructure, preparing for sabotage. ASIO is establishing teams to counter these threats and has warned of the risks of cyberattacks on critical infrastructure.

The security landscape is also being shaped by the rapid development of AI technology. Anthropic, a company developing AI models, has been granted permission by the White House to make its latest models available to select companies and government agencies. Meanwhile, OpenAI has launched an improved version of its GPT-5.5-Cyber model and a new initiative to support open-source projects on vulnerability patching and other security issues. As the AI arms race between China and the US escalates, experts are warning of the risks of a "Chernobyl moment" and the need for responsible development and deployment of AI technology.
