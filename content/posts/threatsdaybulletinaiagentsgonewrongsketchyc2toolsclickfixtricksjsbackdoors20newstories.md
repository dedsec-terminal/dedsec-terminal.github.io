Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the ThreatsDay Bulletin:

The internet remains a vulnerable and messy place, with bad plugins, old bugs, and fake tools posing significant security risks. Recently, Cisco released fixes for a high-severity security flaw in its Unified Communications Manager, which could have allowed unauthenticated remote attackers to conduct server-side request forgery (SSRF) attacks. Additionally, Russia's Federal Security Service (FSB) disclosed a large-scale mobile spyware operation targeting high-ranking officials, and the US Treasury announced sanctions against Iran's largest cryptocurrency exchange, Nobitex, for facilitating terrorist activities.

Threat actors are increasingly relying on social engineering tactics, such as layered keyloggers and JavaScript backdoors, to compromise systems and steal sensitive information. The takedown of a prominent Russian-speaking cybercrime forum, XSS, has led to the emergence of new, harder-to-track factions, including DamageLib, Rehub, and XSSF. Meanwhile, a threat cluster known as DriveSurge has been operating large-scale malware distribution campaigns using ClickFix and FakeUpdates social engineering techniques on compromised websites. Other notable threats include the use of Tiflux, a remote desktop tool, to establish persistence and transmit screenshots, and the exploitation of ROADtools, a Python-based open-source framework, to blend in with normal traffic and evade detection.

The use of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies is becoming more prevalent in cyberattacks, with unknown threat actors using AI to automate Active Directory discovery and refine endpoint detection and response (EDR) evasion tactics. This shift lowers the barrier to entry for sophisticated red team-style attacks, making it easier for attackers to develop and test evasion techniques. Other trends include the rise of data-only extortion campaigns, which target professional services, healthcare, and consumer services firms, and the use of on-chain malware delivery techniques, such as the EtherHiding technique, to route ClearFake payload delivery through smart contracts on the BNB Smart Chain testnet.
