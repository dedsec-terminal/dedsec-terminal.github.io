A cyberattack on 7-Eleven, a convenience store chain with over 86,000 stores in 19 countries, has exposed the personal information of approximately 185,000 people. The exposed data includes email addresses, names, physical addresses, dates of birth, and phone numbers, with some records containing additional information. The breach occurred when an unauthorized party gained access to certain 7-Eleven systems on April 8, 2026.

The incident was claimed by the ShinyHunters extortion gang, which alleged that it had stolen over 600,000 records and published a 9.4 GB archive of data after unsuccessful ransom negotiations. 7-Eleven's Chief Information Security Officer, Jim Kastle, confirmed the breach and apologized for any inconvenience caused, offering affected individuals identity theft protection services and CyberScan monitoring for up to 24 months. The company did not identify the threat actor behind the incident but took immediate action to investigate and notify those affected.

ShinyHunters has been linked to multiple high-profile incidents this year, including breaches at ADT, the European Commission, and Rockstar Games. The FBI has warned organizations and individuals against complying with the gang's extortion demands, noting that payment does not guarantee the deletion of stolen data or prevent future extortion attempts. In response to the breach, 7-Eleven has taken steps to protect affected individuals and prevent similar incidents in the future, emphasizing the importance of taking the security of personal information seriously.
