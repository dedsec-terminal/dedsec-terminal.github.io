A phishing attack on healthcare technology company Xsolis has resulted in a data breach affecting 1.4 million people. The company, which develops AI-powered software for hospitals and health systems, discovered the unauthorized activity on January 22, 2026, and immediately took action to contain the incident. An investigation with external cybersecurity experts was launched to determine the extent of the breach.

The investigation found that attackers gained access to certain files containing sensitive information, including names, addresses, dates of birth, health insurance information, Social Security numbers, and medical treatment information. Xsolis has reported the incident to law enforcement and implemented additional security measures to prevent similar incidents in the future. The company is also notifying potentially affected individuals by mail and has established a toll-free call center to provide support and answer questions.

Xsolis is offering free credit monitoring and identity protection services to eligible individuals who may have been affected by the breach. The company is urging those affected to remain vigilant against identity theft and fraud. This incident is the third cyber breach to affect a healthcare technology company in less than a month, following breaches at iRhythm Technologies and Novo Nordisk. At this time, no threat actor has publicly claimed responsibility for the attack on Xsolis.
