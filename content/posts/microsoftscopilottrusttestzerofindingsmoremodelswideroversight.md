Microsoft's Copilot has been recertified under ISO/IEC 42001:2023 for the second consecutive year, with zero non-conformities and zero improvement observations. This certification evaluates the AI management system in areas such as governance, risk assessment, and human oversight. The recertification demonstrates Microsoft's commitment to responsible AI development and deployment.

Since the first certification, Microsoft has made several changes to its Copilot system, including expanding its model portfolio to support a multi-model, multi-provider approach. The company has also streamlined its responsible AI assessment workflow and introduced a risk-tiered review model to strengthen pre-release risk evaluation. Additionally, Microsoft has added a structured harm-identification capability to its governance framework, which now covers three AI systems under a single certified management system.

Microsoft uses AI internally to support its governance framework, with AI agents assisting engineering teams with assessments and review processes. The company's governance and security approach has supported adoption in regulated industries, including finance, legal services, and government. Microsoft's General Manager, Oliver Bell, emphasized that the company is committed to evolving its AI management system with the technology, with the goal of continuous improvement and scaling its governance framework accordingly. The ISO 42001 recertification is seen as an annual proof point of this commitment.
