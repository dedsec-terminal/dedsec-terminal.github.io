Tidal Cyber has introduced Threat-Led Asset Visibility and Vulnerability Prioritization, a new innovation that extends its Threat-Led Defense platform. This advancement shifts the industry from static asset inventories and disconnected exposure management to an execution-centric model that focuses on how adversaries execute attacks. The platform connects assets, vulnerabilities, and threats through the lens of real adversary execution, allowing organizations to identify the most critical assets and vulnerabilities that enable attacks.

The Threat-Led Defense platform correlates assets, vulnerabilities, and defenses through the lens of real adversary execution, revealing how vulnerabilities enable attacks, which assets are operationally relevant, and where defensive controls can interrupt attacker progress. This approach enables organizations to prioritize vulnerabilities tied to active adversary tradecraft, control gaps impacting critical attack procedures, and defensive actions that reduce real operational risk. By focusing on the procedures used by adversaries, Tidal Cyber's platform provides a more effective way to defend against modern cyber threats.

The introduction of Threat-Led Asset Visibility and Vulnerability Prioritization marks a significant milestone in Tidal Cyber's mission to redefine how organizations understand, prioritize, and defend against modern cyber threats. By aligning defenses to the assets, vulnerabilities, and procedures attackers actually use to execute attacks, organizations can reduce the likelihood of attacker success across the entire attack lifecycle. The platform's focus on procedural-level intelligence, rather than inventory counts or severity scores, provides a more effective way to measure and reduce risk, and to prioritize defensive actions that truly matter.
