Intel 471 has introduced a new Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Maturity Pulse Check, a free self-assessment tool based on the Cyber Threat Intelligence Capability Maturity Model (CTI-CMM v1.3). This lightweight assessment allows organizations to evaluate their CTI program's current capabilities, identify areas for improvement, and prepare for a more comprehensive evaluation. The CTI Maturity Pulse Check is designed to be quick and easy to use, taking only 10-15 minutes to complete.

The assessment is a streamlined version of the CTI-CMM framework, which provides a clear roadmap for improving CTI programs. It distills each of the 11 CTI-CMM domains into a single representative question, with answers mapped to the model's maturity scale. The CTI Maturity Pulse Check is intended for practitioners, program leaders, and security executives, and provides a final evaluation that includes the highest priority gaps and five next steps to improve maturity. The results are categorized into four tiers: Emerging, Reactive, Operational, and Intelligence-led.

The goal of the CTI Maturity Pulse Check is to help CTI teams understand their current level of maturity and identify areas for improvement. According to Intel 471, 43% of CTI programs do not track maturity over time, and only 26% of CISOs feel that CTI drives strategic decisions. By establishing a maturity baseline, teams can make a data-backed case for budget and build cross-functional buy-in to influence strategic decisions. The CTI Maturity Pulse Check is designed to be a directional indicator, providing a clear vision of where a program stands today and encouraging productive internal conversations about where to focus and prioritize efforts.
