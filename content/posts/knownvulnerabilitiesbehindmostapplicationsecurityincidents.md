A recent survey of 902 IT and security professionals by the Cloud Security Alliance found that 80% of organizations experienced an application security incident in the past year due to a known vulnerability that had already been cataloged by their team. This suggests a structural issue in the industry, where the time between identifying a flaw and fixing it is long enough for attackers to exploit it. The National Vulnerability Database reported over 40,000 CVEs in 2025, and the use of AI-powered systems to generate exploits has further compressed the window for remediation.

Most organizations take between one and seven days to close critical and high-severity flaws, with very few managing to remediate within 24 hours. The speed of remediation is strongly correlated with outcomes, with organizations that take longer to close vulnerabilities more likely to experience incidents. Disagreements within the organization, concerns about disrupting application functionality, and lack of visibility into runtime behavior are common challenges that slow down the remediation process. Despite the use of pre-production tooling such as static application security testing and web application firewalls, 80% of respondents experienced at least one application security incident in the past year.

The survey also found that 70% of organizations run AI-powered application components in production, but runtime oversight of these components is limited. Most organizations lack real-time visibility into AI component behavior and rely on reconstructing what happened after an incident. To improve remediation, respondents requested capabilities such as proof of exploitability, containment without code changes, and visibility into code paths and data flows. There is a growing appetite for blocking production exploits with minimal false positives, but current deployments fall short of this standard. The survey suggests a shift in investment priorities towards runtime security, with 42% of organizations planning to increase investment in this area over the next 24 months.
