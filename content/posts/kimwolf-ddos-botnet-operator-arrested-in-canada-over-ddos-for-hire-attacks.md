A 23-year-old Canadian man, Jacob Butler, has been arrested and charged with operating a distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) botnet called Kimwolf. The botnet, a variant of AISURU, infected devices such as digital photo frames and web cameras, and was used to launch DDoS attacks on computers and servers worldwide, including US Department of Defense networks. Butler allegedly sold access to the infected devices to other cybercriminals, who used them to launch attacks. The botnet is estimated to have issued over 25,000 attack commands and was involved in record-setting DDoS attacks. Butler faces up to 10 years in prison if convicted. The arrest is part of a larger law enforcement operation that disrupted the command-and-control infrastructure of several DDoS botnets, including Kimwolf, and seized online services supporting 45 DDoS-for-hire platforms.
