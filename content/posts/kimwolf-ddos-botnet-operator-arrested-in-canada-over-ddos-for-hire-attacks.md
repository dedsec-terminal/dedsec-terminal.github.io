A 23-year-old Canadian man, Jacob Butler, has been arrested and charged with operating a distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) botnet known as Kimwolf. The botnet, a variant of AISURU, infected devices such as digital photo frames and web cameras, and was used to launch DDoS attacks on computers and servers worldwide, including those belonging to the US Department of Defense. Butler allegedly sold access to the infected devices to other cybercriminals, who used them to launch attacks. The botnet is estimated to have issued over 25,000 attack commands and was involved in record-setting DDoS attacks. Butler faces up to 10 years in prison if convicted. The arrest is part of a larger law enforcement operation that has disrupted the command-and-control infrastructure of several DDoS botnets and has resulted in the seizure of online services supporting 45 DDoS-for-hire platforms.
