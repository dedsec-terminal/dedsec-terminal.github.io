LastPass, a password management company, has disclosed a data breach that exposed customer information through a supply chain attack on Klue, a market intelligence platform. The attackers used compromised OAuth tokens to access customer data stored in LastPass's Salesforce environment. The exposed data includes standard business contact information, such as names, phone numbers, email addresses, and physical addresses, as well as CRM records and sales-related data.

The breach was limited to systems integrated with Klue's platform and did not affect LastPass's products, services, infrastructure, or customer vaults. LastPass has warned customers to be cautious of phishing or social engineering attacks using the exposed contact information and has taken steps to mitigate the breach, including revoking employee access to Klue, rotating API tokens, and launching an investigation. The company has also notified law enforcement and released indicators of compromise.

The breach is part of a larger incident affecting multiple companies, including cybersecurity vendors Huntress, Recorded Future, Tanium, and Jamf, which have all disclosed their involvement. An extortion group known as "Icarus" has claimed responsibility for the attack and is likely to continue posting compromised data and pressuring affected organizations to pay a ransom. Klue has acknowledged the breach and is working with affected customers to respond to the incident, but has not indicated whether it plans to negotiate with the hackers.
