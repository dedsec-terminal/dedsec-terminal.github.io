The US Department of Justice (DoJ) has announced the results of a major operation to combat cyber-enabled and cryptocurrency fraud targeting Americans. The "Disruption Week" operation, which began on May 18, 2026, involved government authorities and private sector companies working together to disrupt transnational cybercrime groups in Southeast Asia. The operation resulted in the takedown of millions of social media, email, and internet access accounts used by scammers, as well as the freezing of over $3.8 million in cryptocurrency assets.

The operation is part of the US government's Scam Center Strike Force initiative, which aims to dismantle transnational criminal organizations running cyber-enabled fraud and "pig butchering" scams from compounds in Southeast Asia. These scams typically involve cultivating relationships with prospective victims over time before coaxing them into depositing funds into fraudulent investment platforms. The operation involved several major private sector companies, including Apple, Coinbase, Google, and Microsoft, as well as law enforcement agencies from several countries, including Australia, Canada, New Zealand, Thailand, and the UK.

The operation has resulted in significant disruptions to criminal activity, including the disruption of over 1.4 million accounts, pages, and groups across Facebook and Instagram, and the identification of multiple scammers and scam platforms. The DoJ has reported that cryptocurrency investment scams have emerged as one of the fastest-growing and most financially devastating forms of fraud targeting Americans, with reported losses rising to over $7.2 billion in 2025. The operation highlights the importance of international cooperation and collaboration between government agencies and private sector companies in combating transnational cybercrime.
