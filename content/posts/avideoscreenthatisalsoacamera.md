Researchers at ETH Zurich in Switzerland have developed a new type of pixel, called a Fourier pixel, that can both generate and sense light fields. This innovative pixel can manipulate light's intensity, oscillation phases, and polarization, allowing it to carry more information. The team's findings were published in a paper in Nature, marking a significant breakthrough in technology.

The development of this pixel brings to mind the concept of a telescreen from George Orwell's 1984, which could both receive and transmit information simultaneously. The telescreen could pick up sounds and visuals, allowing for constant surveillance. While the current technology is not yet at this level, it raises concerns about the potential for increased surveillance and data collection.

The commenter notes that this technology is still opt-in, but expresses concern that people will willingly connect their devices to the internet without considering the privacy implications. They also criticize the lack of data privacy legislation, suggesting that without proper regulations, people's personal data will be vulnerable to exploitation. The commenter warns that the masses will "blindly" adopt this technology, leading to a loss of privacy.
