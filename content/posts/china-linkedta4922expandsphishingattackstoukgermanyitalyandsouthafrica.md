A new China-linked cybercrime group, known as TA4922, has expanded its targeting focus to include European organizations in the UK, Germany, Italy, and South Africa. This group is characterized as a Chinese-speaking threat actor that is largely financially motivated, with a focus on obtaining remote access to victim environments for financial gain. TA4922's tradecraft is more focused on cybercriminal objectives than espionage, and it is assessed to share some level of overlap with the Silver Fox threat actor.

TA4922's attacks have relied on phishing campaigns using human resources- and business-themed lures for credential phishing, fraud, and malware delivery. The group has used various malware families, including ValleyRAT, Atlas RAT, RomulusLoader, and SilentRunLoader, to deliver malware and steal sensitive data. Notably, the group has attempted to move conversations from emails to out-of-band communication channels like LINE, WhatsApp, and Microsoft Teams, allowing them to bypass enterprise security controls and steal data or deliver malware.

Recent phishing campaigns attributed to TA4922 have targeted organizations in Japan, the UK, Germany, and Southeast Asia, using various lures such as human resources-related themes, corporate themes, and tax authority-related themes. The group's malware capabilities include the potential for surveillance, which could be used by or sold to espionage groups. Proofpoint warns that organizations should be aware of emerging and complex threats like TA4922, as they can quickly expand and scale their tactics to include more targets at any time, regardless of geographic targeting.
