Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The cybersecurity landscape is becoming increasingly challenging, with security tools finding bugs faster than humans can fix them. However, attackers also have access to these tools, which they use to exploit vulnerabilities. This week's threats include a credible external security threat to ShareFile customers, a critical Zimbra flaw, and a compromised Jscrambler npm package. Additionally, a new post-compromise backdoor called GigaWiper has been detailed, which can render a machine inoperable in three distinct ways.

Other notable threats include the SHELLSTORM operation, which has targeted over 1.4 million domains by exploiting WordPress plugins, and the HalluSquatting technique, which tricks AI coding assistants into installing botnets. The gap between patch existence and exploitation is shrinking, with many high-severity vulnerabilities being actively exploited in the wild. Some of the notable CVEs include those affecting U-Boot, Ubiquiti Unifi, BeyondTrust Remote Support, and Linux Kernel. It is essential to patch these vulnerabilities as soon as possible to prevent exploitation.

The article also highlights the importance of treating AI infrastructure as part of the critical attack surface and not as a standalone application tier. Threat actors have been observed compromising AI gateways for cryptomining, and exploitation of CVE-2026-1207 has been reported. Additionally, a multi-stage infection has been discovered, which leads to a Node.js backdoor, and intrusions are exploiting Citrix Bleed 2 to deploy the DragonForce ransomware. To stay ahead of these threats, it is crucial to prioritize security and implement measures such as least-privilege access, short-lived secrets, and secure-by-default strategies.
