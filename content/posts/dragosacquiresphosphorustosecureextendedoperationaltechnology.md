Dragos, a leading OT cybersecurity platform, has acquired Phosphorus, a company that specializes in securing connected devices. This acquisition extends the Dragos Platform to protect billions of devices embedded across critical infrastructure and operational networks. The move is in response to the growing complexity of operational environments, which now include a diverse mix of connected devices and digital systems that have outgrown traditional OT boundaries.

The acquisition is part of Dragos's strategy to protect the full operational environment, known as Extended Operational Technology (xOT). xOT includes traditional and non-traditional assets, such as power grids, pipelines, and data centers, which are increasingly dependent on connected devices. Adversaries are already operating in this environment, and defenders need a broader scope of visibility, intelligence, and control to defend it. With Phosphorus, Dragos can now offer deeper device visibility, automated remediation, and continuous risk reduction across the full xOT environment.

The integration of Phosphorus into the Dragos Platform will provide customers with expanded asset visibility and integrated device intelligence. Phosphorus's discovery and remediation platform will be integrated into the Dragos Platform, allowing for automated remediation workflows and a unified platform experience. The acquisition builds on Dragos's previous acquisition of Network Perception, which added OT network visibility and segmentation validation to the platform. With Phosphorus, Dragos can now secure both the network architecture and the devices running on it, providing a comprehensive OT cybersecurity solution.
