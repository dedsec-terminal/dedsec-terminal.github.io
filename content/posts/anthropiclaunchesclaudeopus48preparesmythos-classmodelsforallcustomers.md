Anthropic has released Claude Opus 4.8, the latest version of its AI model, which is available to all users with unchanged pricing from the previous version, Opus 4.7. The new model features improvements in honesty, with a higher likelihood of acknowledging when it lacks sufficient information and a lower likelihood of making unsupported claims. According to Anthropic, Opus 4.8 has shown a significant reduction in deceptive behavior and misalignment, with evaluations indicating it is around four times less likely to overlook flaws in generated code.

The release of Opus 4.8 also introduces Dynamic Workflows, a feature available in research preview that enables Claude to take on larger tasks in Claude Code. This feature allows Claude to plan work, run multiple subagents, and verify outputs before returning results to the user. Dynamic Workflows is available for users on Enterprise, Team, and Max plans. Additionally, the release includes controls that allow users to adjust the effort spent on a task, enabling them to balance response speed, reasoning depth, and cost.

Anthropic has also announced plans to make its Mythos-class models available to all customers in the coming weeks, once additional cyber safeguards are in place. These models are currently being tested by a limited number of organizations and are expected to surpass Opus in intelligence. The company aims to provide models with similar capabilities to Opus at a lower cost, indicating ongoing efforts to develop and release more advanced and affordable AI models. With the release of Opus 4.8 and the upcoming availability of Mythos-class models, Anthropic is continuing to advance its AI capabilities and expand access to its technology.
