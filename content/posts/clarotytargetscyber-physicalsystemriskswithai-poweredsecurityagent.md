Claroty has introduced Claroty Claire, an AI-powered security agent designed to protect cyber-physical systems (CPS) from increasing risks. The agent is powered by a CPS language model trained on over a decade of industry expertise and data, enabling organizations to defend their mission-critical infrastructure. This launch expands organizations' capabilities to support the safety, uptime, and availability of CPS.

The need for proactive security measures has become urgent, as the attack surface of CPS is rapidly expanding due to the growing use of AI and robotics. According to Goldman Sachs, the market for humanoid robots is projected to reach $38 billion by 2035, with over 250,000 shipments expected in 2030. AI has also accelerated the threat lifecycle, making it essential for organizations to adopt intelligent and contextual security solutions that can deliver trustworthy insights and swift actions.

The Claroty platform with Claire offers CPS-native, AI-powered protection, trained on the company's deep domain expertise in various sectors. The tool can reduce risk, improve operational resilience, and achieve continuous compliance by proactively prioritizing and orchestrating remediation of exposures. With Claire, organizations can leverage automated asset mapping, research-backed device understanding, and OEM-approved patch levels to maintain the safety and uptime of mission-critical environments. According to Yaniv Vardi, CEO at Claroty, Claire empowers human operators to make decisions with confidence, based on tailored insights and trustworthy actions.
