The FBI and CISA have updated their warning about Russian intelligence hackers targeting Signal and WhatsApp accounts, specifically focusing on Signal's Backup Recovery Key. The attackers, linked to Russian Intelligence Services, use phishing messages posing as Signal support to trick targets into handing over their Recovery Key. This allows the attackers to restore the account's backup, read private and group message history, and take over the account.

The targets of this campaign are individuals of high intelligence value, including current and former government officials, military personnel, and journalists. The phishing messages are designed to appear legitimate, with the attackers walking the target through the process of turning on Signal backups and pasting the Recovery Key into the chat. The FBI and CISA emphasize that this attack does not compromise Signal's encryption, but rather uses social engineering to exploit individual accounts.

To protect themselves, users are advised to treat any in-app message from "Signal support" as hostile and never paste their Backup Recovery Key, verification code, or PIN into a chat. Users should also check their Linked Devices and remove any unrecognized devices. If a user believes they have handed over their Recovery Key, they should generate a new one in Settings and assume any previous backup is compromised. The State Department's Rewards for Justice program is offering up to $10 million for information on the group behind this campaign, known as UNC5792.
