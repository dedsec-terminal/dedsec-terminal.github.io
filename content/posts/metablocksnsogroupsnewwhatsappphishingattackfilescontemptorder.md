Meta has detected and blocked a new spear-phishing attack linked to the Israeli spyware vendor NSO Group, which targeted WhatsApp users. The attack involved tricking people into clicking on malicious links that would drive them to external websites outside of WhatsApp. Meta has also filed a federal court contempt order against NSO Group for violating a permanent injunction that barred it from targeting WhatsApp and its users.

The malicious activity included creating test accounts and groups on WhatsApp, which have since been taken down by Meta. The company has identified several malicious domains linked to the activity, including fr24cast.com, ghazacast.com, and ikhwancast.com. This development comes a year after NSO Group was fined $168 million for exploiting WhatsApp servers to deploy Pegasus spyware, targeting over 1,400 individuals globally. The company was also added to a US Commerce Department blocklist in 2021 for engaging in activities contrary to US national security interests.

Meta has reassured users that their personal messages and calls remain protected with default end-to-end encryption. The company encourages users to keep their apps and devices up to date and report suspicious activity. Additionally, users who believe they may be at elevated risk of sophisticated cyber attacks can enable strict account settings to harden their accounts. This feature reduces the attack surface by locking the account to more private settings, such as turning on two-step verification and limiting who can see their online status and profile information.
