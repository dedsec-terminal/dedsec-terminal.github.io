Scammers are impersonating FBI agents to target individuals who have already been scammed, using the guise of handling Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) complaints. The IC3 has published an update warning of these scams, which have evolved to include new tactics such as AI-generated videos of FBI officials and spoofed versions of the IC3 website. The scammers use various methods to initiate contact with victims, including emails, phone calls, social media, and forums.

The scammers' tactics include posing as FBI agents on Facebook Messenger and Telegram, sending links that may contain malicious code or collect financial data to revictimize the person. They also use deepfake videos of senior FBI officials to promote a spoofed IC3 site that mimics the design of the real site. The fake site accepts a single-step complaint form and promises to follow up, but its purpose is to collect more information from the victim. The FBI warns that these scams can lead to further financial loss and identity theft.

To protect themselves, individuals should be cautious when interacting with anyone claiming to be an FBI agent, especially if they initiate contact or ask for payment to recover lost funds. The FBI advises typing the IC3 website directly into the browser, verifying the domain ends in .gov, and being wary of sponsored search results. Additionally, individuals should be skeptical of videos or messages with distorted features, lag, or unnatural movement, and verify their authenticity through reputable news sources or official channels. By being vigilant and taking the time to verify information, individuals can avoid falling victim to these scams.
