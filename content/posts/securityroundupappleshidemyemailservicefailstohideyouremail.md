Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the security roundup:

A recent investigation has revealed that Apple's Hide My Email service, designed to protect users' email addresses, has a vulnerability that allows real email addresses to be uncovered. Security researcher Tyler Murphy discovered the flaw in June 2025 and reported it to Apple, but the issue remains unfixed. Tests have shown that 100% of Hide My Email addresses are exploitable, allowing attackers to link the hidden email address to the user's real email address.

In other security news, a 19-year-old alleged member of the Scattered Spider hacking group has been extradited to the US to face charges. The group has been responsible for hacking into several businesses, including a luxury jewelry retailer, and demanding cryptocurrency ransoms. Meanwhile, WhatsApp's introduction of usernames has been met with opposition from Indian officials, who claim it could increase fraud and cybercrime. The Indian government has asked WhatsApp to pause the rollout of usernames in the country.

Additionally, an investigation has found that automatic license plate reader cameras (ALPRs) have made mistakes that have led to innocent people being detained by law enforcement officials. At least 24 cases of misidentification have been reported over the last eight years, including instances of cameras misreading license plates or failing to remove plates from wanted lists. The findings highlight the need for improved accuracy and oversight of ALPR systems to prevent false arrests and protect citizens' rights. Other security concerns, such as the targeting of a European politician with Pegasus malware and the use of chatbots to gather information on high-risk subjects, have also been reported.
