Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The development and deployment of autonomous military capabilities are accelerating rapidly, driven by investments from the US, UK, and NATO. As a result, the focus is shifting from individual autonomous systems to the trusted information infrastructure that enables them to operate together seamlessly. This infrastructure must facilitate the secure and efficient exchange of information across different platforms, domains, and coalition partners to support connected missions.

The momentum behind military autonomy is undeniable, with significant investments and dedicated leadership to accelerate unmanned capability development. The US, UK, and NATO are all prioritizing autonomous systems, with programs expanding autonomous testing and experimentation. However, the conversation around military autonomy must shift from just deploying autonomous systems to enabling them to operate together with confidence. This requires trusted information infrastructure that can move securely and efficiently across the entire operational architecture.

To achieve this, defense organizations need to rethink how trust is established across mission-critical environments. Hardware-enforced separation (hardsec) can provide a foundation for trusted information infrastructure, aligning with the speed and scale of autonomous transformation. Companies like Everfox are providing purpose-built information platforms that enable autonomous and coalition missions to securely exchange mission-critical information. By prioritizing trusted information infrastructure, defense organizations can rapidly adopt autonomous capability while maintaining the speed, interoperability, and assurance modern missions demand.
