OpenAI has introduced an optional Advanced Account Security feature to provide an extra layer of protection for at-risk accounts, particularly those belonging to individuals who handle sensitive information, such as journalists, researchers, and elected officials. This feature enforces strict access controls, making it more difficult for attackers to take over accounts. By enabling Advanced Account Security, users can significantly reduce the risk of successful phishing attacks by using two physical security keys or passkeys instead of regular passwords.

The Advanced Account Security feature also eliminates email and SMS texts as account recovery routes, instead requiring users to use recovery keys, backup passkeys, or physical security keys. Additionally, OpenAI has partnered with Yubico to offer lower-cost YubiKey bundles to users who enable this feature. To further enhance security, OpenAI's support team will no longer have access or control over account recovery options, preventing attackers from targeting support portals with social engineering attacks.

The Advanced Account Security feature includes several other security measures, such as shorter sign-in windows and sessions, alerts for login activity, and exclusion from model training by default. These measures are designed to provide an additional layer of protection for users who require enhanced security. OpenAI has also announced that members of its Trusted Access for Cyber program will be required to enable Advanced Account Security or implement phishing-resistant authentication through an enterprise single sign-on mechanism starting June 1.

The introduction of Advanced Account Security is part of OpenAI's broader cybersecurity strategy, which aims to provide a range of protections for its users. As AI services become increasingly mainstream, the need for robust security measures has become more pressing. By offering Advanced Account Security, OpenAI is providing users with a higher level of protection against account takeover attacks and other security threats. This move is likely to be welcomed by users who handle sensitive information and require enhanced security measures to protect their accounts.
