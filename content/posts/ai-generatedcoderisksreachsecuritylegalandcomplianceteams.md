Here is a summary of the article in 3 concise paragraphs:

The use of AI-generated code is becoming increasingly common in engineering organizations, with nearly half of companies running it in production. A survey by Flux found that almost every company uses AI somewhere in development, with under 5% reporting no plans to adopt it within a year. Teams tend to use AI for repetitive tasks such as writing documentation and filling out unit tests, where the stakes are low and mistakes are easy to catch.

However, as the stakes rise, teams become more cautious about deploying AI-generated code. To mitigate risks, companies are investing in safeguards such as code quality analysis, automated code review, and software composition analysis. Despite these efforts, code review remains a significant challenge, with many developers already spending a large portion of their time on review. The use of AI-generated code can exacerbate this problem, as it often requires more time and effort to review.

The survey also highlighted concerns about the impact of AI-generated code on junior developers, with over 40% of organizations citing lost learning opportunities. To address these challenges, companies are adjusting their development and release processes, implementing new policies and training programs, and investing in tools to lower the risk of AI-generated code. Many respondents believe that AI can beat humans at code review in some respects, and are willing to spend money on tools that can help mitigate the risks associated with AI-generated code.
