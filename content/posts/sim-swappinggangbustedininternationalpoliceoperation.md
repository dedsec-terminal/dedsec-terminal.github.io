An international police operation has led to the arrest of four suspected members of a cybercrime group accused of SIM swap attacks, cryptocurrency theft, and money laundering. The operation was conducted by Poland's Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime (CBZC) in collaboration with the US Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) and Homeland Security Investigations (HSI).

The investigation revealed that the group used specialized software and social engineering to gain unauthorized access to IT systems, including those of telecommunications operators and employee email accounts. This allowed them to carry out SIM swap attacks, taking control of victims' phone numbers and accessing their SMS messages and email accounts. The group then used this access to take over cryptocurrency exchange accounts and transfer digital assets to their control.

The suspects have been charged with participating in an organized criminal group, breaking into computer systems, and money laundering, and could face prison sentences of up to 25 years if convicted. The total value of the funds allegedly laundered by the group is estimated to exceed tens of millions of zlotys. The investigation is ongoing, and the international nature of the case limits the amount of information that can be disclosed at this stage. All four suspects have been ordered into pretrial detention.
