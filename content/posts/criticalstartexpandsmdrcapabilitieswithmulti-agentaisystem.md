Critical Start has introduced SOC AI, a multi-agent AI system that enhances its Managed Detection and Response (MDR) capabilities. This system consists of ten specialized agents that work together to cover the entire alert investigation and response lifecycle, including detection, triage, response, threat hunting, and continuous improvement. Each agent has a discrete function, a defined scope, and a complete audit trail, ensuring that speed and efficiency do not compromise accountability.

The SOC AI system has already shown significant results, with the Investigation Agent enhancing thousands of investigations and reducing analyst time-to-investigate to just 10 minutes. The system generates investigation output in seconds, and its deterministic foundation ensures that it can continue to operate at full capacity even if the AI is unavailable. This combination of AI-driven speed, resilience, and accountability allows Critical Start to offer contractual Service Level Agreements (SLAs) that are unmatched in the market.

The SOC AI system includes both customer-facing and platform agents, each with specific functions. Customer-facing agents include the Investigation Agent, Case Agent, Threat Hunt Agent, and Response Agent, which work together to investigate and respond to alerts. Platform agents, such as the TBR Agent and AI Engineering Agent, operate behind the scenes to improve the system's performance and reduce false positives. The system's transparent, auditable, and operationally resilient design has established a new benchmark for responsible AI in security operations, according to Critical Start's CEO, Scott White.
