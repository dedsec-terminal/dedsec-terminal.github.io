Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the situation:

Armenia has detained a Russian tourist named Aleksandr Ermakov on a US extradition request for a REvil ransomware suspect with the same name. However, Ermakov's lawyers claim that the US has the wrong man. The US is seeking Aleksandr Gennadievich Ermakov, who was sanctioned by Australia, the US, and the UK in January 2024 for stealing 9.7 million records from an Australian health insurer. This individual is currently serving a two-year sentence in Russia and is barred from leaving the country.

The detained Ermakov, Aleksandr Yuryevich Ermakov, is a former prison-service lawyer from Omsk who does not speak English. His lawyers argue that the US has mistakenly identified him due to a lack of information, including his patronymic, which is a key distinguishing factor in Russian names. The US charging document and Interpol notice accuse Ermakov of participating in REvil attacks from 2019 to 2021, with over 1,000 victims, including private companies, law enforcement, and government offices.

The case has sparked confusion, with Ermakov's lawyers claiming that the US paperwork only included a given name and surname, without a patronymic, leading to an automated mix-up. The Armenian authorities have not commented on the case, and the US Justice Department has not announced any charges. Ermakov is being held on a 30-day Interpol detention order, while Moscow has requested consular access. The case highlights the complexities of international law enforcement and the potential for mistaken identities in cybercrime cases.
