Censys has expanded its Internet Map to include real-time DNS visibility, allowing security teams to seamlessly connect domains, names, and internet infrastructure. This integration enables analysts to understand the names associated with internet infrastructure, replacing the need for multiple datasets and investigative interfaces with a single cohesive platform. The Censys Platform now brings together domains, DNS records, IPs, hosts, services, and certificates in a single unified view.

The unified view provided by Censys enables faster and more accurate security decisions across every stage of the security operations workflow, including triage, investigation, hunting, and defense. Security teams can validate suspicious domains, pivot between domains and IPs, and understand how infrastructure has evolved over time. This allows them to make more informed decisions and respond to threats more effectively. The integration of DNS data into the Internet Map also enables analysts to start from a single indicator and uncover the broader adversary infrastructure behind an attack.

Censys customers are already using the new DNS capabilities to identify and disrupt phishing campaigns at the infrastructure level. By uncovering the broader campaign infrastructure behind threats, defenders can strengthen their defenses before attacks happen. According to Raj Sivasankar, Senior Director of Product Management at Censys, the integration of DNS into the Internet Map provides defenders with a unified platform to understand the internet infrastructure behind threats, enabling them to make better security decisions and uncover broader adversary campaigns. This is particularly important as attacks become increasingly automated, and security teams need to make critical decisions quickly and accurately.
