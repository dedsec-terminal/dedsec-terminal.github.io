Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

Hackers have allegedly leaked stolen data from Madison Square Garden, including millions of records and potential personal information from customers. The hacking group ShinyHunters published the data, which includes references to Knicks players and coaches, just after the team won their first NBA championship since 1973. This incident highlights the vulnerability of organizations to cyber attacks and the potential consequences of data breaches. Additionally, Madison Square Garden has been using face-recognition systems, which have raised concerns about surveillance and privacy.

In other news, several gay bars in San Francisco's Castro District have been using face scanners to collect detailed information on customers, including facial images, names, and genders. This technology has raised concerns about surveillance and data collection, particularly in the LGBTQ community. Meanwhile, France's domestic spy agency has announced that it will stop using Palantir's data and AI tools, citing surveillance and security risks, and will instead use software from a French firm. This move is part of a broader trend of European governments and companies ditching US technology in favor of local alternatives.

Apple is also making changes to its private email tool, "Hide My Email", which allows users to generate random email addresses to sign up to websites and apps privately. The company plans to change the domain used for these email addresses from @icloud.com to @private.icloud.com, which could make it easier for companies to detect when people are using the service. This change has raised concerns about the effectiveness of the tool in preserving user privacy. Additionally, the UK will soon begin scanning the faces of asylum-seekers as part of age checks, despite evidence that such tools are flawed and can have life-altering consequences.
