A secret society called Dialog, co-founded by billionaire Peter Thiel in 2006, has had its internal records exposed online. The leak reveals the names of participants in its events, including US officials, foreign government figures, and Silicon Valley executives. The group, which convenes annually for off-the-record retreats, has spent two decades declining to disclose its members. The exposed records include a directory of members and a registration list for the 2026 retreat, which is scheduled to take place in Ireland.

The leaked records show an extraordinary convergence of power, with attendees including General Alexus Grynkewich, NATO's supreme allied commander Europe, and sitting Trump administration officials, as well as founders and directors of major surveillance, data-broker, and advertising-data companies. The records also reveal the interests and preoccupations of the group's members, including artificial intelligence, longevity, and the near future. Members were asked to predict the future and returned to themes such as the impact of AI on work and society. The leak also exposes sensitive personal details, including political leanings and matchmaking preferences.

The leak has raised concerns about the secrecy and exclusivity of the group, which has been likened to a tech-industry version of the Bilderberg gathering. Dialog's website and registration process have been criticized for their lack of security and transparency. The group's executive director, Raffi Grinberg, did not respond to requests for comment. The leak has also sparked questions about the potential conflicts of interest and influence that may arise from the convergence of powerful figures from politics, finance, and tech in a private and secretive setting. None of the individuals named in the leak have responded to requests for comment.
