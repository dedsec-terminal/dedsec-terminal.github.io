The Linux Foundation has launched Akrites, a new security framework designed to support critical open-source projects in handling security vulnerabilities. This initiative brings together technology companies, financial institutions, security vendors, AI companies, and open-source projects to establish a common process for addressing security issues in software used across critical infrastructure and enterprise environments. The goal of Akrites is to provide a shared approach to vulnerability response, ensuring that security issues are addressed quickly and effectively.

Akrites establishes a shared Security Incident Response Team (SIRT) and a Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (CVD) process, allowing participating organizations to use common workflows and industry-standard tools to exchange vulnerability information, manage remediation, and coordinate disclosures. The project focuses on software used in sectors such as finance, healthcare, and telecommunications, which are often maintained by small teams despite being used by thousands of organizations. Founding members of Akrites include major companies such as Amazon Web Services, Google, IBM, and Microsoft, who will contribute expertise, resources, and AI technologies to help identify and fix vulnerabilities.

The launch of Akrites is driven by the increasing risk of security vulnerabilities due to the accelerating pace of AI-powered vulnerability discovery and exploit development. The initiative builds on existing Linux Foundation security efforts, such as the Open Source Security Foundation (OpenSSF) and Alpha-Omega, which have demonstrated the effectiveness of industry collaboration in strengthening open-source security. Akrites provides operational support from vulnerability reporting through public disclosure, and organizations that can contribute engineering resources, security expertise, or funding are invited to participate in the initiative. By working together, the goal of Akrites is to ensure that vulnerabilities are addressed at the speed required to protect critical infrastructure and enterprise environments.
