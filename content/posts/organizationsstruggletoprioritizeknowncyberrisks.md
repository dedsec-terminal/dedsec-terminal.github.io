Organizations are struggling to prioritize known cyber risks despite collecting more data than ever. According to the State of Threat Management report from Filigran, 93% of organizations face challenges in maintaining an accurate view of their attack surface, and only 41% have a consolidated view of their cyber risk exposure. This is due to the use of disconnected tools, leaving important context spread across multiple systems, including cloud infrastructure, on-premises environments, and third-party services.

Threat intelligence is widely used, with 99% of organizations using it in their security operations centers. However, only 45% report that it is integrated and operationalized, and managing information from multiple sources remains a manual process. This leads to difficulties in determining which risks require immediate attention, with 97% of organizations reporting challenges in determining whether exposures are exploitable. As a result, analysts spend a significant amount of time investigating risks that later prove to be low priority or not exploitable, accounting for 42% of their working week.

The adoption of Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM) programs is changing assessment practices, with organizations using a different mix of cyber risk assessment tools. Automation is expected to play a larger role in exposure management, with AI currently supporting about one-third of exposure management activities and expected to expand significantly. Regional differences also appear, with North America reporting the highest share of organizations with a consolidated view of cyber risk exposure and the highest use of threat intelligence within a continuous, automated validation process. Overall, organizations need to connect threat intelligence directly to exposure validation and risk reduction in a continuous workflow to improve their cyber risk management.
