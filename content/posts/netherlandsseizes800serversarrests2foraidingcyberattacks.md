Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The Netherlands has arrested two men, Andrey Nesterenko (39) and Youssef Zinad (57), for operating IT infrastructure used by Russia to carry out cyberattacks, influence operations, and disinformation campaigns within the European Union. The men are co-owners of two related internet hosting companies, MIRhosting and WorkTitans BV, which were found to be connected to Stark Industries Solutions, an internet service provider sanctioned by the EU for its role in Russian cyber mischief. The Dutch financial crime agency, FIOD, seized over 800 servers, laptops, and telephones during the arrest.

The investigation revealed that MIRhosting and WorkTitans provided internet connectivity to Stark Industries, which was used to launch distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks against European targets. The companies were also found to be involved in proxy and anonymity services linked to Russia-backed hacking groups. Despite EU sanctions against related entities, MIRhosting and WorkTitans continued to operate, with Nesterenko denying any knowledge of his servers being misused by pro-Russian cybercriminals. However, data reviewed by de Volkskrant showed that the companies' networks were used in pro-Russian attacks on Danish government bodies during the country's municipal elections.

Nesterenko, a Russian native and piano prodigy, founded MIRhosting's parent company in 2004, which has a history of hosting hacktivist websites, including one used to organize cyberattacks against Georgia in 2008. He claims that MIRhosting does not support cybercrime or sanctions evasion and that the allegations against him and his company are harmful. Zinad, on the other hand, has kept a low profile, and little is known about him. The arrest and seizure of assets mark a significant step in the EU's efforts to disrupt Russian cyber operations and hold those involved accountable.
