Incode has introduced On-Device Age Estimation, a new age verification capability that processes age estimation and liveness detection directly on the user's device. This means that facial data is not transmitted off the device, providing a more private and secure verification process. The solution combines on-device age estimation with deepfake and spoofing detection, making it a robust and reliable method for age verification.

The launch of On-Device Age Estimation comes as age assurance laws are being enforced worldwide, with over 30 laws in force globally. Incode's solution aims to bridge the gap between compliance and user trust by offering a seamless, inclusive, and privacy-focused age assurance method. When a user needs to verify their age online, the check happens on their device, and only the outcome (an estimation of the user's age) is transmitted, along with metadata to detect tampering.

Incode's CEO, Ricardo Amper, emphasizes that privacy and fraud prevention are not mutually exclusive, but rather part of the same problem that needs to be solved together. The company's on-device age estimation technology provides a more privacy-preserving option for users, as their biometric data is not accessed or stored by Incode or any client platform. This approach sets a new standard for the age assurance industry, which has traditionally relied on privacy policies that promise to handle biometric data with care and delete it after the check.
