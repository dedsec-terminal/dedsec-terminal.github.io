Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agency's internal watchdog, the Office of Professional Responsibility (OPR), is investigating online critics of the agency. In one instance, ICE agents visited a poll worker, Paigelynne Gonyea, at a polling site in Syracuse, New York, over an Instagram post she made about an ICE agent involved in a fatal shooting. The agents asked her to sign a warning notice stating that it was unlawful to threaten federal officials, but Gonyea refused, citing her right to free speech.

OPR is supposed to investigate internal misconduct and protect against external threats, but court documents show that it has been pursuing civilians for their online activities. Between January 2025 and March 2026, OPR investigated 131 cases involving "incidents of doxing and threats" against ICE employees. While it's unclear how many cases resulted in criminal charges, OPR has been credited with investigating a California man who harassed an ICE attorney and her mother. The agency has also sent administrative subpoenas to tech companies to unmask online critics, but some of these subpoenas have been withdrawn.

The investigation of online critics by OPR has raised concerns about free speech and the expansion of the definition of "doxing" to include lawful activities such as taking photographs of ICE employees. The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) and other civil liberties groups argue that people have a right to criticize the government online and anonymously. The incident has also highlighted the need for legislative oversight of the executive branch, particularly in regards to issues like free speech and online harassment. Gonyea, the poll worker, plans to fight the administration in court, citing the importance of protecting Americans' right to free speech.
