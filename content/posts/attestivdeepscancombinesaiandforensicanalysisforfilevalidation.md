Attestiv has launched DeepScan, a platform that combines AI and forensic analysis to validate submitted files for organizations. This new platform represents a significant shift in the company's approach, moving from detecting fake or manipulated media in isolation to validating the trustworthiness of files within specific business workflows. DeepScan aims to help organizations make faster, more consistent, and confident decisions by determining whether submitted files can be trusted for a particular claim, application, or transaction.

DeepScan's capabilities include unified multi-modal analysis, dynamic rule configuration, contextual business checks, advanced forensic and AI detection, and enterprise integration. The platform uses a combination of generative AI reasoning and a deterministic heuristic engine to understand legitimate enterprise pipelines, resulting in high accuracy and low false positive rates. DeepScan also integrates with best-of-breed forensic and AI transparency signals, including Google AI content detection capabilities, to catch sophisticated fakes and anomalies.

The launch of DeepScan has been well-received by Attestiv's customers, including NARS, which has benefited from the company's ability to help stay ahead of the ever-changing fraud landscape. With DeepScan, organizations can configure rules and workflows to meet their specific industry risk profiles and requirements, and seamlessly deploy the platform into existing claims, risk, and compliance pipelines. By providing a comprehensive file validation solution, Attestiv aims to enhance the ability of organizations to prevent improper payments and strengthen their claims review processes.
