The article discusses various cybersecurity threats and incidents that have occurred recently. Here are the main points:

1. **Zero-day exploits**: The Pwn2Own Berlin 2026 hacking contest revealed 47 zero-day flaws in various products, including Windows, Linux, VMware, and NVIDIA. The contest awarded $1,298,250 in rewards to security researchers.
2. **Agentic AI security warning**: The UK National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) warned organizations to implement adequate security controls when using agentic artificial intelligence (AI) tools, as they can pose a significant risk if not properly designed or privileged.
3. **Signal alternative**: The Polish government is urging public officials to stop using Signal and instead use an encrypted messenger called mSzyfr, citing social engineering attacks orchestrated by advanced persistent threat (APT) groups.
4. **Fraud suspects unmasked**: The Dutch police have unmasked 74 out of 100 suspected fraudsters through an initiative called Game Over?! that displays blurred photos of the suspects on billboards and online advertisements.
5. **Espionage admission**: US President Donald Trump admitted that the US and China engage in cyber espionage activities, including spying on each other.
6. **Ransomware attack**: A ransomware family known as Gunra has targeted five South Korean companies, with the group claiming 32 victims as of March 2026.
7. **Composer token leak**: A vulnerability in Composer, a dependency manager for PHP, has been discovered, which leaks the full contents of GitHub Actions issued tokens to the GitHub Actions logs.
8. **Linux rootkit**: A Linux malware named OrBit has been found to be actively refined and maintained by its operators, with new artifacts indicating that the malware is being used by a cybercrime group running Embargo ransomware campaigns.
9. **AI-driven intrusions**: Two emerging campaigns, SHADOW-AETHER-040 and SHADOW-AETHER-064, have been found to be using agentic AI to facilitate intrusion operations against governments and financial organizations in Latin America.
