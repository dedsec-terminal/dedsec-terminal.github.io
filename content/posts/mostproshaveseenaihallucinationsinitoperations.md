A recent survey of 1,500 IT professionals across six countries found that 68% have personally seen AI produce "hallucinations" with potential operational impact, where the AI output is incorrect or misleading. Despite this, 49% of advanced AI users in IT completely trust AI-generated outputs that influence IT decisions, and trust grows alongside exposure to AI. The survey also found that autonomous AI action is becoming more common in IT operations, with 52% of respondents reporting AI autonomously adjusting performance settings and 46% reporting AI applying patches or fixes.

The survey highlights the need for human review in certain areas, particularly high-severity work. 55% of IT professionals say they would never rely on AI without human review for high-severity incidents, and 52% say the same for communicating incidents to executives or stakeholders. This suggests a two-tier model, where routine remediation runs autonomously, but consequential decisions require human validation. Additionally, governance has become a major concern, with 27% of IT professionals citing governance, security, or compliance concerns as the biggest obstacle to AI deployment.

To address these concerns, Ivanti recommends codifying trust thresholds by workflow, allowing AI to autonomously perform routine tasks while requiring human validation for more critical decisions. This approach can help organizations capture the benefits of autonomous remediation while minimizing the risks of AI hallucinations or faulty signals. The survey also found that governance maturity climbs alongside AI maturity, with 69% of organizations at scaled, business-critical AI use reporting comprehensive governance in place. By establishing clear governance structures and trust thresholds, organizations can ensure the safe and effective deployment of AI in IT operations.
