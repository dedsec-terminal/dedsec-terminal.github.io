The Bahamian government has joined the Have I Been Pwned (HIBP) free government service, becoming the 44th government to do so. This allows the National Computer Incident Response Team of The Bahamas (CIRT-BS) to monitor government domains for potential data breaches.

As the national CIRT, CIRT-BS is responsible for coordinating and supporting cybersecurity efforts across the country. With access to HIBP, they can now identify and mitigate incidents involving compromised credentials and data exposure affecting government entities and stakeholders. This will help prevent and respond to cyber threats more effectively.

The addition of CIRT-BS to HIBP's government service is a key use case for the platform, which aims to help national cybersecurity teams protect their digital ecosystems. CIRT-BS joins a growing list of national cybersecurity teams using HIBP to safeguard government departments, public resources, and critical stakeholders from cyber threats.
