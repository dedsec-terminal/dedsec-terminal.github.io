A recent industry study by Spur Intelligence found that 94% of security incidents involve anonymized infrastructure, such as VPNs and residential proxy networks. Despite having access to vast amounts of IP data, security teams struggle to understand who is behind an IP address and what action to take. The study revealed that many organizations lack the visibility, context, and operational workflows needed to make effective decisions based on IP data, leading to a reactive approach to managing IP-based risks.

The widespread use of anonymization tools has changed the way cybercriminals operate, making it difficult for security teams to identify malicious activity. Traditional approaches based on reputation or static blocklists are becoming less effective, and security teams are encountering attacks where the IP address provides little insight into intent. The study found that nearly half of companies reported significant operational or financial impact from account takeover attempts and credential abuse via VPNs and residential proxies. To combat this, security teams need additional layers of context, including infrastructure classification, VPN and proxy attribution, and behavioral indicators.

The study highlights the need for security teams to move beyond reactive security and leverage IP intelligence in a more proactive and predictive way. This includes applying IP intelligence for adaptive authentication, risk-based access controls, and automated policy enforcement. To achieve this, organizations must prioritize automation, integrate IP intelligence into detection and prevention workflows, and focus on gaining a deeper understanding of the infrastructure, behavior, and intent behind IP addresses. By doing so, security teams can make better decisions and respond more effectively to modern threats in an environment where anonymized infrastructure is increasingly common.
