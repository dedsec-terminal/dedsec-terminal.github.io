Many companies have adopted internal AI tools, but this has led to a significant administrative debt, including "orphaned agents" and "standing privileges". Orphaned agents refer to AI tools that continue to run after their creator has left the company, often retaining unmonitored access to sensitive databases and source code. This poses a significant security risk, as the company may not be able to identify who authorized the AI agent's access or revoke its privileges.

Traditional security tools are not equipped to handle the dynamic nature of AI, which continuously interacts with data on its own. These tools treat AI like standard software, failing to account for the fact that AI agents can pull and shift data without human intervention. As a result, security teams may not be able to detect when an AI tool is accessing sensitive information without proper authorization. To address this issue, it is necessary to map AI tools back to a living owner and unify human, machine, and AI identities under one control plane.

A upcoming technical briefing, hosted by The Hacker News, aims to help security teams bridge this accountability gap. The session will cover the identity gap, finding shadow AI, and deployment reality, providing a step-by-step walkthrough to track down undocumented AI tools active on the network. The briefing will also discuss how to revoke access to orphaned agents and prevent attackers from exploiting these hidden access risks. By attending the webinar, security teams can gain practical insights into securing their company's internal AI tools and mitigating the risks associated with orphaned agents and standing privileges.
