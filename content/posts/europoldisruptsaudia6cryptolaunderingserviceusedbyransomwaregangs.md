Europol has disrupted AudiA6, a cryptocurrency laundering service used by ransomware gangs and cybercriminal networks. The service, which was launched in 2021, is estimated to have laundered over €336 million (~$389 million) in illicit profits. AudiA6 was a central hub for ransomware actors and cybercriminals to cash out stolen digital assets while hiding the money trail from authorities. The service was marketed as a cryptocurrency mixing service guaranteeing anonymity and speed, allowing customers to transfer their ill-gotten proceeds to wallets controlled by the group and receive "cleaned" funds in return within an hour.

The disruption of AudiA6 was the result of a coordinated operation involving law enforcement agencies from several countries, including the US, Poland, and Georgia. Two alleged administrators of the service, Ruslan Igorevich Tkachuk and Alexander Vladimirovich Ledenev, were arrested in Georgia and charged with conspiracy to launder monetary instruments and money laundering. The operation also involved the seizure of over 80 vehicles, multiple properties, and cryptocurrency assets worth €692,000 ($798,000). Additionally, 25 domains and over 30 servers were taken down, and Telegram accounts used by the network were blocked.

The investigation revealed that AudiA6 relied on thousands of fraudulent exchange accounts opened using stolen or purchased identities to launder cryptocurrency. The service was linked to over 15 investigations worldwide related to ransomware attacks and large-scale cryptocurrency theft. The disruption of AudiA6 highlights the rise of industrial-scale cryptocurrency laundering services that enable the cybercrime economy. Europol warned that ransomware groups and cybercriminal networks are increasingly relying on chain-hopping, decentralized exchanges, and "mixer-as-a-service" platforms to move illicit cryptocurrency across multiple blockchains, making it difficult for authorities to track and seize criminal profits.
