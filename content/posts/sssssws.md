A joint international law enforcement operation has resulted in the seizure of the "First VPN" service, a virtual private network used in ransomware and data theft attacks. The service, which was advertised as a privacy-focused VPN that does not log user data, had its servers located in 27 countries and was used by threat actors to hide their location and infrastructure. Authorities have seized dozens of servers, arrested the administrator, and conducted a house search in Ukraine, effectively taking the service offline.

The investigation into the service started in December 2021 and was led by the French and Dutch authorities, who formed a joint investigation team in November 2023. During the investigation, authorities infiltrated the VPN infrastructure and collected the user database, identifying the VPN connections used by cybercriminals in attacks. The seized data has been shared internationally, with information about 506 users and 83 "intelligence packages" being distributed to aid ongoing or upcoming investigations.

The operation, which was conducted between May 19 and 20, resulted in the seizure of 33 servers linked to "First VPN", as well as the disruption of key infrastructure supporting the service. A Ukrainian suspect has been identified and questioned, and notifications have been issued to identified users of the platform. Europol has stated that the gathered intelligence has exposed thousands of users linked to the cybercrime ecosystem and generated operational leads connected to ransomware attacks, fraud schemes, and other serious offenses worldwide.

The takedown of the "First VPN" service is a significant blow to cybercriminals who rely on VPNs to hide their activities. While VPNs are legitimate tools used to protect privacy and security, they can also be used by threat actors to evade detection. The operation highlights the importance of international cooperation in combating cybercrime and the need for VPN providers to comply with law enforcement requests. The investigation and subsequent takedown of the service demonstrate the effectiveness of joint law enforcement efforts in disrupting cybercrime operations and bringing perpetrators to justice.

The success of the operation is a testament to the power of collaboration and information sharing between law enforcement agencies. The seized data and intelligence gathered will likely be used to inform and support future investigations, helping to disrupt and dismantle cybercrime networks. As cybercrime continues to evolve, it is essential for law enforcement agencies to stay ahead of the threats and work together to protect individuals and organizations from these types of attacks.
