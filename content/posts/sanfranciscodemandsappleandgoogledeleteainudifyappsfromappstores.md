The city of San Francisco has sent cease-and-desist letters to Apple and Google, demanding that they remove 13 apps from their app stores that can "nudify" or "undress" people using AI-generated images. The apps, which allow users to create nonconsensual nude images, are considered harmful and illegal. San Francisco city attorney David Chiu says that Apple and Google have likely made millions of dollars in fees from these apps and should stop profiting from the technology.

The apps in question use in-app payments, with Apple and Google taking a cut of the revenue. Researchers have found that many of these apps are rated as suitable for children, despite their ability to generate explicit content. Both Apple and Google have policies prohibiting pornography and abuse on their platforms, but the companies have been criticized for not doing enough to prevent these apps from appearing in their stores. Google has removed hundreds of apps with nudifying features, while Apple has removed three of the apps flagged by Chiu's office and is in the process of terminating the developer accounts of the others.

The issue of deepfake "nudification" tech has become a major concern, with a lucrative industry emerging online that allows people to create and share nonconsensual nude images. The technology has been used to bully, humiliate, and threaten women and girls, with devastating effects on their mental health and reputation. Chiu says that his office will continue to pursue the problem and is considering all legal options to ensure that Apple and Google take responsibility for preventing these apps from appearing on their platforms. The city attorney hopes that the companies will do the right thing and remove the apps, but is prepared to take further action if necessary.
