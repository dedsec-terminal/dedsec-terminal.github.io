Kore.ai has launched the Kore.ai Agent Platform Artemis edition, an AI-native platform designed for enterprise multiagent systems. The platform enables businesses to deploy production-ready AI systems in days, rather than months, with built-in governance, observability, and operational control. It is initially available on Microsoft Azure, with plans for broader cloud availability in the future. The platform features three core innovations: Agent Blueprint Language (ABL), Arch, and a dual-brain architecture, which work together to create a fundamentally AI-native foundation for building, governing, and optimizing AI agents.

The Kore.ai Agent Platform is designed to address the challenges of enterprise AI, particularly in terms of governance, observability, and trust. The platform allows AI to build, govern, and optimize AI agents, with AI-driven tools such as Arch generating production-ready agents from plain-language objectives and validating them before deployment. The platform also enforces deterministic constraints and flow controls, ensuring that AI behavior is predictable and auditable. This approach enables enterprises to scale AI with confidence, with a standardized agent building system and robust governance in place.

The launch of the Kore.ai Agent Platform has significant implications for CIOs, CISOs, and CFOs. For CIOs, the platform accelerates AI delivery and makes it more manageable. For CISOs, it provides predictable AI behavior and enforceable governance. For CFOs, it reduces the marginal cost of deploying additional AI agents and improves the return on investment. The platform's integration with Microsoft Azure and other Microsoft tools, such as Microsoft Foundry and Microsoft Agent 365, also makes it an attractive option for enterprises already standardized on the Microsoft stack. Overall, the Kore.ai Agent Platform has the potential to transform the way enterprises approach AI, making it more scalable, trustworthy, and cost-effective.
