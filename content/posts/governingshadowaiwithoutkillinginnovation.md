The use of artificial intelligence (AI) in companies is a double-edged sword, as it can bring numerous benefits but also poses significant risks if not managed properly. According to Alan Snyder, CEO at NowSecure, companies must adopt AI quickly to stay ahead of competitors and attackers, but they also need to ensure they do so safely. This creates a challenge for leaders, who must balance the need for innovation with the need for risk management.

Snyder argues that the pressure to move quickly will ultimately win out, and therefore leaders must work to manage AI risk along the way. He cites the example of the first 8-K cybersecurity filing related to unauthorized AI use by an employee, highlighting the potential consequences of unmanaged AI adoption. To mitigate these risks, Snyder recommends creating an AI operations team to set approved tools and patterns, as well as building a governance tracking system to monitor AI usage.

To effectively govern shadow AI, Snyder suggests several practical steps, including publishing a pre-cleared list of authorized AI tools, gaining visibility into where AI is used within apps and third-party components, and sorting AI usage into authorized, unauthorized, or unknown categories. By taking these steps, companies can minimize the risks associated with AI adoption while still allowing for innovation and growth. Additionally, resources such as the "Secure Foundations for AI Workloads on AWS" guide can provide further guidance on securing AI workloads.
