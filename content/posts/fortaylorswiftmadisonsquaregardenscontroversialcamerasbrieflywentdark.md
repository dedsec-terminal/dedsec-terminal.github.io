This move prevented the use of the face-recognition program, which has been criticized for its potential misuse, including tracking individuals and banning those who have been critical of Dolan.

The shutdown of the surveillance system was unusual, as MSG's security cameras are typically always on, even during private events. The move has raised concerns among civil libertarians, who see it as an example of how the wealthy and powerful can opt out of surveillance, while others are subject to pervasive monitoring. The incident has also highlighted the controversy surrounding MSG's use of biometric surveillance, which has been used to track and ban individuals who have been critical of Dolan or have been involved in legal disputes with his company.

The accommodation made for Swift and Kelce's wedding celebration has sparked debate about the use of surveillance technology and the unequal access to privacy. While MSG has defended its use of face recognition as a security measure, critics argue that it is often used for retribution or to monitor individuals who are not a security threat. The incident has also raised questions about the effectiveness of the surveillance system, with some arguing that it is not necessary for high-profile events like the Swift-Kelce wedding. The controversy surrounding MSG's use of biometric surveillance is likely to continue, with many calling for greater transparency and accountability in the use of such technology.
