The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has settled with three marketing companies - Cox Media Group, MindSift LLC, and 1010 Digital Works - over allegations of deceptive advertising practices. The companies claimed to offer a service called "Active Listening" that could target ads based on audio recordings collected from consumers' smart devices. However, the FTC found that this service did not actually work as advertised. The companies claimed to collect consumer conversations from devices such as smartphones, smart TVs, and smart speakers, and use AI to target ads based on location and conversation content. They also claimed that consumers had consented to the collection and use of their voice data.

In reality, the FTC alleges that the companies were simply reselling consumer email lists at a significant markup, without actually collecting or using audio recordings. The companies have agreed to pay a total of $930,000 to settle the allegations, with Cox Media Group paying $880,000 and the other two companies paying $25,000 each. The settlement also requires the companies to stop making misrepresentations about their marketing services and the collection and use of audio recordings.

The FTC's action highlights the importance of honesty in business practices, particularly when it comes to the collection and use of consumer data. The settlement does not address the legality of using audio recordings to target ads, but rather the deceptive advertising practices used by the companies. The $930,000 payment will go to businesses that were impacted by the companies' practices, specifically those that purchased the Active Listening service under the impression that it worked as advertised.
