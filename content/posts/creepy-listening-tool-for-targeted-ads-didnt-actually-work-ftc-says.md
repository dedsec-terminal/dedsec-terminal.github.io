The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has settled with three marketing companies - Cox Media Group, MindSift LLC, and 1010 Digital Works - over allegations of deceptive advertising practices. The companies claimed to offer a service called "Active Listening" that could target ads based on audio recordings collected from consumers' smart devices. However, the FTC found that this service did not actually work as advertised. The companies claimed to collect and use voice data from consumers, but in reality, they were simply reselling consumer email lists at a significant markup.

The FTC alleged that the companies made false claims about their ability to collect and use audio recordings, and that they misled their customers - other businesses - into thinking that the service was effective. The companies have agreed to pay a total of $930,000 to settle the allegations, with Cox Media Group paying $880,000 and the other two companies paying $25,000 each.

The settlement highlights the importance of honesty in business practices, particularly when it comes to the collection and use of consumer data. The FTC's director of the bureau of consumer protection stated that "it is a basic rule of business that you need to be honest with your customers, and these companies failed to do that." The settlement also serves as a reminder that companies must be transparent about their data collection and use practices, and must not make false or misleading claims about their services.
