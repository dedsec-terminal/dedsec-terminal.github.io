The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has settled with three marketing companies - Cox Media Group, MindSift LLC, and 1010 Digital Works - over allegations of deceptive advertising practices. The companies claimed to offer a service called "Active Listening" that could target ads based on audio recordings collected from consumers' smart devices. However, the FTC found that this service did not actually work as advertised. The companies claimed to collect and use voice data from consumers with their consent, but in reality, they were simply reselling consumer email lists at a significant markup.

The FTC has ordered the companies to pay a total of $930,000 to settle the allegations, with Cox Media Group paying $880,000 and the other two companies paying $25,000 each. The settlement also requires the companies to stop making misrepresentations about their marketing services and the collection and use of audio recordings.

The FTC's complaint highlights the importance of honesty in business practices, particularly when it comes to the collection and use of consumer data. The settlement does not address the legality of using audio recordings to target ads, but rather the deceptive practices used by the companies to sell their services. The case serves as a reminder to businesses to be transparent and truthful in their advertising and marketing practices.
