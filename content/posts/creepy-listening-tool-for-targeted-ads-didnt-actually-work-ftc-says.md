The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has settled with three marketing companies - Cox Media Group, MindSift LLC, and 1010 Digital Works - over allegations of deceptive advertising practices. The companies claimed to offer a service called "Active Listening" that could target ads based on audio recordings collected from consumers' smart devices. However, the FTC found that this service did not actually work as advertised. The companies claimed to use AI to target ads based on conversations collected from devices such as smartphones, smart TVs, and smart speakers, and also claimed that consumers had consented to the collection and use of their voice data. 

In reality, the FTC alleges that the companies were simply reselling consumer email lists at a significant markup. As part of the settlement, the companies have agreed to pay a total of $930,000, with Cox Media Group paying $880,000 and the other two companies paying $25,000 each. The settlement also requires the companies to stop making misrepresentations about their marketing services and the collection and use of audio recordings. The FTC emphasized the importance of honesty in business practices, stating that companies must be truthful with their customers.
