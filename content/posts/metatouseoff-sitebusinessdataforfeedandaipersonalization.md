Meta has announced that it will be using data from other businesses to personalize users' feeds and AI chatbot responses. This data, which includes information about users' activities on other websites, such as games played or purchases made, is already used to make targeted ads more relevant. By expanding its use, Meta aims to create a more personalized experience for its users.

The social media giant emphasized that it is not collecting any new data, and users have control over how their data is used for personalization. To manage this, Meta is updating its "Activity from other businesses" setting, which allows users to decide whether to allow data from other businesses to be used for personalization. If users opt-in, they will see more relevant content, such as ads and Reels, based on their activities on other websites.

The change is expected to roll out in several countries, including the US, UK, and Brazil, starting next month. Users who do not opt-in will still see content based on their activity on Meta's platforms, such as liking or posting. The update aims to provide users with more control over their data and to create a more personalized experience, while also giving businesses more opportunities to reach their target audiences with relevant content.
