Hackers are now using Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools to launch powerful DDoS attacks that can take a business offline in seconds. These AI-assisted attacks are faster, stronger, and harder to stop, as they can find weak spots in systems and adapt to evade traditional security measures. This new threat means that old security habits, such as setting up a simple firewall and updating software, are no longer sufficient to protect a business.

The use of AI in cyberattacks has changed the game, and businesses must adapt to the new rules of security. AI-assisted attacks can think and adapt, looking for hidden entry points and tiny mistakes in cloud setups, making them much more effective than traditional attacks. To combat this, businesses can use AI to fight back, but they need to know how to use the new tools and strategies available. This requires a new approach to security, one that is proactive and uses automated tools to spot threats before they hit.

A 45-minute webinar will provide businesses with the knowledge and tools they need to protect themselves from these new threats. The webinar will cover topics such as the importance of patching flaws quickly, common mistakes in cloud security, and how to use automated tools to spot threats. Attendees will also receive a step-by-step checklist to secure their business. With AI security being a major topic, spots for the webinar are filling up quickly, and businesses are encouraged to sign up to learn how to protect their digital assets and keep their business safe.
