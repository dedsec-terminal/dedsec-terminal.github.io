Tamnoon has introduced a skill-based AI orchestration for autonomous cloud defense, expanding its AI engine, Tami, to generate customer-specific remediation skills tailored to each enterprise environment. Trained on over 6 million real cloud fixes, Tami coordinates specialized AI skills to safely and autonomously address every class of cloud risk. This development comes as the cloud attack surface is rapidly evolving due to the increasing use of AI, making traditional defense methods inadequate.

The traditional approach to cloud security, relying on generic playbooks and single-purpose agents, is no longer effective in keeping pace with the rapidly changing cloud attack surface. According to Gartner, security and risk management leaders must adopt agentic remediation platforms that can prioritize, validate, and execute fixes with confidence at machine speed. Tamnoon's Tami platform addresses this need by delivering a range of skills, including a Remediation Confidence Score and a Safe Vulnerability Patching Simulator, to provide a more effective and efficient cloud defense.

The Tami platform has shown impressive results, delivering a 97% exposure reduction in 90 days, a 142:1 efficiency ratio, and zero production incidents across over 10 million workloads. The platform's open orchestration layer also allows enterprises and partners to bring their own remediation skills into Tami's safety, validation, and execution pipeline. According to Idan Perez, CTO of Tamnoon, Tami is an orchestrator that coordinates remediation skills generated for each customer's environment, with the safety controls to ship in production, and will soon allow customers and partners to edit existing and add their own skills.
