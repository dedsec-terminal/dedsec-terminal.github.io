OpenAI is taking steps to prepare ChatGPT for the upcoming 2026 election cycle, aiming to mitigate the risks of AI-generated election misinformation. The company plans to focus on providing users with accurate voting information, supporting cybersecurity defenders, and increasing transparency around AI-generated content. This includes partnering with The Associated Press to provide live election results and with Democracy Works to offer voting and registration information in the United States.

To combat the spread of misleading information, OpenAI is investing in a multi-layered approach to verify the authenticity of content created or modified with AI. This includes introducing digital watermarks, such as SynthID, to images generated through ChatGPT and other OpenAI tools. The company will also continue to restrict the use of its tools for political impersonation, voter suppression, and deceptive campaign activity. By doing so, OpenAI aims to promote the responsible use of AI in discussing political issues while limiting misuse by malicious actors.

The efforts come as concerns grow about the potential for AI-generated misinformation to influence public opinion and shape the outcome of elections. Researchers have found that generative AI systems can produce deceptive content when prompted in specific ways, and the use of AI tools to create and share misleading information on social media and other platforms is becoming increasingly common. By addressing these risks, OpenAI hopes to ensure that ChatGPT and other AI systems are used to promote informed discussion and civic engagement, rather than to spread misinformation and undermine democratic processes.
