Here is a 3-paragraph summary of the report on The Gentlemen ransomware:

The Gentlemen, also known as Storm-2697, is a Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) program that has been active since at least July 2025. The operators, approximately 20 in number, are believed to have originated from a private entity called ArmCorp, which was an affiliate of the Qilin RaaS program. The Gentlemen's ransomware variants are written in both C and Go programming languages, allowing them to target different operating systems and virtual infrastructure. They offer an unprecedented 90% payout to affiliates, which has contributed to their rapid growth.

The Gentlemen have been observed using a wide range of initial access techniques, including exploiting vulnerabilities in edge devices, brute force attacks, and collaborating with initial access brokers. They have also developed custom tools, such as a Go-based backdoor and an EDR killer framework called "GentleKiller". In May 2026, they announced a partnership with HasanBroker's BreachForums to recruit affiliates, penetration testers, and initial access brokers. The leak of an internal database by an alleged insider in May 2026 has provided further insight into their operational structure and tactics.

The Gentlemen's activity has increased significantly in 2026, with a total of 580 victims claimed across 77 countries since their inception. The number of victims claimed by The Gentlemen has increased by over 6x in the first six months of 2026 compared to the last six months of 2025. They have become the second most active RaaS program of 2026, and their lucrative affiliate payout structure and custom tooling make them a formidable threat to enterprise organizations. To mitigate this threat, organizations are recommended to patch vulnerabilities, establish robust visibility into internet-facing systems, and implement strong security requirements for third-party dependencies and vendors.
