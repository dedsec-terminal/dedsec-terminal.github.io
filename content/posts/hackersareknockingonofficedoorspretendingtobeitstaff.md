The FBI has issued a warning about a hacking group known as the Silent Ransom Group (SRG), which is targeting law firms using social engineering techniques and an unusual tactic: showing up at victims' offices in person while posing as IT staff. The group, also known as Luna Moth, Chatty Spider, and UNC3753, has been active since at least 2022 and has targeted companies in several sectors, including insurance, finance, and healthcare.

The SRG actors use phone calls and phishing emails to pose as employees from a victim's IT department, attempting to gain remote access to systems or trick employees into opening a remote desktop session. If these attempts fail, the group sends a threat actor to the company's office to gain physical access to devices, claiming they need to create a backup or image the system. The actor then inserts a storage device into the computer, allowing the group to steal data and use it to extort victims through ransom emails.

To prevent SRG attacks, the FBI advises organizations to verify anyone claiming to be from internal IT support, train employees to identify callback phishing and social engineering attempts, and implement security measures such as multi-factor authentication (MFA) and restricted remote access tools. The agency also urges companies to review their help desk procedures and ensure that employees are aware of the risks of social engineering attacks. By taking these precautions, organizations can reduce their risk of falling victim to the SRG's tactics.
