Hackers recently exploited a vulnerability in Meta's AI support bot to seize control of several high-profile Instagram accounts, including those of the Obama White House and the Chief Master Sergeant of the U.S. Space Force. The attackers used a simple exploit that involved tricking the AI bot into resetting account passwords by providing a new email address and using a VPN connection with an IP address near the target's location. The hackers then used the exploit to deface the accounts with pro-Iranian images and messages.

The exploit was made possible by Meta's use of an AI-powered support assistant to handle common account recovery workflows, such as relinking lost email addresses and triggering password resets. However, this approach has been criticized for creating a new attack surface that can be exploited by hackers. According to threat researcher Ian Goldin, AI chatbots can be social engineered into providing unauthorized access to accounts, just like human customer support employees. As a result, securing online accounts requires taking full advantage of multi-factor authentication (MFA) offerings, such as passkeys or security keys.

Meta has since resolved the issue and secured the impacted accounts, with the company's Andy Stone confirming on Twitter that an emergency patch was pushed over the weekend. The incident highlights the importance of robust security measures, particularly when using AI-powered support assistants. Experts warn that AI chatbots can create new vulnerabilities, and users should be cautious when interacting with these systems. By enabling MFA, users can significantly reduce the risk of their accounts being compromised, as the hackers' exploit would have been blocked if the targeted accounts had MFA enabled.
