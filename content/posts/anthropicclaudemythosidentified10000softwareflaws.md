Anthropic, a company specializing in AI technology, has made significant progress in identifying software vulnerabilities through its Project Glasswing and Claude Mythos Preview. In partnership with various companies and open-source communities, Anthropic has discovered over 10,000 high- or critical-severity vulnerabilities in critical software systems. This achievement is a result of the company's large language model, Claude Mythos, which can autonomously find zero-day vulnerabilities and create exploits for them.

The identification of these vulnerabilities has been a major success, with Anthropic scanning over 1,000 open-source projects and identifying 23,019 issues, including 6,202 high- or critical-severity vulnerabilities. Independent security research firms have validated over 90% of these findings as true positives. However, the company notes that the process of patching these vulnerabilities has become a bottleneck, with open-source maintainers struggling to keep up with the volume of bug reports. Anthropic has partnered with the Open Source Security Foundation to help address this issue and make vulnerability discovery and patching easier.

Anthropic plans to expand Project Glasswing and make its Mythos-class models generally available, but only after developing stronger safeguards to prevent misuse. The company is working with partners, including the US government and allied governments, to achieve this goal. In the meantime, Anthropic has released Claude Security in public beta and is providing tools and resources to qualifying security teams to help them identify and prioritize vulnerabilities. The company acknowledges that the development of safeguards is crucial to preventing the misuse of its models and potentially causing severe harm.
