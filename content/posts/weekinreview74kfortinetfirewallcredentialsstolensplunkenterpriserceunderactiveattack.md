Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the week's cybersecurity news:

The past week saw several significant cybersecurity incidents, including the theft of 74,000 Fortinet firewall credentials by a Russian-speaking cybercriminal group. Additionally, a critical vulnerability in Splunk Enterprise (CVE-2026-20253) was found to be under active attack, prompting CISA to add it to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. Other notable incidents included the exploitation of vulnerabilities in Cisco's SD-WAN Manager and SimpleHelp's remote monitoring and management tool, as well as the discovery of a backdoor attack that hides in plain sight in hardware neural networks.

The use of AI in cybersecurity continues to grow, with many security operations centers (SOCs) adopting AI and machine learning tools. However, a recent survey found that while four in five practitioners use AI or machine learning tools, only about a third have built these tools into a defined workflow with structure, governance, and consistent validation. Meanwhile, researchers have warned that AI agents could lower the skill floor for offensive cyber operations, making it easier for low-skilled attackers to breach companies. In one recent example, a low-skilled attacker used AI agents to breach 14 companies.

Several organizations and initiatives are working to improve cybersecurity, including the Chainguard Athena coalition, which has shipped over 2,000 patches across 500 open-source projects. The Car Connectivity Consortium (CCC) is also working to secure digital keys used to unlock cars, while researchers are developing new systems, such as Safecloud, to provide secure decentralized storage. Law enforcement agencies have also made progress in taking down malicious operations, including the SocGholish malware campaign, which was disrupted in a recent international operation.
