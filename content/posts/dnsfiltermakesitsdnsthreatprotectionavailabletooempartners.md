DNSFilter has introduced an Original Equipment Manufacturing (OEM) program, allowing external partners to integrate its DNS threat protection, domain analysis, and privacy solutions into their platforms. This program enables ISPs, cybersecurity firms, device makers, and consumer app developers to embed DNSFilter's technology, choosing between two product paths: DNSFilter Protective DNS for DNS-layer filtering and threat blocking, and/or DNSFilter Guardian Firewall and VPN services for full-device traffic encryption and privacy.

The OEM program offers partners three commercial models: resell and bundle, embedded (headless), or white-label co-brand for ISPs and MSSPs. With no platform lock-in, integrations can block threats from day one. DNSFilter's platform processes 200 billion DNS queries per day, blocks over 235 million threats daily, and protects over 45,000 organizations. The company's existing OEM integrations already support 65 million users daily, and its Guardian Firewall and VPN is powering privacy and threat blocking on Amazon's eero home and small business networks.

To facilitate developer integration, DNSFilter has created a dedicated Software Developer Kit (SDK) that allows developers to map out, configure, and model their systems prior to initiating an OEM partnership. The SDK supports integration across various operating systems and enables developers to access a sandbox environment, run their first privacy block in minutes, and build from there. With this program, DNSFilter aims to provide partners with the infrastructure and support to grow and stay ahead in embedded DNS protection, which is becoming an expectation in the industry.
