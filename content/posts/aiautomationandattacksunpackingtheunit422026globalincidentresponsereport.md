The Unit 42 2026 Global Incident Response Report highlights four key trends shaping the threat landscape, with a focus on how AI is being used by attackers. The report notes that AI has become a force multiplier for attackers, allowing them to reduce the time and effort required to launch attacks. However, the fundamental threat landscape remains relatively consistent, with attackers relying on established techniques such as credential theft, phishing, and ransomware deployment. AI is primarily being used to streamline and automate existing attack methods, rather than introducing new ones.

The report's findings suggest that defenders already have the knowledge and capabilities to prevent, detect, and respond to AI-enhanced cyberattacks. However, the speed and efficiency of AI-powered attacks require defenders to adapt and evolve their strategies. As AI continues to advance, it is essential for cybersecurity professionals to understand its capabilities and limitations, as well as its potential to amplify both attacks and defenses. The report emphasizes the need for defenders to prioritize prevention controls and stay informed about emerging technologies, rather than relying solely on detect-and-respond models.

Experts from Unit 42, including Andy Piazza and Richard Emerson, agree that AI has not introduced fundamentally new attack vectors, but rather has enabled attackers to operate more efficiently and at scale. They emphasize the importance of understanding AI's limitations and being able to critically evaluate AI-generated responses. As AI continues to evolve, defenders must be prepared to combat AI-powered attacks with AI-powered defenses, while also maintaining a strong foundation in traditional cybersecurity principles. The report concludes that AI is a force multiplier for both attackers and defenders, and that its potential to shape the future of cybersecurity will depend on how it is understood and leveraged by security professionals.
