**Expansion of Codex Knowledge Work**
Codex, an agentic coding product from OpenAI, has expanded its user base to 5 million weekly active users, with knowledge workers adopting it more than three times as fast as developers. These workers, who account for about 20% of users, are using Codex for tasks such as research, reports, and spreadsheets, in addition to coding. This shift is significant, as knowledge workers spend a substantial amount of time on email triage and searching for internal information, with the average worker spending 28% of their workweek on email and 20% on searching for information.

**Growth in Data Analysis and Research**
Among knowledge workers, data analysis is growing at a rate of 110% week over week, with research and knowledge artifacts also showing significant growth. Data labeling dominates data analysis tasks, with other categories such as drafting messages, building and designing products, and understanding contracts and regulations also showing growth. The use of Codex for these tasks is becoming increasingly routine, with about 50% of users now running multiple tasks simultaneously. This shift is enabling users to become orchestrators of workstreams, inspecting datasets, drafting scripts, and assembling reports in parallel.

**Case Studies and Policy Recommendations**
Codex is being used in a variety of contexts, including government data, sales, teaching, and personal projects. Case studies highlight the potential of Codex to automate tasks, improve efficiency, and enhance productivity. OpenAI has also released policy recommendations to support the adoption of AI tools like Codex, including deploying agents to cut administrative backlogs, funding hands-on AI training, and updating public procurement to prioritize operational outcomes. These recommendations aim to support worker-led adoption and ensure that AI tools are used in a way that is secure, private, and auditable.
