WhatsApp has announced the introduction of usernames, a feature designed to protect user privacy by allowing them to connect with others without sharing their phone numbers. This optional feature will enable users to create and reserve a unique username, which can be used to initiate conversations. The username feature is intended to be a privacy-focused tool, rather than a social media handle, and will not include a directory or suggestions.

To reserve a username, users can navigate to the Settings > Account > Username section in the app. WhatsApp will also provide a username generator to help users choose a unique handle. Additionally, users can set up a username key for an extra layer of protection, requiring others to know both the username and key to initiate contact. This feature will give users more control over who can reach them on WhatsApp.

The introduction of usernames on WhatsApp is a significant development, as it will allow users to maintain their privacy while still being able to connect with others. The feature is particularly useful for content creators, small businesses, and organizations that want to maintain a consistent online presence across platforms. WhatsApp will roll out usernames gradually over the coming months, with users being notified when the feature becomes available in their country. This move follows a similar feature introduced by Signal over two years ago, and is a major step forward in protecting user privacy on the messaging platform.
