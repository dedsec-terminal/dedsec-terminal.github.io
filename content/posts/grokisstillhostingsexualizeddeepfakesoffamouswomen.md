Elon Musk's Grok chatbot is still being used to create and host nonconsensual explicit images and videos of women, despite promises to introduce restrictions. A WIRED analysis found dozens of public links on Grok.com that led to sexualized AI images and videos, including those created without the subject's consent. The images and videos depicted celebrities and a politician in explicit scenarios, with some being photorealistic and showing plausible real-world scenarios.

The use of Grok Imagine to create and host explicit content has raised concerns among experts and advocates, who say that the platform has not done enough to prevent the creation of nonconsensual and sexualized deepfakes. Henry Ajder, an expert on deepfakes, notes that other generative AI systems have more safety guardrails in place than Grok. The company has faced lawsuits and scrutiny from regulators since January, when the Grok implementation on X was used to create a flood of "nudification" images.

The issue has sparked criticism from advocates, including Carrie Goldberg, who represents Ashley St. Clair, a woman who was allegedly targeted with sexualized deepfakes on X. Goldberg says that the creation of sexual abuse material featuring celebrities is nothing new, but the ability to create and share it on social media is a "historic" development. Imran Ahmed, CEO of the Center for Countering Digital Hate, claims that Elon Musk knowingly added a feature to his platform that helps users undress women and children, with no regard for the damage it would cause. SpaceX, xAI's parent company, is set to go public on Friday, despite facing ongoing legal complaints and warnings from regulators.
