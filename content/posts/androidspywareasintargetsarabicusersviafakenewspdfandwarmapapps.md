A new Android spyware, codenamed Asin, has been detected targeting Arabic-speaking users, according to ESET, a Slovakian cybersecurity company. The malware was first detected in early 2025 and has been spread through multiple campaigns using fake websites, including those mimicking government news sources, PDF editors, and war-related updates. These websites, such as govlens.net, pdf-reader.help, and live-war-map.com, distribute malicious apps that combine legitimate functionality with stealthy spyware capabilities.

The malicious apps are promoted through dedicated social media accounts on platforms like Facebook and Telegram, with names that may be inspired by legitimate platforms, such as Live Universal Awareness Map. The apps require manual installation and permission granting from the user to achieve their goals. ESET has identified multiple artifacts associated with Asin, including samples uploaded to VirusTotal and detected on Xiaomi devices running Android 15. The spyware has been masquerading as various apps, including "Syria Defense Map" and "GovLens".

The primary objectives of the Asin campaigns are unknown, and the activity cluster remains unattributed. However, based on the lures used, it is suspected that journalists and open-source intelligence (OSINT) researchers in Arabic-speaking regions may have been the target. Three of the five fraudulent apps discovered by ESET appear to be intended for people interested in open-source investigation, suggesting that the spyware may have been designed to target Arabic-speaking journalists or OSINT practitioners. The true intentions and scope of the Asin campaigns are still unclear, but the discovery highlights the ongoing threat of Android spyware targeting specific regions and communities.
