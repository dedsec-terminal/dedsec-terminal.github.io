Here is a summary of the article in three concise paragraphs:

Stelios Kouloglou, a Greek politician and member of the European Parliament's PEGA Committee, was investigating the use of Pegasus spyware in 2022. Ironically, his iPhone was hacked with the same spyware that fall, according to a forensic analysis by the University of Toronto's Citizen Lab. Kouloglou was shocked and angry when he found out, stating that being a member of the committee investigating Pegasus while being hacked by it was "really too reckless."

The Citizen Lab report reveals that Kouloglou's device was targeted multiple times, potentially giving the perpetrator access to internal information about the committee's activities and findings. The researchers do not have conclusive evidence of who was behind the attacks, but note that the targeting occurred while the committee was holding key hearings and finalizing its report. The incident highlights the endemic and brazen nature of spyware targeting in the EU and beyond, with Citizen Lab senior researcher John Scott-Railton stating that "it's open spyware season on Europe's lawmakers."

The incident has sparked concerns among MEPs that other members of the committee may have also been targeted, and that the group's recommendations to combat spyware use have not been adopted. The PEGA Committee's report called for the creation of an EU-based tech lab and a spyware taskforce for elections, but these recommendations have not been implemented. Citizen Lab and MEPs are urging European institutions to take action to protect against spyware abuse, citing the need for public-private collaboration and concerted policy efforts to address the issue.
