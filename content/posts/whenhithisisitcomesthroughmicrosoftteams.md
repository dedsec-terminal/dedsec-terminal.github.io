Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

Threat actors are increasingly using Microsoft Teams to launch social engineering attacks on employees. By impersonating IT support staff or other trusted entities, attackers can manipulate employees into taking actions that compromise their accounts. This technique has been successfully used by groups such as Cloaked Ursa and UNC6692, who have leveraged compromised accounts to send malicious links and phishing messages through Microsoft Teams. These attacks often start with a message that appears to be from a legitimate IT support person, asking the employee to approve a multi-factor authentication prompt or reset their credentials.

The rise of chat-based social engineering attacks is a result of threat actors adapting to the increasing effectiveness of email phishing defenses. As email gateways and user awareness have improved, attackers have turned to collaboration tools like Microsoft Teams to launch their attacks. Microsoft Teams' federation feature, which allows users to communicate with external tenants, can be exploited by attackers to initiate chats with employees. To prevent these attacks, organizations must configure their Microsoft Teams settings to restrict external communication and educate their users to be cautious when receiving messages from external senders.

To harden Microsoft Teams against external abuse, organizations should disable external communication settings, configure federation to only allow specific external domains, and implement conditional access policies to protect identity systems. User awareness training should also be extended to cover Microsoft Teams and other collaboration tools, teaching employees to recognize external indicators and verify requests through separate channels. Additionally, monitoring and reporting suspicious activity can help detect and prevent attacks. By taking these steps, organizations can reduce the risk of chat-based social engineering attacks and protect their employees and data from compromise.
