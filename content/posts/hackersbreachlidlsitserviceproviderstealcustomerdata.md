Lidl, a German discount supermarket chain, has announced that customer data was stolen after hackers breached one of its IT service providers. The incident affected customers in Germany, Belgium, and the Netherlands, and Lidl was informed of the breach last week. The company stated that despite having high IT security standards, the attackers were able to briefly access a stored file containing customer data.

The stolen data includes sensitive information such as names, telephone numbers, email addresses, dates of birth, customer numbers, and salutations. Additionally, Lidl warned that passwords, billing and delivery addresses, bank details, and other payment information may have been compromised. However, the company emphasized that customer accounts were not affected and that there is currently no evidence of data misuse.

Lidl has taken steps to address the incident, including notifying the affected IT service provider, which has restored the security of its IT systems and filed a police report. The company has also engaged IT forensic experts to investigate the breach and notified the relevant data protection authority. As a precaution, Lidl is warning customers to be aware of potential phishing attempts or identity fraud, although no additional details about the attackers or their motives have been released.
