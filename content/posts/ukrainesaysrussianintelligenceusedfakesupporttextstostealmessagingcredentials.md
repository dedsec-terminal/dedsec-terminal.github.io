The Security Service of Ukraine (SSU) and the US Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) have uncovered a long-running campaign by Russian intelligence services to hack into the messaging accounts of government officials, military personnel, politicians, and activists in Ukraine, Europe, and the US. The goal of these cyber attacks is to steal sensitive information, including military, political, and economic data, as well as personal information.

The attackers use fake support texts that masquerade as the messaging platform's support bot, urging users to disclose their account credentials. These attacks target not only organizations and public figures but also personal accounts of Ukrainian nationals. While the SSU did not attribute the campaign to a specific hacking group, similar attacks on Signal and WhatsApp users have been linked to Russian threat activity clusters such as Star Blizzard and UNC5792.

To counter these threats, users are advised to take precautions such as reviewing active messaging app sessions, enabling two-factor authentication, and refraining from scanning QR codes or disclosing sensitive information. The discovery of this campaign comes as the FBI has attributed Russian Intelligence Services to a phishing campaign targeting high-value targets, and the Computer Emergency Response Team of Ukraine has attributed a spear-phishing campaign to the Belarus-aligned threat actor UNC1151. Users are urged to be vigilant and take steps to protect their messaging accounts from these types of cyber attacks.
