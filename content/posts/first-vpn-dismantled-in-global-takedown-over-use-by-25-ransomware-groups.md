Authorities in Europe and North America have dismantled a criminal virtual private network (VPN) service known as First VPN, which was used by 25 ransomware groups to carry out attacks and other illicit activities. The service, promoted on Russian-speaking cybercrime forums, offered anonymous payments and a hidden infrastructure that enabled users to hide their identities. First VPN was taken down in an international operation that involved interviewing the service's administrator, conducting a house search in Ukraine, and seizing infrastructure used to support cybercriminal activity globally.

The operation, which took place between May 19 and 20, resulted in the takedown of 33 servers and the seizure of several domains, including 1vpns[.]com, 1vpns[.]net, and 1vpns[.]org. The service had been active since 2014 and provided 32 exit node servers in 27 countries, including three in the US. First VPN offered various connection protocols and encryption options, including OpenConnect, WireGuard, and OpenVPN ECC, and accepted payments through Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies.

The FBI reported that First VPN's website promoted itself as a tool for anonymity, promising not to cooperate with judicial authorities and not to store user data. However, the service's FAQ noted that it prohibited the use of its servers for illicit activities, which was likely an attempt to escape liability. The takedown of First VPN is a significant blow to ransomware groups and other cybercriminals who relied on the service to carry out their activities. The international operation demonstrates the cooperation and coordination between law enforcement agencies in disrupting and dismantling criminal infrastructure.

The dismantling of First VPN highlights the importance of cooperation between law enforcement agencies and the private sector in combating cybercrime. The operation also underscores the need for individuals and organizations to be vigilant in their online activities and to use reputable and secure VPN services. The takedown of First VPN is a significant step in disrupting the activities of ransomware groups and other cybercriminals, and it is likely to have a lasting impact on the cybercrime landscape.
