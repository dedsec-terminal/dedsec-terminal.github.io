**Introduction to UNC3753 Campaign**
A financially motivated data theft extortion campaign, attributed to the threat actor UNC3753 (also known as Chatty Spider, Luna Moth, and Silent Ransom Group), has targeted dozens of organizations in the U.S. between January and May 2026. The campaign, which affects professional, legal, and financial services, involves voice phishing (vishing) and social engineering deception techniques to gain remote access into corporate environments.

**Tactics and Techniques**
UNC3753 uses pretexts such as data migration or invoice-related emails to initiate phone conversations, posing as IT support and convincing targets to host screen-sharing sessions and download remote monitoring and management (RMM) utilities. The threat actors then search for and exfiltrate sensitive files, including proprietary legal agreements, personally identifiable information (PII), and financial records. In some cases, they have also accessed victims' systems in person, posing as IT technicians to steal data using removable USB media.

**Extortion and Infrastructure**
The stolen data is sent to the threat actors, who then send an extortion demand to the victims, giving them a three-day deadline to initiate ransom negotiations. The attackers threaten to publish the stolen information on the data leak site "business-data-leaks[.]com" if the victims do not respond. UNC3753's infrastructure includes a fast-flux network with domains such as "business-data-leaks[.]com" and "ep6pheij[.]com", which are backed by a botnet spread across 18 countries and 22 ISPs, making it resilient against takedowns. The campaign's fast-tempo operational model allows the attackers to initiate data searches, staging, and theft in under an hour, often completing the entire operation within a single business day.
