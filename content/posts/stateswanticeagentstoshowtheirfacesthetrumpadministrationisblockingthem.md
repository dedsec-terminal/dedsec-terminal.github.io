The Trump administration has sued several states and the city of Philadelphia over laws that require Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officers to identify themselves during immigration raids. The laws, which vary by state, prohibit ICE officers from wearing masks and require them to wear individual identifiers. The Trump administration argues that these laws put ICE officers in danger, as they claim that people use facial recognition software to identify and harass officers.

However, the creator of an art project called ICESpy, which is cited by the Trump administration as an example of the danger posed by facial recognition software, says that the project's database is outdated and cannot recognize most current ICE agents. The database is based on photos and job titles of people who self-identified as ICE employees on LinkedIn nearly a decade ago. Additionally, another website, ICE List, which is also cited by the Trump administration, relies mostly on manual research and does not have a public-facing way for visitors to analyze photos using facial recognition software.

State lawmakers argue that their laws are necessary to hold ICE officers accountable for misconduct and to protect residents from unconstitutional behavior by the federal government. They point to instances of ICE officers fatally shooting people without wearing body cameras, and the federal government's refusal to produce evidence in investigations. The lawmakers say that the Trump administration should prioritize accountability and transparency within ICE, rather than challenging state laws that aim to promote democracy and justice.
