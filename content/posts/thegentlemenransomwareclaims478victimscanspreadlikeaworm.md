The Gentlemen ransomware group, also known as Phantom Mantis, has been active since March 2025 and has claimed 478 victims to date. The group is led by a Russian-speaking cybercriminal known as LARVA-368, who has been identified as 36-year-old Alexander Andreevich Yapaev from Izhevsk, Russia. Initially, the group operated as an affiliate of various ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) schemes, but in July 2025, it transitioned into an independent partnership program.

The Gentlemen group is known for its highly adaptive and fast-moving ransomware operation, which combines mature ransomware techniques with RaaS features, double extortion, cross-platform lockers, and flexible propagation. The group has emerged as one of the most active threat actors, accounting for 10% of ransomware activity in April 2026. The majority of its victims are concentrated in Thailand, the U.K., Brazil, Germany, and India, with only about 13% of victims based in the U.S. The group uses various tactics, including manipulating GPOs, compromising privileged accounts, and using custom methods to bypass endpoint protections.

The Gentlemen group provides its affiliates with a range of tools and support, including five versions of ransomware designed for different platforms, as well as a range of utilities for reconnaissance, privilege escalation, and defense evasion. The group courts affiliates with an aggressive profit-sharing model, offering 90% of profits to affiliates and 10% to the operator. The group's ransomware uses a hybrid cryptographic scheme and can spread like a worm, attempting to deploy its encryptor to every reachable system on the network. The group's inner workings have been shed light on through a leak of an internal Rocket.Chat database, which has revealed a highly responsive development cycle and a clear division of roles and responsibilities among its members.
