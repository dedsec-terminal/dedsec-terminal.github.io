The article discusses several cybersecurity-related news stories. Here's a summary:

1. **Cybercriminal twins caught**: Two brothers, Muneeb and Sohaib Akhter, pleaded guilty to destroying 96 government databases after being fired from their jobs. They were caught because they forgot to turn off a Microsoft Teams meeting recording, which captured their plans for revenge.
2. **Foxconn cyberattack**: The electronics manufacturer was hacked by a ransomware group called Nitrogen, which claimed to have stolen 8 TB of data. The theft remains unconfirmed.
3. **iPhone unlocking and phishing**: Research shows that there's a thriving ecosystem for tools that let criminals unlock iPhones and target phone numbers found inside.
4. **5G-connected drones**: The Department of Homeland Security and Defense Research and Development Canada plan to test 5G-connected drones for collecting real-time battlefield intelligence.
5. **Instructure ransomware attack**: The company behind the educational software Canvas reached a deal with the hackers, who had disrupted the platform and posted ransom messages. The company claims that the stolen data has been returned and destroyed.
6. **Dream Dark Web Market arrest**: The alleged administrator of the Dream Market, a dark web market for drugs and contraband, was arrested in Germany.
7. **OpenAI supply chain attack**: Two OpenAI employees were impacted by a supply chain attack on an open source project called TanStack. The company investigated and found unauthorized access, but no evidence of user data being accessed.
8. **Data broker removes opt-out page code**: Findem, a data broker, removed code that had hidden its opt-out page from Google search for three years, after being called out by Senator Maggie Hassan.

These stories highlight the ongoing threats to cybersecurity, including ransomware attacks, phishing, and supply chain attacks, as well as the importance of transparency and accountability in the tech industry.
