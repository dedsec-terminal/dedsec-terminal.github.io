The article discusses various cybersecurity news and incidents. Here are the key points:

1. **Cybercriminal twins caught**: Two hackers, Muneeb and Sohaib Akhter, pleaded guilty to destroying 96 government databases after being fired from their jobs. They were caught because they forgot to turn off a Microsoft Teams meeting recording, which captured their discussion of the revenge plan.
2. **iPhone unlocking and phishing**: Research shows that there is a thriving ecosystem for tools that let criminals unlock iPhones and target phone numbers found inside, leading to phishing attacks.
3. **Foxconn cyberattack**: The electronics manufacturer was hacked by a ransomware group called Nitrogen, which claimed to have stolen 8 TB of data.
4. **5G-connected drones**: The Department of Homeland Security and Defense Research and Development Canada plan to test 5G-connected drones for collecting real-time battlefield intelligence.
5. **Iran's "mosquito fleet"**: Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps is using a fleet of small boats to block the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial shipping route.
6. **Instructure ransomware deal**: The company behind the educational software Canvas reached a deal with the hackers who disrupted the platform, with the hackers claiming to have returned stolen data and destroyed it on their systems.
7. **Dream Dark Web Market arrest**: The alleged administrator of the Dream Market, a dark web market for drugs and contraband, was arrested in Germany and charged with making millions of dollars from commissions.
8. **OpenAI supply chain attack**: Two OpenAI employees were impacted by a supply chain attack on an open source project, with the company requiring macOS users to update their apps by June 12.
9. **Data broker removes opt-out page code**: Findem, a data broker, removed code that hid its opt-out page from Google search results for three years, after being called out by Senator Maggie Hassan.

These incidents highlight the importance of cybersecurity and the need for individuals and organizations to be vigilant in protecting their data and systems.
