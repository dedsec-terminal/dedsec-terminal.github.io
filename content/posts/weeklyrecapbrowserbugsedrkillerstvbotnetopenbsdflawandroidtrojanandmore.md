Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the weekly recap of browser bugs, EDR killers, TV botnets, OpenBSD flaws, Android trojans, and more:

This week's threat list includes familiar issues such as abused integrations, fake tools, poisoned websites, and ransomware crews trying to shut down security tools. The FortiBleed campaign has compromised over 80,000 Fortinet FortiGate firewall and SSL VPN gateway devices worldwide, with suspected Russian-speaking threat actors using automated tools to exploit weak credentials and brute-force techniques. Meanwhile, Salesforce has disabled the Klue app integration after an extortion group compromised and exfiltrated data from customers.

Other notable threats include the Gentlemen RaaS operation developing an EDR killer suite called GentleKiller, which targets over 400 processes belonging to 48 security products. A critical flaw in Splunk Enterprise, CVE-2026-20253, has been actively exploited in the wild, allowing unauthenticated users to conduct file operations and remote code execution. Additionally, a working exploit called usbliter8 has been released, targeting Apple's A12 and A13 chips, although it requires physical access to the device. Operation Endgame has disrupted SocGholish servers, taking down 106 servers and cleaning up nearly 15,000 infected WordPress websites.

New malware campaigns have also been discovered, including a cryptocurrency-stealing malware campaign that fakes its own popularity and a Rust-based clipper malware targeting Windows and macOS users. The Rokarolla Android trojan combines banking fraud with screen surveillance, targeting 217 distinct cryptocurrency and banking applications and granting complete control of a compromised device. The trending CVEs for the week include high-severity vulnerabilities in Cisco SD-WAN Manager, LiteSpeed cPanel Plugin, and NVIDIA Display Driver for Windows and Linux, among others. It is essential to patch these vulnerabilities and stay vigilant to prevent exploitation.
