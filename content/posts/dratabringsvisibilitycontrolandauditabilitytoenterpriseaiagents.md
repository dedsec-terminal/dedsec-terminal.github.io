Drata has introduced AI Agent Governance, a new security category that aims to manage the risks and oversight requirements of AI agents in enterprise settings. This move is in response to the growing adoption of AI and the need for companies to govern these autonomous systems. According to McKinsey, 57% of business leaders cite governance friction as the top blocker to deploying more AI, highlighting the need for a solution like Drata's.

The Drata platform has processed over 2.1 million security questions in the last nine months, with a 30% surge in AI-specific questions. These questions cluster around five core themes: identifying AI agents, understanding their permissions, monitoring their behavior, and proving their posture. However, 89% of companies are unable to answer these questions, leaving them unprepared to govern their AI agents. Drata's AI Agent Governance is designed to empower security leaders to see, authorize, monitor, and prove the posture of AI agents in their environment.

Drata's solution provides enterprise security teams with capabilities to govern AI agents, including inline sensors that detect and inventory all AI agents, evaluate their actions against individual policies, and log decisions in a tamper-evident record. This provides a single, verified evidence trail for auditors, customers, and regulators. According to Adam Markowitz, CEO of Drata, the company is uniquely positioned to solve this problem, having spent five years building a trust layer between companies and helping customers prove trust faster. The introduction of AI Agent Governance is the next step in supporting the growth of AI technology.
