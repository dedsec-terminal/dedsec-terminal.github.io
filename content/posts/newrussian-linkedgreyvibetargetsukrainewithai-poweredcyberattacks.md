A newly discovered threat actor, dubbed GREYVIBE, has been linked to ongoing cyberattacks targeting Ukraine and Ukraine-related entities since at least August 2025. The group is believed to be Russian-speaking and operates in the Russian time zone, with activities aligning with Kremlin state interests, particularly intelligence gathering efforts aimed at Ukraine. GREYVIBE has been observed using various attack vectors, including spear-phishing emails, fake captcha pages, and fraudulent websites, to deliver malware to a diverse set of victims across military, government, civilian, and business-related organizations.

GREYVIBE's attacks have been found to utilize custom-developed obfuscators, loaders, and malware, with the group relying on generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) and large language models (LLMs) to enhance its operations. The group has been observed using multiple attack chains, including PhantomMail, PhantomClick, PrincessClub, DroneLink, and Nebo, to deliver various malware and tools, such as PhantomRelay, FallSpy, and LegionRelay. The use of AI has allowed GREYVIBE to bridge gaps in technical expertise, accelerate development, and reduce reliance on previously known malware, making attribution efforts more challenging.

Despite its nation-state-affiliated activity, GREYVIBE is believed to have ties to the broader Russian cybercrime ecosystem, with some members possibly being current or former cybercriminal actors. The group's use of AI has also introduced design flaws, exposing the malware's backend functionality, which suggests that GREYVIBE may not be a pure nation-state actor. The exact nature of GREYVIBE's relationship to the Russian state remains unclear, and the group's activities occupy a grey area between cybercrime and state-affiliated activity, complicating attribution efforts and blurring traditional distinctions between these categories.
