Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The rapid adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) in business operations has created a significant challenge for enterprise security. Employees are using AI to automate tasks, analyze data, and create workflows, often without the knowledge or approval of their organizations. This has led to the growth of "shadow AI," which refers to the use of unauthorized or unapproved AI applications, workflows, and features within an organization. As a result, many organizations have more AI operating inside their environments than they are aware of, creating a significant visibility gap in enterprise security.

The use of shadow AI creates significant risks, particularly with regards to unauthorized data movement. Employees may upload sensitive information to AI tools without understanding how the data is handled, stored, or used. Additionally, the use of AI features within existing software applications can create new pathways for sensitive information to be exposed. The rapid expansion of no-code AI agents has introduced another layer of complexity, allowing business users to create workflows that connect AI models directly to sensitive systems and data. Security teams are struggling to keep pace with the spread of AI across the business, with 47% of enterprise generative AI usage occurring through personal accounts rather than organization-managed ones.

To address the risks associated with shadow AI, organizations need to invest in building visibility into how AI is being used across the business. This requires an ongoing understanding of where AI is being used, what has changed, and whether new tools or features introduce risks that weren't there before. Governance models need to be updated to keep pace with the rapid evolution of AI technology, and employees need to be trained to understand the risks associated with AI adoption. By investing in discovery and visibility, organizations can better manage the risks associated with AI and ensure that the benefits of the technology are realized while minimizing its risks.
