A 23-year-old Canadian man, Jacob Butler, also known as "Dort," has been arrested and charged with operating the KimWolf DDoS botnet. The botnet is linked to attacks that infected over one million devices worldwide, including digital photo frames and web cameras. These devices were then used to carry out DDoS attacks against targets globally, resulting in significant financial losses for some victims.

The KimWolf botnet is alleged to have issued over 25,000 attack commands, with some attacks reaching a record volume of nearly 30 Terabits per second. Authorities linked Butler to the operation through IP address data, online account information, and transaction records. The arrest is part of a larger effort to disrupt DDoS-for-hire platforms, with the Central District of California unsealing seizure warrants targeting online services that supported 45 such platforms.

Butler faces one count of aiding and abetting computer intrusion and could receive up to 10 years in prison if convicted. The arrest follows an international law enforcement operation in March 2026 that disrupted infrastructure linked to several botnets, including KimWolf. The operation targeted millions of infected devices worldwide, primarily IoT systems such as digital video recorders, web cameras, and WiFi routers. The seizure of online services supporting DDoS-for-hire platforms is expected to broadly disrupt these operations and prevent further attacks.
