Thieves can steal keyless cars in under a minute using a simple method called a relay attack. This involves two people, one with a device that detects and boosts the signal from the key fob, and another who relays the signal to the car, allowing it to unlock and start. The vulnerability affects around 85% of keyless cars on the market, according to Germany's largest auto club, ADAC. This has led to a significant number of keyless car thefts, with 59% of passenger cars stolen in the Netherlands in 2025 having keyless entry.

The relay attack works by targeting the radio signal sent by the keyless fob, which can be boosted and relayed to the car, allowing thieves to unlock and start the vehicle without setting off the alarm. Thieves can also use the car's diagnostic port to program a new key, allowing them to drive the car away. The ease of purchasing systems that can copy keys or hardware for relay attacks has made it simple for thieves to carry out these attacks. Additionally, the use of online connections and NFC keys has introduced new vulnerabilities, making it easier for thieves to access vehicles.

To protect against keyless car theft, drivers can take several measures. These include storing the key fob in a signal-blocking pouch, keeping keys away from exterior walls, and adding a lock for the OBD-II port. A software immobilizer or a kill switch can also be installed to add an extra layer of security. Other measures include placing a GPS tracker or Bluetooth tag in the car, parking in lit and monitored areas, and fitting a visible steering lock. By taking these steps, drivers can reduce the risk of their car being stolen and make it more difficult for thieves to carry out a relay attack.
