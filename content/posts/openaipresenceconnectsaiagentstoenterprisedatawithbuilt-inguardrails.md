OpenAI has introduced Presence, a deployment platform designed to help companies deploy AI agents for customer support and internal service requests. Presence connects AI agents to enterprise data while providing built-in guardrails to ensure secure and compliant interactions. The platform brings together various components, including policies, simulations, evaluation tools, and a Codex-powered improvement process, to enable teams to manage and improve AI agent performance.

Presence allows teams to test and evaluate AI agents before deployment, using simulations and graders to assess their performance and adherence to company policies. The platform also includes guardrails that can intervene when an interaction moves outside the company's boundaries, ensuring that the AI agent stays within established guidelines. After launch, Presence continues to learn from production sessions and escalations, with Codex reviewing interactions and suggesting improvements to the agent's behavior.

OpenAI's Presence is currently available through a limited general availability program for enterprise customers, with support from OpenAI's Forward Deployed Engineers and select partners. The company has already seen success with Presence, using it to handle its own English-language phone support line and resolving 75% of inbound calls without human intervention. Interested companies must work with their OpenAI account team to access Presence, as it is not available as a self-service product.
