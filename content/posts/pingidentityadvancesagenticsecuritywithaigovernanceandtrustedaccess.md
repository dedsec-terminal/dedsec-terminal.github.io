Ping Identity has introduced new capabilities to its platform, focusing on agentic security with AI governance and trusted access. The company aims to address the changing landscape of identity management, where AI agents, automation, and developers are increasingly involved in managing access across organizations. AI agents are becoming new actors that need to be discovered, governed, and managed, while also serving as operators that help administer and secure identity environments through machine-native interfaces.

The Ping Identity Platform now offers a unified set of capabilities, including programmable identity, agent discovery and governance, and privileged access for desktop agents. These features enable enterprises to support AI-driven operations without creating a parallel identity stack, preserving governance and control across human, non-human, and AI-agent access. The platform provides AI-first headless interfaces and skills, allowing builders and agents to work with identity programmatically, and introduces agent-ready skills to help AI agents understand and perform common identity tasks.

The new capabilities help organizations manage AI agents across their full lifecycle, from discovery and ownership assignment to access review, policy enforcement, auditability, and decommissioning. Ping also enables trusted access for desktop agents, brokering access to enterprise resources without exposing secrets to the agents themselves. This approach allows enterprises to adopt AI agents more safely and efficiently while maintaining centralized identity governance, accountability, and control. According to Ping Identity's CEO, Andre Durand, "AI agents are fundamentally changing how enterprise systems operate," and the company's new capabilities are designed to support this shift while preserving governance and control.
