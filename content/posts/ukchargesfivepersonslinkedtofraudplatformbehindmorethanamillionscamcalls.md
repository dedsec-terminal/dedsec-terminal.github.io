The UK's National Crime Agency (NCA) has charged five individuals in connection with a fraud platform known as Russian Coms, which was used to make over a million scam calls. The platform, established in 2020, allowed fraudsters to hide their identity by spoofing caller ID, making it appear as though they were calling from legitimate institutions such as banks and law enforcement agencies. The goal of the scam was to steal funds and personal details from victims.

The five individuals charged, all from London, face offenses including conspiracy to supply articles for use in fraud and transferring and converting criminal property. The NCA believes that the scam resulted in financial losses in the tens of millions, with approximately 170,000 people in the UK falling victim to it. The average loss per reported victim was over £9,400. The scam was not limited to the UK, with victims in 107 countries, including the USA, New Zealand, and France.

Russian Coms was shut down in March 2024, but not before it had generated over 1.3 million calls to 500,000 unique UK phone numbers between 2021 and 2024. The platform was marketed through social media platforms such as Telegram, Snapchat, and Instagram, and offered features such as encrypted calls and voice changing. Criminals paid for access to the platform using cryptocurrency, with prices ranging from £1,200 to £1,400 for a six-month contract. The five charged individuals are due to appear in court, with the NCA continuing to investigate the scam and its impact on victims.
