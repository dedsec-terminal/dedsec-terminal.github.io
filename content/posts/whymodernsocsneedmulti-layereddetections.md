Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The traditional cybersecurity cycle of defenses improving and attackers adapting is no longer effective, as AI-equipped attackers are now outpacing defenses. According to the CrowdStrike Global Threat Report, 79% of attacks are malware-free, with threat actors using credential theft and other techniques to bypass host-level monitoring. This has led to a need for security practices to adapt and prioritize rapid containment and post-compromise behavior analysis, with a focus on real-time detection that goes beyond host-level coverage.

To achieve this, multi-layered network detections are necessary, extending defense beyond the endpoint and providing a more comprehensive view of corporate security. Network Detection and Response (NDR) validates, enriches, and connects separate signals from endpoint, identity, and cloud platforms, using network data to provide vital context and undeniable proof of attacks. This approach helps to build confidence in decisions and reduces the cognitive load on analysts, who can use integrated network detection layers to establish certain proof of threats.

The effectiveness of multi-layered detections depends on the quality of the data behind them, with rich network telemetry providing the evidence needed to support AI-driven security automation. By investing in full-lifecycle protection and advanced network telemetry, security leaders can achieve operational clarity and reduce the risk of attacks. With a solid foundation of network evidence, organizations can turn their network into their most powerful defensive asset, delivering improved detection quality, faster investigations, and higher confidence in results. This approach enables security teams to respond quickly and effectively to modern threats, including those posed by autonomous exploit engines like Mythos.
