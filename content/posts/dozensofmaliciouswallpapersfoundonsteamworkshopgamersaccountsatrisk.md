**Malicious Wallpapers on Steam Workshop**
Dozens of malicious wallpapers have been discovered on the Steam Workshop, a platform where users can create and share custom content. These wallpapers, which are primarily targeting gamers in China and Russia, contain hidden malware that can hijack Steam accounts, install backdoors, or deploy crypto miners. The attackers are exploiting the Wallpaper Engine app, which allows users to create and share animated wallpapers, to spread their malware.

**How the Malware Works**
The malicious wallpapers are embedded with executable files, DLLs, or scripts that can run on a user's computer. Once a user applies one of these compromised wallpapers, the malware can drop a backdoor file, install a custom system library, and hijack the user's live Steam session. The attackers can then use the stolen account credentials to upload more malicious content to the Steam Workshop. The malware can also lead to ransomware, system crashes, or other types of cyber threats.

**Staying Safe**
To avoid falling victim to these malicious wallpapers, users are advised to run antivirus scans on any downloaded content before applying it. The Steam team has already removed some of the identified malicious wallpapers, but new ones may still appear on the platform. Users should be cautious when downloading and installing custom content, and ensure that their antivirus software is up to date. By taking these precautions, gamers can protect their accounts and systems from these types of cyber threats.
