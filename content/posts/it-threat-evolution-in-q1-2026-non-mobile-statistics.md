In Q1 2026, Kaspersky products blocked over 343 million attacks originating from various online resources, with Web Anti-Virus responding to 50 million unique links and File Anti-Virus blocking nearly 15 million malicious and potentially unwanted objects. The quarter also saw the detection of 2938 new ransomware variants, with more than 77,000 users experiencing ransomware attacks. Notably, 14% of all ransomware victims whose data was published on threat actors' data leak sites were victims of the Clop ransomware. Law enforcement agencies made significant strides in combating ransomware, with the FBI seizing the domains of the RAMP cybercrime forum and apprehending individuals linked to the Phobos group.

The most prolific ransomware gangs in Q1 2026 included Clop, Qilin, and The Gentlemen, with Clop returning to the top of the rankings after displacing Qilin. The quarter also saw the emergence of new threat actors, such as The Gentlemen, which had already surpassed the activity levels of established groups like Akira and INC Ransom. In terms of vulnerabilities and attacks, the Interlock group was found to be heavily exploiting a zero-day vulnerability in Cisco Secure FMC firewall management software, demonstrating the ongoing reliance on zero-day vulnerabilities for initial access. The vulnerability enabled arbitrary Java code execution with root privileges on the affected device, highlighting the importance of timely patching and vulnerability management.

The geography of threats varied across different regions, with the United States, the Netherlands, and Germany accounting for the highest proportions of SSH-based attacks on IoT devices. China continued to account for the largest proportion of Telnet attacks, although there was a marked increase in activity originating from Pakistan. In terms of web-based attacks, Kaspersky solutions blocked over 343 million attacks launched from internet resources worldwide, with Web Anti-Virus being triggered by nearly 50 million unique URLs. The countries and territories where users faced the greatest risk of online infection included those with high levels of malicious activity, such as the United States, Russia, and China.

The quarter also saw significant activity in the realm of miners, with Kaspersky solutions detecting 3485 new modifications of miners and protecting over 260,000 unique users from miner attacks. The TOP 10 countries and territories attacked by miners included those with large numbers of Kaspersky users, such as the United States, Russia, and China. In terms of attacks on macOS devices, the quarter saw the discovery of new cryptocurrency theft campaigns and the exploitation of vulnerabilities in iOS and macOS devices. The TOP 20 threats to macOS included a mix of traditional adware and spyware, as well as more specialized threats like the PasivRobber spyware. Overall, the Q1 2026 threat landscape was marked by significant activity across various threat vectors, including ransomware, miners, and web-based attacks.
