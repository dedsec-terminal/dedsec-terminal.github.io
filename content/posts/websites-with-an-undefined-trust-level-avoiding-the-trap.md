The article discusses the dangers of suspicious websites that cannot be definitively classified as phishing but engage in unsafe activities. These sites manipulate users into voluntarily transferring money for non-existent services, signing up for hidden subscriptions, or disclosing personal data. Kaspersky has introduced a new web filtering category, "Sites with an undefined trust level," to detect such resources.

The article highlights the following key points:

1. **Types of suspicious sites**: Fake online stores, dubious crypto exchanges, investment platforms, and services with paid subscriptions are common examples of suspicious sites.
2. **Dangers of suspicious sites**: Visiting or buying from untrusted websites can lead to financial loss, fraud, and exposure of personal data.
3. **Common indicators of suspicious sites**: Strange domain names, cheap top-level domains, recently registered domains, unrealistic promises, lack of company contact information, and payments only via cryptocurrency or irreversible bank transfers.
4. **Visual and manual clues**: Scam sites often have unprofessional visuals, poor language, and persuasive content. Legitimate businesses provide verifiable contact details, while scam sites hide this information.
5. **Technical indicators**: Kaspersky's system analyzes domain name and age, IP address reputation, DNS configuration, HTTP security headers, and SSL certificate to detect suspicious resources.
6. **Regional threats**: Kaspersky's regional statistics reveal specific threats in different regions, such as fake online trading scam platforms in Africa and fake betting services in Latin America.
7. **Protection measures**: Combining visual inspection, technical checks, and trusted online tools can help protect against financial loss and data theft.

To stay safe, users should be cautious when visiting unfamiliar websites and look out for red flags such as strange domain names, unrealistic promises, and lack of contact information. It is also essential to use reputable security software and keep software up to date to protect against online threats.
