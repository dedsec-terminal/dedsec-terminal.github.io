The article discusses the threat of suspicious websites that cannot be strictly classified as phishing but are still unsafe. These sites manipulate users into willingly paying for non-existent goods and services or signing up for subscriptions that are difficult to cancel. Kaspersky has introduced a new web filtering category, "Sites with an undefined trust level," to detect such suspicious resources.

The article highlights the following key points:

1. **Types of suspicious sites**: Fake online stores, dubious financial platforms, and online services that mimic legitimate businesses are categorized as suspicious.
2. **Dangers of shady websites**: Visiting or buying from untrusted websites can lead to financial loss, data leaks, and unauthorized use of personal information.
3. **Common types of suspicious sites**: Fake online stores, online exchanges and trading platforms, subscription traps, and shady sites masquerading as providers of mediation services.
4. **How to identify suspicious websites**: Visual and manual clues, such as strange domain names, poor visuals, and unrealistic promises, can indicate a suspicious site.
5. **Technical indicators**: Technical checks, such as analyzing the domain name and age, IP address reputation, DNS configuration, HTTP security headers, and SSL certificate, can help detect suspicious sites.
6. **Payment options**: Legitimate e-commerce platforms typically offer secure and reversible payment options, while suspicious sites may only accept cryptocurrency, wire transfers, or third-party P2P payments.
7. **Regional statistics**: Kaspersky's regional statistics reveal the specific nature of these threats, with fake browser extensions, online trading scam platforms, fake betting services, and crypto scams being prevalent in different regions.

To stay safe, users are advised to:

1. Be cautious of websites with strange domain names, poor visuals, and unrealistic promises.
2. Check for verifiable contact details, such as a physical address, company name, and customer support.
3. Evaluate the website's social media presence and look for signs of artificial or superficial activity.
4. Be wary of payment options that are irreversible, such as cryptocurrency or wire transfers.
5. Use trusted online tools and security products, such as Kaspersky's web filtering category, to detect suspicious sites.

By being aware of these indicators and taking protective measures, users can reduce the risk of falling victim to suspicious websites and protect their personal and financial information.
