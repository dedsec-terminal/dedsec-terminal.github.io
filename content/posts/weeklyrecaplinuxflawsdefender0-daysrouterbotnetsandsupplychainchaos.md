Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the weekly recap of cybersecurity news:

The cybersecurity landscape remains chaotic, with phishing crews becoming more sophisticated and botnets exploiting vulnerable devices. A recent breach at GitHub was attributed to a compromised employee device, which was infected with a poisoned version of the Nx Console VS Code extension. The breach allowed the threat actor, TeamPCP, to exfiltrate approximately 3,800 repositories. Other companies, including OpenAI, Mistral AI, and Grafana Labs, were also impacted by the TanStack supply chain attack.

Several high-severity vulnerabilities were disclosed this week, including a 9-year-old Linux kernel flaw that enables root command execution and a critical security flaw in Drupal Core that is being actively exploited. Microsoft warned of two actively exploited Defender vulnerabilities and released mitigations for a BitLocker bypass vulnerability named YellowKey. Additionally, Cisco patched a maximum-severity security flaw in Secure Workload, and Anthropic's Project Glasswing uncovered over 10,000 high- or critical-severity vulnerabilities in popular software.

The gap between patching and exploiting vulnerabilities is shrinking, with many high-severity bugs being actively exploited in the wild. Verizon reported that vulnerability exploitation has overtaken compromised credentials as the most common initial access vector for data breaches. To stay ahead of threats, security teams must prioritize patching and proactive threat hunting. Upcoming webinars will discuss how attackers use AI to supercharge DDoS efficiency and how to hunt for threats that don't rely on exploits. Staying informed and taking proactive measures is crucial in today's rapidly evolving cybersecurity landscape.
