Delinea and Cyera have announced a product integration that connects privileged access to sensitive data exposure, allowing security teams to identify, prioritize, and remediate high-risk access paths. This integration aims to address the challenge of governing privileged identities and their access to critical data, especially with the increasing number of human, machine, and AI agents interacting with data. By bringing together identity and data context, security teams can prioritize risk and govern access with confidence.

The integration between Delinea and Cyera enables data-aware identity security, where accounts with access to mission-critical data are automatically elevated in risk scoring. This allows teams to prioritize access reviews and least-privilege enforcement based on the sensitivity of the data at stake. The Delinea Platform and Cyera Data Security Posture Management (DSPM) integrate via API, delivering data-aware identity security at scale. Cyera continuously discovers, classifies, and monitors sensitive data, which is then correlated with identities in Delinea to provide a continuously updated risk picture.

The integration provides several benefits to security teams, including the ability to remediate the most critical exposures first, spend less time chasing every alert, and get a complete picture of identity and data risk in one place. With Delinea and Cyera, security teams can prioritize remediation, access reviews, and privileged access controls based on the sensitivity and exposure level of the underlying data. This integrated approach enables teams to focus on the risks that matter most and govern access with confidence, ultimately reducing the risk of a breach.
