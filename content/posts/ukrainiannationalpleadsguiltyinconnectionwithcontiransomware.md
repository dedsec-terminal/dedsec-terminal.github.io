A Ukrainian national, Oleksii Oleksiyovych Lytvynenko, has pleaded guilty to conspiracy to commit wire fraud in connection with the Conti ransomware operation. Lytvynenko, 44, joined the conspiracy in September 2021 and was involved in possessing stolen data from 12 victims, including eight in the US. He also worked on coding a "loader" malware used to execute other malicious attacks.

The Conti ransomware operation targeted over 1,000 victims worldwide, including organizations in 47 US states, Puerto Rico, and 31 countries. The attacks, which occurred between 2020 and 2022, generated at least $150 million in ransom payments. The operation involved breaching victim networks, encrypting files, and using stolen data to pressure organizations into paying ransoms. The FBI estimates that the attacks caused significant damage to victims worldwide.

Lytvynenko's guilty plea is seen as a significant step towards holding cyber criminals accountable for their actions. He faces up to 20 years in prison and is scheduled to be sentenced on September 10, 2026. The case is part of a larger effort to dismantle the Conti ransomware operation, which ceased operating under its original name in 2022. Former members are believed to have moved on to other ransomware and cybercrime operations, and US authorities have already sentenced another former member to 102 months in prison.
