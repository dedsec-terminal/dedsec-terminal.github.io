Digimarc has introduced new provenance and verification infrastructure to secure autonomous and AI-enabled workflows. This move aims to address the growing need for trusted provenance and verifiable authenticity in AI systems that generate content, orchestrate workflows, and take action with minimal human intervention. The company's new capabilities will help organizations determine whether digital content and artifacts produced or consumed by autonomous AI agents can be trusted before downstream action occurs.

The new infrastructure is grounded in the Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity (C2PA) standard, an open specification adopted by organizations such as Adobe, Google, and Microsoft. Digimarc's approach combines provenance, verification, and audit capabilities into a standards-based trust layer for AI-powered workflows. The company extends C2PA's output attestation capability with a trust enforcement layer purpose-built for agentic environments, ensuring that every provenance seal is policy-gated and issued only when the identity of the agent, the integrity of the artifact, and the timing of the request all satisfy defined trust criteria.

The initial release of Digimarc's solution centers on a new Model Context Protocol (MCP) server that enables systems to stamp, verify, log, audit, and retrieve provenance information. This allows organizations to introduce trusted verification and traceability directly into content and automation pipelines without requiring systems to manage the underlying cryptographic infrastructure themselves. The MCP server is backed by Digimarc's Illuminate platform and the company's decades of experience in authentication, watermarking, and trusted identification technologies. With this solution, Digimarc aims to help organizations build trust in their AI-powered workflows and mitigate the risks associated with autonomous agents.
