Dashlane, a password manager, experienced a brute-force attack that triggered temporary account lockouts and authentication issues for some users. The company first acknowledged the incident on May 31 after users reported receiving account suspension emails and experiencing login problems. The emails stated that the accounts had been suspended due to multiple failed attempts to register a new device.

The incident affected Dashlane's email notification and 2FA systems, but the company confirmed that there was no evidence of compromise of its systems. Dashlane's engineering teams investigated the root cause of the account suspension notifications and worked on a resolution, treating the incident as a high-priority issue. The company marked the incident as resolved on May 31 but changed the status to "monitoring" on June 1.

The handling of the outage drew criticism on Reddit, with some users complaining about the lack of information provided by Dashlane during the incident. Others were unsure about the legitimacy of the account suspension emails, which arrived before Dashlane publicly explained the situation. Dashlane later responded on Reddit, reassuring users that there was no evidence of system compromise and that the company was taking measures to address the issue. Despite the resolution, the incident highlighted the importance of transparency and communication during security incidents.
