A recent Google paper has accelerated the expected arrival of a cryptographically relevant quantum computer from 2035 to 2029, giving organizations approximately two and a half years to prepare. This new timeline is crucial as a quantum computer, combined with Shor's algorithm, would be capable of breaking the public key encryption currently in use. As a result, Chief Information Security Officers (CISOs) must take immediate action to mitigate the potential risks associated with post-quantum migration.

Garfield Jones, SVP Global Strategy and Research at QuSecure, has outlined a plan for CISOs to follow over the next 24 months. The plan begins with workforce training and conducting a cryptographic inventory to understand the organization's current encryption landscape. This is followed by engaging in conversations with vendors to assess their preparedness for post-quantum migration. Additionally, Jones emphasizes the importance of achieving crypto agility, implementing hybrid systems, and conducting TLS 1.3 pilots to ensure a smooth transition.

To protect against the "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" threat, where adversaries store encrypted data now and decrypt it later, CISOs must prioritize the security of operational technology, IoT, embedded, and legacy systems. By taking proactive steps, organizations can minimize the risks associated with post-quantum migration and ensure a secure transition to quantum-resistant encryption methods. By following Jones' plan, CISOs can help their organizations stay ahead of the curve and protect against the potential threats posed by quantum computers.
