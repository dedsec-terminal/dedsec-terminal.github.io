Google has filed a lawsuit against a Chinese cybercrime network accused of using its Gemini artificial intelligence (AI) agent to send phishing text messages targeting Americans. The network, known as the Outsider Enterprise, is behind a phishing-as-a-service (PhaaS) software kit that allows threat actors to create and deploy massive SMS phishing attacks. These attacks often impersonate legitimate brands, alerting recipients of fake issues or rewards, and prompt users to click on links leading to fraudulent websites that steal personal and financial information.

The Outsider Enterprise operates through a network of interconnected groups, including developers, data brokers, spammers, and thieves, who collaborate to execute phishing attacks. The phishing kit, which can be purchased for as little as $88 a week, provides over 290 pre-built templates that impersonate legitimate websites, real-time keystroke logging, and a performance dashboard to track the effectiveness of a campaign. The kit also allows users to weaponize AI-generated code to create counterfeit websites that can be used to steal personal or financial information from victims. Google is partnering with major telecom companies, including AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon, to block such messages from reaching customers.

The lawsuit aims to dismantle the Outsider Enterprise's infrastructure and disrupt its operations, which are estimated to have victimized over 100,000 people, leading to millions of dollars in losses. The FBI has also commented on the case, noting that criminals are increasingly using AI to make fraud more convincing and harder to detect. This lawsuit comes seven months after Google filed a similar lawsuit against China-based hackers behind a massive Phishing-as-a-Service (PhaaS) platform called Lighthouse. The case highlights the growing threat of AI-powered phishing attacks and the need for tech companies and law enforcement to collaborate to disrupt and dismantle these types of operations.
