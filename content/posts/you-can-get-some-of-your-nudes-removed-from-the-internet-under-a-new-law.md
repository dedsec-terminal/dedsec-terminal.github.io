A new US law, the Take It Down Act, requires tech platforms to provide a way for people to report nonconsensual intimate images and videos (NCII) uploaded to their platforms. The law, which went into effect on May 19, applies to a range of apps and online services, including social media and gaming platforms. 

To comply with the law, platforms must make it easy for people to submit removal requests, which must include:

1. A way to locate the content, such as a link
2. A statement explaining that it was not uploaded consensually
3. A signature from the person depicted or someone authorized to act on their behalf
4. Contact information for the person submitting the request

Once a request is submitted, the platform has 48 hours to determine its validity and remove the content if it is deemed nonconsensual. Some platforms, such as Meta, Microsoft, Google, and Reddit, have dedicated forms for submitting takedown requests, while others may use email addresses or third-party tools.

Experts note that the effectiveness of the law will depend on how easily people can find and use the reporting tools, particularly for younger users who may not be aware of their rights or able to navigate complex reporting forms. Some companies, such as Snap and TikTok, have updated their reporting forms to include references to the Take It Down Act, while others, such as X Corp. and Verizon, did not respond to requests for comment.

The law is seen as an important step in protecting individuals from digital harm, particularly in the context of nonconsensual intimate imagery and deepfakes. However, its success will depend on the willingness of tech platforms to comply with the law and make reporting tools accessible and user-friendly.
