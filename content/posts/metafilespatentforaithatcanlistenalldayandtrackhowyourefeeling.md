Meta has filed a patent application for an AI system that can listen to a user's voice throughout the day, analyze their emotional state, and keep a timestamped log of their feelings. The system can be integrated into various devices such as smart glasses, phones, smartwatches, or smart home speakers. It uses AI to transcribe and analyze the user's speech, including tone, pace, and language, to determine their emotional state and provide a summary of their patterns over time.

The patent application describes a system that can not only analyze voice but also biometric signals such as pupil size, blink rate, and eye moisture to detect stress or emotions. Additionally, it can track how the user interacts with their devices, including screen time, app usage, and posts viewed or liked. The system can provide personalized feedback, such as "You sigh most frequently before bed, and you're happiest when with friends." The patent also includes a workout coach feature that uses the emotional analysis to adjust its coaching style, easing off if the user is tired or discouraged.

The concept of emotional state analysis is not new, with Amazon having introduced a similar feature in its Halo wearable in 2020. However, Meta's system is more comprehensive, incorporating multiple data sources and potentially raising more significant privacy concerns. Regulators have already expressed doubts about the effectiveness and intrusiveness of such systems, with the EU's AI Act banning AI that infers emotions in workplaces and schools. Meta has not announced any plans to develop a product based on this patent, and it remains to be seen how the company would handle user data and address potential regulatory concerns if it were to move forward with the technology.
