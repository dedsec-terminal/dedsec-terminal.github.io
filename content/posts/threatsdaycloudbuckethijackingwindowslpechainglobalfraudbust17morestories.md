Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

This week's ThreatsDay list highlights various security threats, including a global anti-fraud operation that resulted in the arrest of 5,811 individuals and the interception of $293 million in illicit assets. The operation, codenamed First Light 2026, targeted social engineering scams and associated money laundering activities. Additionally, a cluster of 17 malicious npm and PyPI packages were found to typosquat payment SDKs, such as Paysafe, Skrill, and Neteller, to steal system information and developer secrets.

Other notable threats include a technique called Process Parameter Poisoning (P³) that can be used to inject code into foreign processes without raising security alarms. A critical security flaw in Esri ArcGIS Server 12.0 and prior could be exploited to access sensitive files on the system without requiring valid credentials. Furthermore, a new analysis of the Interlock ransomware operation has identified links with TAG-124, and a social engineering campaign has been combining email phishing with a fake IT support scam to deliver EtherRAT.

Other threats mentioned in the article include a warning from China's National Vulnerability Database to uninstall recent Claude Code versions due to concerns that they can gather sensitive user data without consent. A social engineering campaign has been using Microsoft Teams calls to deliver EtherRAT, and the notorious cybercrime group Scattered Spider has been linked to various attacks, including SIM swapping and cryptocurrency theft. Additionally, Taiwanese authorities have charged two local businessmen with allegedly assisting Chinese government-linked hackers in a sprawling espionage campaign targeting politicians and academics.
