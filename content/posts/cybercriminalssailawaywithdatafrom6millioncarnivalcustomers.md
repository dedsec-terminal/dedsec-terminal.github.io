Carnival Corporation, a major cruise operator, has confirmed a data breach after the ShinyHunters hacking group claimed to have stolen millions of customer records. The breach occurred when an unauthorized actor used social engineering to gain access to a limited portion of the company's IT system through a single employee account. This incident was discovered by the company's IT security team on April 14, 2026.

The leaked data allegedly contains 8.7 million records, including names, dates of birth, genders, email addresses, and loyalty program status information. However, according to a data breach notice filed by Carnival, the incident actually affected 5,995,277 people. The exposed information is related to the Mariner Society loyalty program operated by Holland America Line, a Carnival Corporation subsidiary. The breach was made public by the ShinyHunters hacking group on April 18, 2026.

Carnival has begun notifying affected individuals and is offering eligible U.S. residents two years of complimentary credit monitoring services through TransUnion. The company has also taken steps to enhance its security and monitoring controls to prevent similar incidents in the future. This is not the first time Carnival's systems have been breached by cybercriminals, making it crucial for the company to implement effective security measures to protect customer data. Carnival has stated that it will continue to advance its IT security and data privacy controls to address evolving threats.
