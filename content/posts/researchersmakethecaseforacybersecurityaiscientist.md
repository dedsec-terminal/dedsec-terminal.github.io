Researchers from the Chinese Academy of Sciences are proposing the concept of a Cybersecurity AI Scientist, a system that can autonomously conduct research in cybersecurity, from framing a question to designing experiments, building tools, and evaluating results. This system, dubbed Hephaestus, would consist of multiple agents with specialized roles, working together to produce both offensive and defensive research. The goal is to close the gap between the slow and manual nature of current cybersecurity research and the rapid advancements in autonomous AI agents that can probe software for flaws and run penetration tests.

The researchers argue that cybersecurity is a unique domain that requires a different approach than other fields, such as machine learning or biology, where automated research systems have already been developed. Cybersecurity involves an adaptive adversary, rapidly changing environments, and a need for digital twins, cyber ranges, and evidence chains to validate findings. To address these challenges, the researchers propose a four-zeros frame, which focuses on four types of failure: risk, trust, incident, and energy. They also introduce the concept of resilient agent legions, where a large crowd of defensive agents work together to protect a network, rather than relying on traditional perimeter-based defenses.

The proposed Cybersecurity AI Scientist system would need to be evaluated over time, using a longitudinal protocol that perturbs the model stack, tools, and threat environment. The output would be a profile matrix reporting research yield, evidence quality, and other metrics. The researchers also emphasize the need for containment and control, to prevent dual-use capabilities from being misused. Ultimately, the success of a Cybersecurity AI Scientist would be measured not only by its speed, but by its ability to improve strategic composure, prioritization, and defensive design. The paper provides a framework for developing such a system, but also highlights the open challenges and complexities involved in creating a Cybersecurity AI Scientist.
