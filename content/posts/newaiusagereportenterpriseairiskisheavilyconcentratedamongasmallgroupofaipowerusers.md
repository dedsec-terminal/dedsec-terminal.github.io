The State of AI Usage Report 2026 by LayerX Security reveals that enterprise AI risk is heavily concentrated among a small group of "AI power users" and a handful of dominant AI platforms. Despite the common perception that "everyone uses AI now," the report shows that only 18% of employees use AI on a weekly basis, and the top 5% of users generate a disproportionate amount of enterprise AI exposure. These power users conduct far more conversations, interact across multiple AI platforms, and engage in significantly deeper prompt chains than average employees, creating a new class of users that pose a significant risk to enterprise security.

The report also highlights the fragmentation of AI usage across personal accounts, AI browser extensions, embedded copilots, AI connectors, and secondary AI tools operating outside traditional visibility and governance controls. ChatGPT remains the dominant AI platform inside enterprises, accounting for 36% of enterprise AI users and over 55% of all AI conversations. However, Copilot M365 is growing quickly, and the growth of copilots signals a split between governed enterprise-native AI and consumer-driven AI adoption. The report notes that not all enterprise AI adoption carries the same level of risk, and the real governance challenge comes from consumer AI usage operating inside enterprise workflows under the appearance of legitimate productivity tools.

The report identifies several key challenges for organizations, including the use of personal AI accounts, which can create a major governance blind spot, and the growing long tail of AI tools that operate outside traditional visibility and governance controls. To address these challenges, the report recommends that security leaders identify and monitor high-risk AI power users, stop focusing only on approved AI, block personal account usage, and shift from a "block or allow" approach to inline AI guardrails that monitor prompts, uploads, responses, and AI-driven actions in real-time.
