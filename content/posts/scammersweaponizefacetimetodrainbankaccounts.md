Apple is warning iPhone and iPad users about a new scam where scammers use FaceTime calls to trick people into handing over money and account details. The scammers pose as trusted organizations, such as Apple or a bank, and use social engineering to gain the victim's trust. They may use a technique called spoofing to make the call appear as if it's coming from a legitimate source, and often bring up private information to sound credible.

Once the scammer has established trust, they will try to create a sense of urgency to get the victim to hand over sensitive information or money. They may claim that the victim's account has been compromised and offer to help, or ask the victim to turn off two-factor authentication (2FA) or other security features. However, Apple emphasizes that it will never ask users to log in to a website, provide passwords or codes, or enter sensitive information into a website. The company advises users to be cautious and never share sensitive information with someone who contacts them first.

To protect themselves, Apple recommends that users be wary of unexpected calls or texts, keep their iPhone updated to the latest iOS version, and never trust someone who contacts them out of the blue. If a user receives a suspicious FaceTime call, they can take a screenshot and send it to reportfacetimefraud@apple.com. Apple's advice is to always contact companies directly if they need to verify information, and to presume that unexpected messages or calls are scams. By being vigilant and following these basic protection measures, users can help protect themselves from these types of scams.
