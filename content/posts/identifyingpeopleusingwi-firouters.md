Researchers have developed a method to identify people using Wi-Fi signals, rather than identifying people based on their use of Wi-Fi routers. This technique, known as WiFi sensing, involves analyzing how Wi-Fi signals interact with objects and people in a physical environment. By studying the reflections, scattering, and absorption of radio signals, researchers can infer details about the surrounding space.

The process works by comparing the expected behavior of Wi-Fi signals to how they are actually received. This allows researchers to create an image of the environment and detect the presence of individuals. According to Thorsten Strufe, a professor and study co-author, this method is similar to using a camera, but instead of light waves, it uses radio waves to recognize objects and people.

The use of WiFi sensing has the potential to create detailed images of a physical space and detect individuals within it. This technology could have various applications, although the specifics of its potential uses are not mentioned in the study. The development of WiFi sensing highlights the innovative ways in which researchers are exploring the use of Wi-Fi signals to gather information about the physical world.
