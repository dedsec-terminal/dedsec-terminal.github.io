Box has introduced new security features to enhance enterprise AI governance, providing organizations with greater control over AI agents working with sensitive content. The new capabilities include agent guardrails, prompt injection detection, and classification-based access policies, allowing customers to extend Box's security controls to both native and third-party agents. This expansion of Box's trusted governance framework enables organizations to confidently deploy and scale AI agents while maintaining oversight of how enterprise data is accessed and used.

The new security features address the primary concerns of security and privacy, which are the leading obstacles to deploying AI agents at scale. According to Box's 2026 State of Enterprise AI report, 90% of IT leaders identified security, regulatory, and trust concerns as the biggest barrier to granting AI agents access to enterprise content. Box's new controls provide protections at the content layer, ensuring that every agent action is intentional, permissioned, and auditable. The features include agent activity oversight, agent audit trails, and human-in-the-loop control, allowing organizations to detect and respond to suspicious behavior.

The new security and governance capabilities are designed to support various industries, including financial services, healthcare, legal, and insurance. For example, financial services firms can safeguard sensitive information by enforcing agent-level access controls, while healthcare organizations can secure patient data by enforcing classification-based access controls. Box's new controls establish a trust standard that allows enterprises to confidently scale both native and third-party AI agents across their most sensitive content. According to Amy Machado, Senior Research Director at IDC, Box's new security and governance controls address the primary barriers of privacy and unauthorized access, establishing a vital trust standard for enterprises to scale AI agents.
