Many companies have already adopted Artificial Intelligence (AI) without proper governance of access. This can happen quickly, such as when an employee connects a free AI tool to their Google Workspace without a security review. As a result, exposure to potential security risks can occur in various areas, including OAuth grants, agent credentials, and unclear authority when an agent acts.

Antoine Berton, CTO at Elba Security, identifies five key places where exposure can land, highlighting the need for proper governance. He draws a comparison to the "shadow SaaS decade," but notes that AI agents move faster and require more urgent attention. Fortunately, existing identity governance practices can be applied to address these risks, as they already answer key questions about inventory, ownership, and approval.

To mitigate these risks, Berton recommends two practical steps that any team can start on Monday morning. By applying existing identity governance principles to AI agents, companies can ensure that access is properly managed and security risks are minimized. The good news is that a new discipline is not required, and existing practices can be leveraged to secure AI workloads, as outlined in the downloadable resource "Secure Foundations for AI Workloads on AWS".
