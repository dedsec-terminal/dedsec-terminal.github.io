The 2026 Cybersecurity Stars Awards have announced their winners across 95 subcategories in four main award categories. The awards aim to recognize and appreciate the often-invisible work of cybersecurity professionals, teams, and companies that contribute to the industry. The winners were selected by an independent panel of judges who evaluated nominations based on innovation, impact, and technical excellence.

The awards cover a wide range of subcategories, including AI security, post-quantum cryptography, threat exposure management, and zero trust security, among others. Notably, some subcategories have multiple winners, as the awards recognize every entry that meets the standard, rather than just one per category. The winners list is available at awards.thehackernews.com/winners/2026/, showcasing the diverse range of cybersecurity work being recognized.

The Cybersecurity Stars Awards provide a unique opportunity to acknowledge the important work of cybersecurity professionals and companies that often go unnoticed. The awards will open for nominations again later this year, and interested parties can join a waiting list to be notified when nominations open for the 2027 awards. The event serves as a celebration of the work that prevents security breaches and ensures the safety of digital systems, highlighting the importance of recognizing and appreciating cybersecurity efforts.
