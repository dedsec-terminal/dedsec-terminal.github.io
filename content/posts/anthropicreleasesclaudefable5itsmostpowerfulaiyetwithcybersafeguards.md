Anthropic has released Claude Fable 5, its most powerful AI model yet, with built-in cyber safeguards to prevent misuse. The model is available to the public, while a twin version, Claude Mythos 5, with the same capabilities but without the safeguards, is only accessible to a vetted group of cyber defenders and critical infrastructure operators. The safeguards, which include classifiers that detect and prevent cyber attacks, biology, chemistry, and distillation requests, route flagged requests to a weaker model, Claude Opus 4.8.

The decision to release two versions of the model is due to the potential risks associated with giving the general public access to a model that can identify and exploit software vulnerabilities. Anthropic's testing has shown that the model can find and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in major operating systems and web browsers, and can even write remote code execution exploits. The company has designed the safeguards to block not just exploit development but also offensive cyber tasks, and has tuned them conservatively to minimize false positives.

The release of Fable 5 and Mythos 5 raises important questions about the responsible development and deployment of powerful AI models. Anthropic's approach is to prioritize safety and security, and to work with the cybersecurity community to identify and mitigate potential risks. The company has also announced a 30-day data retention requirement for all traffic on Fable 5, Mythos 5, and future models, to help detect novel attacks and jailbreaks. As other labs develop similarly capable models, the industry will need to consider how to balance the benefits of these models with the potential risks, and how to ensure that they are developed and deployed in a responsible and secure manner.
