Criminal IP has integrated its threat intelligence with OpenCTI, a platform that enables security teams to automatically convert IP addresses, domains, and URLs into structured intelligence. This integration allows for the automatic enrichment of ingested indicators with various types of intelligence, including infrastructure intelligence, dual-perspective reputation scoring, vulnerability data, and phishing analysis. The enriched data is then structured as OpenCTI entities and relationships, facilitating investigation and analysis.

The integration provides several key features, including contextual risk scoring, infrastructure intelligence, and service exposure and vulnerability correlation. These features enable analysts to prioritize high-risk infrastructure, map vulnerabilities and attack surfaces, and gain insight into whether an IP is malicious or exploitable. Additionally, the integration provides threat labeling and behavioral signals, domain and phishing intelligence, and infrastructure mapping. These features help analysts to identify hosting patterns, regional clustering, and other indicators of malicious activity.

The integration of Criminal IP with OpenCTI has several key use cases, including SOC triage and alert validation, threat hunting and infrastructure pivoting, and phishing and campaign analysis. By utilizing the enriched relationships and intelligence provided by the integration, security teams can validate suspicious IPs and domains, trace connected infrastructure and attacker operations, and identify malicious domains and supporting infrastructure. This enables security teams to prioritize high-risk indicators, track broader campaign patterns, and ultimately improve their threat detection and response capabilities.
