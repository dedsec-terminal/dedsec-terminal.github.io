Anthropic has released Claude Sonnet 5, the latest version of its general-purpose AI model, which boasts improved capabilities in reasoning, coding, tool use, and knowledge work. The model can autonomously make plans, use tools like browsers and terminals, and complete tasks. According to Anthropic, Sonnet 5 demonstrates an overall lower rate of undesirable behaviors compared to its predecessor, Sonnet 4.6, and is generally safer to use in agentic contexts.

Claude Sonnet 5 includes robust cybersecurity safeguards that detect and block dangerous activity in real-time, similar to those found in Opus 4.7 and 4.8. These safeguards are enabled by default and are less restrictive than those in Fable 5, as Anthropic considers Sonnet 5 to pose a lower overall cybersecurity risk. The model is also part of Anthropic's Cyber Verification Program, which provides approved organizations with access to reduced guardrails for legitimate security research.

In terms of performance, Sonnet 5 outperformed Sonnet 4.6 across all effort levels and matched Opus 4.8 on some tasks. It also demonstrated improved resistance to malicious requests and prompt injection attacks, and scored lower in Anthropic's automated behavioral audit. Claude Sonnet 5 is available on all Claude plans, with pricing set at $2 per million input tokens and $10 per million output tokens until August 31, 2026, after which the price will increase to $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens.
