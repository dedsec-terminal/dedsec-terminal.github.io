Here is a summary of the article in 3 concise paragraphs:

INTERPOL has released a report highlighting a significant increase in cybercrime across Asia and the South Pacific. The report reveals that phishing has become the most widespread and financially damaging form of cybercrime, with over 10,000 cases reported in a third of countries in the region between January 2024 and March 2025. Additionally, over half of INTERPOL member countries reported that cybercrime accounted for at least 30% of all crimes recorded nationally.

The report also notes a surge in ransomware attacks, deepfake and AI-driven scams, and other sophisticated cybercrimes. The region experienced over 135,000 ransomware-related attacks in 2024, primarily targeting the real estate, manufacturing, and financial services sectors. Transnational organized crime syndicates have also set up extensive scam centers, using forced labor to carry out investment scams and "romance baiting" scams, resulting in $37 billion in regional cybercrime losses.

To combat the rising threat of cybercrime, law enforcement organizations across the region are scaling up joint efforts, supported by INTERPOL. These efforts include coordinating operations against cybercriminal infrastructure, collaborative investigations, specialized training initiatives, and creating policies to improve cyber resilience. The report highlights the need for strengthened operational cooperation, information sharing, and cyber resilience to protect communities and critical infrastructure from the evolving cyber threat landscape.
