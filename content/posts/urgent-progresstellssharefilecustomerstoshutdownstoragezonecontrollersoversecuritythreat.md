Progress Software has instructed ShareFile customers to shut down their Storage Zone Controllers due to a "credible external security threat". The company has temporarily disabled access to the affected accounts as a precautionary measure, stating that it has no indication of unauthorized access to any ShareFile accounts or data. The threat only affects the Storage Zone Controller, a server that allows companies to store files on their own storage while using ShareFile's cloud to share and manage them.

The order to shut down the controllers was made public after a customer posted the company's email to Reddit, and Progress has confirmed the disruption on its status page. The fact that Progress is telling customers to shut down the controllers rather than patch them suggests that there is no fix available yet, and the company is working to close a newly found flaw. The company's statement that no accounts or data were accessed is carefully worded, and does not rule out potential trouble on the controllers themselves.

ShareFile customers are advised to follow the shutdown order and keep the affected controllers offline until Progress provides further guidance. Customers should also confirm that their version is current and handle any internet-reachable controllers as a possible incident. This is not the first time ShareFile has faced a security threat, having previously been exploited in 2023, and Progress has also dealt with a mass file-transfer attack on its MOVEit product. The central question remains unanswered, with Progress working to resolve the issue but not yet disclosing the nature of the threat or when customers can safely bring their controllers back online.
