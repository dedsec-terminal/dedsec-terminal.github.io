The article discusses how the European Union, particularly France, is accelerating its efforts to reduce its dependence on US-based technology due to concerns over data security, the Trump administration's unpredictability, and changing prices. The French government is developing and deploying its own technology for government officials, including a video-calling platform called Visio, and a set of productivity tools called "LaSuite". The goal is to shift to as many French, European, and open-source technology alternatives as possible in the coming years.

The move is part of a broader trend in Europe, with other countries such as the Netherlands, Austria, Belgium, Denmark, and Finland also pushing for digital sovereignty. The French government has already started using its own technology, with over 40,000 government staff using Visio, and plans to move away from US-based technology such as Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Gmail by 2027.

The article highlights the importance of open-source software in this effort, with the French government working with other countries and private firms to develop its tools. The use of open-source software allows for greater control and flexibility, as well as the ability to reuse and contribute to existing code.

The move away from US-based technology is driven by concerns over data security, with the French government requiring that all data handled by its alternatives be processed in France and stored with providers who have approval from the country's cybersecurity agency. The article also notes that the US Cloud Act, which gives US law enforcement the ability to request access to data stored on servers internationally, is a major concern for European countries.

While the effort to reduce dependence on US-based technology is gaining momentum, the article notes that it will be challenging to completely untangle European countries from American technology. US firms dominate all major software layers, and many European countries depend on US tech firms for critical national security functions. However, the use of open-source technology and the development of digital public infrastructure, such as India's "India Stack", may provide a way forward for European countries to reduce their dependence on US-based technology.

Key points:

* The EU, particularly France, is accelerating its efforts to reduce its dependence on US-based technology due to concerns over data security, the Trump administration's unpredictability, and changing prices.
* The French government is developing and deploying its own technology for government officials, including a video-calling platform called Visio, and a set of productivity tools called "LaSuite".
* The use of open-source software is a key part of this effort
