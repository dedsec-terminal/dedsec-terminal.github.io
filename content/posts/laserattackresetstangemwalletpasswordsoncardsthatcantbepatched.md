Researchers at Ledger's Donjon security team have discovered a vulnerability in Tangem crypto wallet cards that allows an attacker to reset the card's password using a precisely timed laser pulse. This attack requires physical access to the card, a $250,000 lab setup, and cutting the card open, leaving visible damage. The vulnerability is due to a flaw in the password reset feature, which can be exploited by disturbing the chip's circuitry with a laser pulse, allowing the attacker to set a new password without knowing the old one.

The attack is not a concern for most users, as it requires significant resources and physical access to the card. However, users who have lost or stolen cards with significant value should take action to protect their funds. Tangem cards cannot be updated with software patches, so the flaw cannot be fixed. The company has downplayed the vulnerability, stating that it is a lab-only physical method that is not unique to their cards and that the cost and difficulty of the attack make it impractical for most attackers.

The discovery highlights the importance of physical security for crypto wallets. Users who have lost or stolen cards with significant value should move their funds to a new card or use a seed phrase to protect their assets. The vulnerability is not the first time Donjon has used laser fault injection to break a hardware wallet, having previously demonstrated a similar attack on the Trezor Safe 7. The research emphasizes the need for users to prioritize physical security and take precautions to protect their crypto assets, even if the attack is difficult and expensive to execute.
