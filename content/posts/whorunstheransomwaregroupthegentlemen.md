The Gentlemen is a ransomware group that has emerged as the second most active by victim count, with at least 332 published victims since its inception in mid-2025. The group operates on a "ransomware-as-a-service" (RaaS) model, paying affiliates 90% of any ransom paid by victims, which has attracted a talented pool of hackers. The group targets internet-facing devices, such as VPNs and firewalls, and can encrypt entire networks within hours.

Researchers at Check Point Software have identified the administrator of The Gentlemen as a individual using the nickname Zeta88, who was previously known as Hastalamuerte. Further investigation by Intel 471 and other cyber intelligence firms has revealed that Hastalamuerte is a Russian-speaking person who has registered on numerous cybercrime forums since 2019. The individual has used various email addresses, phone numbers, and usernames, including a GitHub account under the username SantaMuerte, which is linked to malware tools and exploits.

The real-life identity of Hastalamuerte has been linked to Alexander Andreevich Yapaev, a 36-year-old from Izhevsk, Russia. Yapaev's phone number and email address have been connected to various online accounts, including a LinkedIn profile where he lists himself as the head of B2B marketing at a Russian company. The fact that Yapaev appears to have made little effort to hide his real-life identity is not uncommon among Russian cybercriminals, who may feel insulated from prosecution due to the Russian government's tolerance of cybercrime activity within its borders. Yapaev's early posts on crime forums show a relatively unsophisticated hacker, and it is likely that he made basic operational security mistakes that ultimately led to his identification.
