Anastasia Tikhonova, Global Threat Research Lead at Group-IB, emphasizes the importance of incorporating software supply chain security into daily operations. She suggests that teams should utilize a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) as a tool for daily tasks such as vulnerability triage, vendor access reviews, and incident response, rather than just filing it away as a compliance document.

Tikhonova highlights the evolving threat landscape, citing Group-IB's High-Tech Crime Trend Report 2026, which reveals that supply chain attacks are increasingly linking phishing, ransomware, and data breaches through inherited trust. She provides guidance on how to prioritize exposed systems, define a compromise window, and contain stolen credentials. Additionally, she discusses the importance of vendor risk scoring based on access and blast radius.

To stay ahead of attackers, who are now using AI to compress attack timelines from weeks to minutes, Tikhonova stresses the need for a proactive approach to software supply chain security. She also references a guide that outlines the limitations of automated pentesting, implying that a comprehensive security strategy requires a combination of automated and manual approaches to effectively mitigate supply chain risks and protect against evolving threats.
