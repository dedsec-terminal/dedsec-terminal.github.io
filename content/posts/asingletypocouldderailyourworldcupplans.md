The FBI has issued a warning about cybercriminals spoofing Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) websites ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup. These attackers are using a tactic called typosquatting, where they register lookalike domains with small spelling changes or different domain endings to impersonate FIFA websites and services. This can lead to users handing over personal information, such as names, addresses, and banking details, to fake sites.

The fake sites are being used to steal personal information, sell counterfeit World Cup tickets and hospitality packages, and support additional fraud schemes. The FBI has identified several spoofed domains, including those with minor misspellings or alternative top-level domains. Additionally, attackers are registering domains that impersonate FIFA-related services and subdomains, such as jobs-fifa.com. The demand for World Cup tickets is extremely high, with over 150 million ticket requests submitted within the first 15 days of sales, making it an attractive target for scammers.

To avoid scams, the FBI has published a list of identified spoofed domains and shared safety tips. Users are advised to be cautious when entering website addresses and to verify the authenticity of websites before providing personal information. The risk of disruption is high due to the massive demand for World Cup tickets and the global spotlight on the event. Experts recommend monitoring both cyber and physical domains for risk indicators to preempt potential disruptions. By being aware of these risks and taking precautions, users can help protect themselves from falling victim to these scams.
