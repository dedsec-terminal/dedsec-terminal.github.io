Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

This week's cybersecurity threats include a range of malicious activities, from game cheats that drop spyware to fake installers that deploy remote access tools (RATs). Researchers have discovered 11 malicious NuGet packages that pose as game utilities but actually download and execute a second-stage Python payload. Additionally, a sophisticated adversary known as UAT-11795 has been targeting users in the US and Europe with trojanized installers that deliver a Python-based RAT and a command-and-control (C2) memory implant.

Other notable threats include a previously undocumented ransomware family called Spirals, which was used to target an IT services company in South Asia. The ransomware payload was deployed across the network in less than 24 hours, and the attacker demanded a ransom in exchange for not publishing stolen data. The US Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has also added two vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, including an improper privilege management vulnerability in Oracle E-Business Suite and an overly restrictive account lockout mechanism vulnerability in KNX Association KNX Protocol.

In other news, authorities have dismantled a 700-person scam network that was conducting investment fraud, and Spanish National Police have disrupted a cybercrime network that stole and laundered around €140 million through fake investment platforms and other means. Meanwhile, researchers have demonstrated three attack techniques that can be used to misuse Windows' bind links and evade endpoint detection and response (EDR) products. A financially motivated threat actor has also set up over 290 fake GitHub repositories to distribute a Windows infostealer that targets cryptocurrency wallets and other sensitive information.
