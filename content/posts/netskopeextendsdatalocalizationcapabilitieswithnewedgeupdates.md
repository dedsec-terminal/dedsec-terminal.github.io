Netskope has enhanced its NewEdge Network infrastructure, expanding its data sovereignty capabilities to more regions than any other SASE cloud provider. The NewEdge Network architecture provides national data localization features that address requirements for network transport, data processing, and metadata governance in major regions worldwide. This allows Netskope to extend its coverage to additional countries and offer third-party validation to help customers meet compliance and data localization requirements.

The NewEdge Network enables organizations to select configurations that support the four core components of data localization: network transport, data processing, domestic storage, and metadata governance. This comprehensive approach to data location and control gives customers complete visibility and control over their data, whether it's in use, at rest, or in motion. Netskope's support for data sovereignty is fundamental to its customer service, allowing customers to monitor, inspect, and control traffic flows and metadata within national boundaries.

The expansion of NewEdge, which now comprises over 120 data centers across more than 80 regions, ensures that compliance does not come at the cost of performance. The recently announced NewEdge AI Fast Path provides optimized routing and local processing for AI workloads, eliminating the trade-off between strict security and governance controls and the speed required for modern AI applications. According to Joe DePalo, Chief Platform Officer at Netskope, this enables customers to meet the demand for digital control while optimizing the user experience for business-critical AI, web, cloud, and SaaS traffic.
