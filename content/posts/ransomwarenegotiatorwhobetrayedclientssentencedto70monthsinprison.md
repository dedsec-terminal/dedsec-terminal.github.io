A former ransomware negotiator, Angelo Martino, has been sentenced to 70 months in prison for betraying his clients' trust. Martino, who worked at incident response firm DigitalMint, shared confidential client information with the BlackCat ransomware group, including negotiating positions, insurance policy limits, and internal assessments. This information helped the group maximize ransom demands against five organizations.

Martino's actions were part of a larger scheme involving two other individuals, Ryan Goldberg and Kevin Martin, who also worked in the cybersecurity industry. The three men used their expertise to support the BlackCat ransomware group's activities, with Martino and Martin working as ransomware negotiators and Goldberg serving as an incident response manager. The conspirators received approximately $1.2 million in Bitcoin from one victim and laundered the money through cryptocurrency transactions.

As a result of the investigation, federal authorities seized over $10 million in cryptocurrency and other assets linked to Martino, including luxury vehicles, a fishing boat, and a food truck. Martino pleaded guilty to conspiring to interfere with interstate commerce through extortion and will face a restitution hearing to determine how much he must repay his victims. His co-conspirators, Martin and Goldberg, were each sentenced to 48 months in prison for their roles in the conspiracy. The BlackCat/ALPHV ransomware group was disrupted by law enforcement in late 2023, with its data leak site being seized.
