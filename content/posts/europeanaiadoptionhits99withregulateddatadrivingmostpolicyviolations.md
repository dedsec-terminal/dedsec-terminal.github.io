European organizations have achieved near-total adoption of AI tools, with 99% of companies using them and 65% of individual users actively interacting with AI applications. This widespread adoption has led to a significant increase in data exposure incidents, with regulated data being the most common type of information involved in policy violations, accounting for 59% of incidents. The use of AI-powered features in everyday productivity tools has also become more prevalent, with 95% of users now touching applications that incorporate AI-powered features indirectly.

The report by Netskope Threat Labs found that companies in Europe have made efforts to move towards sanctioned environments, with the share of users on personal AI accounts dropping from 79% to 43% over the past year. However, a countertrend has emerged, with 15% of users switching between personal and enterprise accounts, indicating that shadow AI activity continues to be a concern. The most widely used AI services in Europe include ChatGPT, Anthropic Claude, and Google Gemini, with Claude's adoption curve steepening sharply in September 2025.

To address the risks associated with AI adoption, Netskope Threat Labs recommends pairing data loss prevention controls with application-specific governance. This is particularly important given the blurred lines between work and personal data flows, as well as the use of reputable cloud storage services to host malicious payloads. By implementing these measures, organizations can better protect themselves against data exposure incidents and ensure that their AI adoption is secure and compliant with regulatory requirements.
