The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) has issued new guidelines to help organizations protect themselves against AI-assisted cyber threats. With the increasing use of artificial intelligence (AI) and large language models (LLMs) by threat actors, the time required to identify, weaponize, and exploit vulnerabilities has significantly decreased. As a result, CERT-In is recommending that organizations patch critical security vulnerabilities in internet-exposed systems within 12 hours of being flagged, where feasible.

CERT-In has outlined several defensive principles to reduce exposure and better respond to AI-assisted cyber threats. These include adopting a Zero Trust approach, implementing a defense-in-depth strategy, monitoring and reducing exposure to security vulnerabilities, and embedding a secure-by-design paradigm into systems and AI workflows. The agency is also urging organizations to implement continuous, risk-based vulnerability and patch management practices to reduce exposure arising from security flaws and misconfigurations. Additionally, CERT-In has provided risk-based remediation times for different types of vulnerabilities, ranging from 12 hours for critical externally exposed vulnerabilities to 5 days for high-severity vulnerabilities.

The guidelines emphasize the need for organizations to be proactive and prepared in the face of evolving AI-assisted cyber threats. CERT-In recommends that organizations continuously reassess exposure, validate security controls, strengthen resilience capabilities, and enhance operational preparedness through ongoing audits, monitoring, testing, and coordinated cybersecurity governance. By following these guidelines, organizations can reduce their exposure to AI-assisted cyber threats and maintain cyber resilience in the face of rapidly evolving threats. The guidelines also highlight the importance of keeping pace with frontier AI-driven cyber developments and rigorously enforcing baseline cybersecurity controls to stay ahead of potential threats.
