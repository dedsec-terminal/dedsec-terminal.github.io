Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

Despite having more visibility than ever, organizations still face significant security risks due to the challenges of executing tasks between tools. The growing tech stack and adoption of AI and automation have not solved the problems of outages, slow threat response, and misconfigurations. The issue lies in the operational layer, where network security teams must gather context, validate ownership, and route tickets across multiple systems, leading to manual processes that are time-consuming and prone to human error.

The work between tools creates risks in three critical areas: alert triage and incident response, access and change management, and hybrid and multi-environment operations. In these areas, manual coordination can lead to delays, missed threats, and security incidents. For example, alert triage and incident response require manual gathering of context, which can lead to delays in identifying and remediating issues. Similarly, access and change management rely heavily on humans, leading to inconsistent validations and gaps in policy enforcement.

To address these challenges, forward-thinking organizations are adopting intelligent workflows that connect systems, teams, approvals, automation, and decision-making across all environments. Intelligent workflows combine deterministic automation, AI, and human judgment to orchestrate entire processes from beginning to end. By using intelligent workflows, organizations can standardize responses, reduce inconsistencies, and improve auditability, ultimately enhancing their network security posture and reducing risk. This approach enables teams to operate at scale, extend their capacity, and close the gap between detection and execution, making operational coordination as crucial as visibility itself.
