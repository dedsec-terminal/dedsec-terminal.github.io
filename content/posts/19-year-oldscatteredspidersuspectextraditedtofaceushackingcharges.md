A 19-year-old dual U.S. and Estonian citizen, Peter Stokes, has been extradited from Finland to face U.S. charges of conspiracy, computer intrusion, and fraud. Stokes, who went by the online handle "Bouquet," is accused of being a member of the hacking group Scattered Spider. He was arrested in April by Finnish police on an Interpol Red Notice and appeared in a Chicago federal court, where he was ordered to be held in custody.

Scattered Spider is a loose group of young people, mostly teenagers, spread across the U.S., U.K., and Europe. The group's main tactic is to trick companies' IT help desks into resetting passwords or approving logins, allowing them to steal files and demand ransom payments. The group has been linked to over 100 network intrusions, resulting in more than $100 million in ransom payments. Recent attacks have targeted various sectors, including casinos, retailers, airlines, and insurers.

Stokes' extradition is part of a broader crackdown on Scattered Spider, with several other members facing charges and convictions. To defend against such attacks, companies are advised to implement stricter identity checks and use sign-in keys that are resistant to phishing. Investigators are also warning that the group's tactics are being copied by other crews, and that companies should be vigilant in monitoring their systems and chat tools for potential intruders. The case highlights the importance of international cooperation in combating cybercrime and bringing perpetrators to justice.
