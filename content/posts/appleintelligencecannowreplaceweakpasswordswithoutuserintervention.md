Apple's next-generation Apple Intelligence system has introduced a new security feature in its Passwords app, allowing it to automatically replace weak or compromised passwords without user intervention. The app, which stores and manages passwords, passkeys, and other credentials, can now use Apple Intelligence and Safari to securely navigate websites and upgrade accounts to strong passwords. This update eliminates the need for manual password changes, making it easier for users to maintain secure online accounts.

The updated Passwords app uses a combination of on-device processing and Private Cloud Compute to process complex AI requests while protecting user data. Apple's privacy-focused architecture ensures that personal context is used to inform AI decisions without storing user data on cloud servers. This approach enables the system to provide personalized support while maintaining user privacy and security. The automatically updated passwords are saved in the Passwords app, allowing users to access them whenever needed.

While the new feature offers convenience and improved security, it also raises concerns about the potential risks of autonomous AI agents. Researchers warn that attackers could manipulate agent decisions, highlighting the need for safeguards such as policy checks and approval workflows. To mitigate these risks, users should maintain visibility and control over agent actions, reviewing and approving or rejecting changes to ensure that important decisions remain in human hands. By striking a balance between convenience and control, users can harness the benefits of Apple's updated Passwords app while minimizing potential security risks.
