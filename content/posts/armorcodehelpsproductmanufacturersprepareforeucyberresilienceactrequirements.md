The European Union's Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) is a major regulation that requires manufacturers of products with digital elements (PDEs) to remediate vulnerabilities without delay. As of September 11, 2026, manufacturers must meet strict reporting obligations for actively exploited vulnerabilities, including delivering 24-hour early warning notifications, 72-hour vulnerability notifications, and a final report within 14-days of a corrective or mitigating measure being available. Failure to comply will result in penalties of up to €15 million or 2.5% of global annual turnover.

To help manufacturers prepare for the CRA requirements, ArmorCode has announced new capabilities within its Agentic AI Platform. The platform provides a unified system of record that combines product security data, exploit-aware risk prioritization, disclosure workflow management, software bill of materials (SBOM) and vulnerability exploitability exchange (VEX) support, and continuous compliance reporting. This enables organizations to operationalize CRA requirements and meet the strict reporting obligations. The platform also incorporates exploitability data into risk prioritization workflows, helping teams focus on the vulnerabilities that matter most for CRA.

The ArmorCode Agentic AI Platform has been expanded to serve key CRA workflows and requirements, including native PDE classification and lifecycle tracking, exploit status and CRA notification status fields, and automated workflow orchestration and deadline tracking. The platform provides always-on dashboards, immutable audit trails, compliance reporting, exception management, and service level agreement tracking to support audit readiness. By using the ArmorCode platform, organizations can operationalize security, compliance, and disclosure at scale, reducing the risk of missed deadlines and significant financial penalties, and maintaining customer trust and confidence.
