Grafana has disclosed a security breach in which an unauthorized party obtained a token to access the company's GitHub environment, allowing them to download its codebase. Fortunately, the investigation found that no customer data or personal information was accessed, and there is no evidence of impact to customer systems or operations. The company immediately launched a forensic analysis, identified the source of the leak, and invalidated the compromised credentials, implementing extra security measures to prevent future unauthorized access.

The attacker attempted to extort Grafana by demanding a payment in exchange for not publishing the stolen database. However, the company has chosen not to pay the ransom, citing guidance from the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) that warns against negotiating with perpetrators. The FBI advises that paying ransoms can encourage perpetrators to target more victims and incentivize others to engage in similar activities. Grafana did not reveal when the incident occurred or which codebase was downloaded, but the company offers various solutions, including Grafana Cloud, a fully-managed observability platform.

The breach has been claimed by a cybercrime group named CoinbaseCartel, which is believed to be an offshoot of the ShinyHunters, Scattered Spider, and LAPSUS$ ecosystems. CoinbaseCartel is a data extortion crew that focuses on data theft and extortion, and has reportedly amassed 170 victims across various industries. The group's claim of responsibility was reported by Hackmanac and Ransomware.live, but Grafana has not confirmed the attribution. The incident highlights the importance of robust security measures and the need for companies to be prepared to respond to potential breaches and extortion attempts.

The decision by Grafana not to pay the ransom is in line with the FBI's guidance, and it will be interesting to see how the situation develops. The company's transparency in disclosing the breach and its response is commendable, and it serves as a reminder to other organizations to prioritize cybersecurity and be prepared for potential incidents. The fact that CoinbaseCartel has claimed responsibility for the breach suggests that the group is actively targeting companies and attempting to extort them, highlighting the need for vigilance and robust security measures to prevent similar incidents.
