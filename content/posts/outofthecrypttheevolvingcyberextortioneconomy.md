Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the evolving cyber extortion economy:

The cyber extortion economy is shifting away from traditional ransomware attacks, with a notable decrease in the use of encryption for extortion-related cases. In 2025, the total percentage of cases involving encryption dropped to 78%, down from near-or-above 90% levels observed between 2021-2024. Instead, threat actors are focusing on data theft and extortion, with 15% of incidents involving data theft and extortion in 2025, up from 2% in 2020. This shift is driven by advanced backup and recovery performance, endpoint maturity, exfiltration speed, and increased pressure from regulatory frameworks.

Threat actors are using various techniques to pressure victims into paying ransom demands, including single and double extortion techniques. Some groups, like TGR-CRI-1135, use software supply chain compromise to gain initial access, while others, like Bling Libra, use vishing to trick victims into divulging sensitive information. These groups often partner with ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) and extortion-as-a-service (EaaS) operators to monetize their activities. The use of data leak sites, DDoS attacks, and information leaks to media outlets are also common tactics used to extort victims. The regulatory landscape, with strict mandates like the SEC's 4-day disclosure window and GDPR's 72-hour reporting rule, has created a countdown clock that allows threat actors to force rapid negotiations.

The evolving cyber extortion economy is expected to become even more complex with the arrival of frontier AI models. These models can accelerate the speed and scale of attacks, making it easier for threat actors to find and chain vulnerabilities together to exploit flaws in applications and infrastructure. The use of AI-powered call center platforms can also automate vishing attacks, lowering the barriers to entry for less sophisticated cybercriminals. As a result, organizations must be prepared to secure their environments from the inevitable weaponization of frontier AI
