Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the new infosec products of the week:

Several companies have released new infosec products, including Fortinet, which has announced FortiSOC, a unified cloud-delivered security operations center (SOC) platform. This platform brings together six security operations functions into a single Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) experience, embedding agentic AI to investigate and correlate alerts. Legit Security has also launched new remediation agents that use agentic AI to prioritize issues, generate fixes, and confirm results in AppSec remediation and risk reduction.

Other companies have introduced products focused on specific areas of security, such as ArmorCode, which has announced new Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) capabilities to help manufacturers prepare for EU cybersecurity regulations. Flip has expanded its platform with digital identity, no-code apps, and AI automation, while Tigera has introduced a unified control plane for Kubernetes-based AI agent security. WitnessAI has also launched Agentic Control, which secures AI agents, tools, and Model Context Protocol (MCP) server access with a single control plane.

Additional releases include Blue Planet's unified network change governance solution, which reduces risk by unifying device configuration, change, and lifecycle management across multi-vendor networks. Barracuda Networks has introduced AI-powered email security with automated threat response, delivering protection against evolving AI-driven threats. These new products demonstrate the ongoing innovation in the infosec industry, with a focus on using AI and automation to improve security operations and reduce risk.
