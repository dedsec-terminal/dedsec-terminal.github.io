Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

A cybercrime crew known as "Play" has claimed to have hacked MyPillow, a company owned by Mike Lindell, who is running for governor of Minnesota. The group, which has affected over 900 organizations since 2022, claims to have stolen private and personal data, including client documents, budget, payroll, and tax records. However, Lindell has denied the allegations, calling it a "political hit job" due to his involvement in the 2020 election and his support for Donald Trump.

In other cybersecurity news, ransomware groups are becoming increasingly aggressive, with some groups sending people to steal data directly from companies in person. The FBI has warned about the Silent Ransom Group (SRG), which is targeting law firms and using social engineering techniques to gain access to computers. Additionally, scammers are using real hotel reservation data to conduct spear-phishing campaigns, potentially accessing customer data from 350 hotels and vacation rentals worldwide.

The use of AI and surveillance technology is also raising concerns, with companies like BusPatrol installing cameras in school buses that can record license plate numbers and provide data to law enforcement without a warrant. Meanwhile, a study has found that dropping ShotSpotter gunshot detection technology in Chicago has improved police response times for non-gunshot 911 calls. The study suggests that ShotSpotter alerts may have been occupying officers with false positives, delaying their response to other critical calls. These developments highlight the evolving landscape of cybersecurity and surveillance, and the need for individuals and organizations to stay vigilant and adapt to emerging threats.
