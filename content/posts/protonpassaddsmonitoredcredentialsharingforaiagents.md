Proton Pass, a secure password manager, has introduced a new feature that allows users to share credentials with AI agents through monitored access tokens. This feature enables users to grant AI agents limited access to specific items, such as passwords or credit card information, while maintaining control and visibility over the agent's activities. To access the feature, users must have a Proton Pass plan, including Pass Plus, Pass Family, Pass Professional, or Proton Workspace.

The access tokens can be easily set up by creating a new token in the Proton Pass settings and copying the setup instructions to the AI agent. Users can then restrict the agent's visibility to specific vaults, allowing it to perform tasks such as reviewing bank transactions or generating reports. The tokens support read-only permissions, ensuring that AI agents cannot create, edit, or modify stored items. Additionally, users can set expiration periods for the tokens and revoke permissions at any time.

The use of access tokens is recorded in an activity log, allowing users to monitor how AI agents interact with shared items. This feature provides an added layer of security and control, enabling users to review and manage the activities of their AI agents. With Proton Pass's end-to-end encryption, users can trust that their data remains protected, even when shared with AI agents. The introduction of monitored credential sharing for AI agents enhances the security and functionality of Proton Pass, making it a more attractive option for individuals and organizations looking to integrate AI into their workflows.
