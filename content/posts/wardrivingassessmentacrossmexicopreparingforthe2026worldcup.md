Here is a 3-paragraph summary of the wardriving assessment across Mexico:

In preparation for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, Kaspersky GReAT conducted a wardriving assessment in three major cities in Mexico: Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey. The goal was to analyze the characteristics, deployment patterns, security configurations, and potential exposure risks of public Wi-Fi infrastructure in urban wireless environments. The assessment involved collecting wireless information using passive wireless reconnaissance techniques, including SSID analysis, default router configurations, and wireless security configurations.

The results of the assessment revealed that approximately 34% of the detected networks retained default SSID naming conventions, while 66% used customized identifiers. The analysis also identified recurring SSID naming conventions associated with ISP-provided deployments, suggesting extensive standardization of wireless infrastructure deployment. Additionally, the assessment found that many networks used the physical MAC address of a Wi-Fi access point (BSSID) as the visible SSID, exposing hardware-level information that could facilitate vendor fingerprinting and targeted reconnaissance activities.

The assessment also identified the most common wireless equipment manufacturers and ISP deployments across the three locations. The results showed a strong concentration of wireless infrastructure among a limited number of vendors, including Huawei Technologies and MediaTek-based devices. The widespread presence of standard vendor platforms may facilitate infrastructure fingerprinting and large-scale targeting of known device-specific vulnerabilities. The findings suggest a high degree of deployment standardization across networks associated with major residential internet providers, which could pose security risks for visitors to the 2026 World Cup. Overall, the assessment highlights the need for improved wireless security configurations and awareness of potential exposure risks in public Wi-Fi infrastructure.
