Geopolitical cyber threats are increasingly becoming a concern for security teams, with attacks coming from unexpected actors. Roman Sannikov, Global Research Coordinator at iCOUNTER, emphasizes the importance of including geopolitics in every security team's threat model. This is due to the rise of open and simmering conflicts around the world, which can lead to attacks from previously unknown adversaries.

Sannikov highlights the need to widen the scope of security teams to include threats that may be two or three steps beyond the usual list of adversaries. This includes monitoring traffic heading to unexpected regions and treating HR as a key part of defense against fake employees and deepfakes. Additionally, security teams should connect with physical and executive protection teams to ensure a comprehensive defense strategy.

The video also covers specific threats such as DDoS attacks as a form of opinion warfare and the pre-positioning of threats like Volt Typhoon inside telecom networks. When a company becomes linked to a geopolitical cause, the number of potential attackers increases, making it essential for security teams to be proactive and vigilant in their defense strategies. By taking a broader approach to security, companies can better protect themselves against these emerging threats.
