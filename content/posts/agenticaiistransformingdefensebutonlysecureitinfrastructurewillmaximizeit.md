The integration of agentic AI in defense networks has the potential to significantly impact US defense and intelligence operations. However, recent incidents, such as the reported unauthorized access to Anthropic's Claude Mythos model, have highlighted the importance of secure IT infrastructure in maximizing the benefits of AI. The US government's deployment of AI capabilities on classified networks requires a robust security framework to mitigate risks and ensure the trustworthy operation of AI models.

The successful adoption of AI in defense networks depends on three key areas: the quality and security of the data entering the model, governed access to the AI system, and the integrity of the classification layer when the AI agent interacts with external systems. AI models are only as trustworthy as the data they process, and the networks they operate on, making it essential to ensure that training data and commercial models are securely integrated into classified environments. Additionally, access to AI systems must be strictly controlled to prevent unauthorized access and maintain security boundaries.

To deploy AI responsibly and securely, defense and intelligence organizations must prioritize the development of a secure network fabric that can support the operation of AI models at scale. This requires the implementation of cross-domain capabilities, hardware-enforced protection, and strict access controls to prevent threats and policy violations. By building security into the foundation of AI systems, organizations can ensure that AI models operate trustworthy and effectively, ultimately delivering the expected decision advantage in mission-critical operations.
