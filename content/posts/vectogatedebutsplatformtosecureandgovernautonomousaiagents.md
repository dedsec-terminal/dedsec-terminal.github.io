Vectogate has launched a platform designed to secure and govern autonomous AI agents, providing organizations with centralized control over these agents as they take on increasingly autonomous tasks. The platform aims to address the key governance challenges posed by enterprise AI, particularly as AI agents access sensitive data and internal business systems. Initially available in private beta, companies can apply for early access to the platform.

The need for AI governance is growing rapidly as organizations integrate AI agents into business-critical processes, facing increasing security risks and governance and compliance requirements. Vectogate's platform is designed to meet the requirements of major regulatory frameworks, including the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, ISO/IEC 42001, and the EU AI Act. The platform acts as a governance layer between AI agents and the existing IT landscape, managing access rights, enforcing security and compliance policies, and logging all interactions.

Vectogate's platform provides a single dashboard for managing all AI models, clouds, and tool servers, with identical governance logic and audit functions available in both SaaS and on-premise deployment models. The platform uses a dual-layer approach, guiding the agent's reasoning and acting as a safety net to catch and block any unauthorized actions. Access permissions are granted granularly, with default-deny and least-privilege as the standard, and every tool call is logged with context and grouped into sessions for chain traceability. This provides companies with transparency and control over their AI agents, addressing the growing need for AI governance and security.
