The article discusses a quote about technology and problem-solving that has been attributed to the author, but actually originated from Roger Needham, a cryptologist. The quote states: "If you think technology will solve your problems, you don't understand technology and you don't understand your problems." The author modified the quote from Needham's original version, which was: "If you think cryptography can solve your problem, you don't understand your problem and you don't understand cryptography." The quote has been used by the author in various forms since 2000 and has now been quoted by artist Laurie Anderson in her new album. The article also explores the unclear origins of the quote, with multiple people claiming to have said it first, including Robert Morris and Butler Lampson. The author reflects on the evolution of the quote and the importance of giving credit to the original source, while also acknowledging that ideas can be shared and built upon without always knowing their exact origin.
