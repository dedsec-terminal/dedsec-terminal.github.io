BlackFog has announced the general availability of ADX Vision for macOS, a platform that provides shadow AI detection, governance, and prevention for Apple endpoints. This release allows enterprises to apply a single, consistent AI data-loss policy across Windows and macOS devices, stopping sensitive data from leaving the organization through unsanctioned Large Language Models (LLMs). With this launch, BlackFog addresses a significant blind spot in enterprise AI governance, as nearly half of employees use AI tools without employer approval.

The launch of ADX Vision for macOS marks a significant milestone in addressing exfiltration threats. Traditional approaches to shadow AI security, such as browser extensions, network proxies, or SaaS-side integrations, have limitations as they cannot see AI-bound data flows originating from native desktop applications or local agents. In contrast, ADX Vision provides direct visibility into AI-bound data flows on the device, before data is encrypted or transmitted, regardless of the application or browser used. This comprehensive visibility is achieved without relying on browser extensions, network proxies, or certificate interception.

According to Dr. Darren Williams, CEO of BlackFog, the endpoint is the right place to stop data from leaving the organization, whether it's ransomware or shadow AI. By bringing ADX Vision to macOS, BlackFog applies its architectural approach natively to the platform where many sensitive conversations with AI are happening, such as on the laptops of executives, engineers, and creative teams. This launch extends BlackFog's endpoint-native architecture, which has disrupted ransomware double extortion since 2019, to now include shadow AI, providing a single, consistent AI data-loss policy across Windows and macOS devices.
