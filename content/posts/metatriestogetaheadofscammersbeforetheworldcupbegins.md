With the 2026 FIFA World Cup approaching, scammers are expected to target football fans, particularly those traveling to the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The FBI has warned of cybercriminals spoofing FIFA websites to steal personal information, sell fake tickets, and promote fraudulent hospitality packages. As a result, Meta is taking proactive measures to combat scams and reduce online abuse targeting fans and players during the tournament.

Meta is investing in advanced AI, tools, and partnerships with law enforcement and industry peers to identify and disrupt scam campaigns. The company has already collaborated with Visa to disrupt a scam network using FIFA World Cup 2026 branding, which led to the identification of Facebook-linked pages directing users to fraudulent gambling websites. Additionally, Meta plans to display warnings to users searching for World Cup tickets on Facebook or visiting related Groups to help them identify fraudulent offers and avoid scams.

To address the expected increase in online abuse during the tournament, Meta uses AI systems and user reports to identify content that violates its policies on bullying, harassment, violent threats, and hateful conduct. The company claims to have removed 2.6 million pieces of hateful content from Facebook and Instagram between October and December 2025, with over 74% detected before users reported it. Meta is also working closely with players, teams, and associations to ensure they are aware of its safety policies and features, and to gather feedback on how to improve its efforts to combat online abuse.
