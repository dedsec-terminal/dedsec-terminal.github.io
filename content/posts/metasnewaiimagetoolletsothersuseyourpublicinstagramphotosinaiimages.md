Meta has introduced a new AI image tool called Muse Image, which allows users to generate AI content using public Instagram posts and reels. The feature is enabled by default, and users can @-mention Instagram accounts to bring specific profiles into their images. This means that public Instagram photos can be used to create new content, such as custom event invitations or personalized graphics, without the original user's explicit consent.

Users have the option to control how their content is used by going to their Instagram settings and turning off the "Allow people to create with and reuse your content" setting. However, it's worth noting that this setting only prevents future use of their content, and does not delete any existing content that has already been created using their photos or videos. Additionally, users under 18 with public accounts can only have their media reused by accounts that follow them, if their account settings allow for reuse.

The development of Muse Image is part of a broader industry trend of tech companies embedding AI into their products and making them opt-out rather than opt-in by default. Google has also recently introduced similar features, including a new Search Services History option that allows the company to store media to improve its AI models. These changes have raised concerns about user privacy and control over their online content. As Meta's new feature rolls out to more countries and platforms, including Facebook, Messenger, and WhatsApp, users will need to be aware of their settings and take steps to protect their online presence if they do not want their content to be used in AI-generated images.
