Nudge Security has introduced a browser-based discovery feature for shadow AI agents, expanding its AI security platform's capabilities. This new feature allows for the discovery of AI agents built on platforms that do not provide a public API for agent identity and inventory, such as Airbyte, ChatGPT Workspace Agents, and Zapier Agents. This development addresses a significant gap in the market, where other solutions rely solely on platform APIs that are not always available.

The browser-based discovery feature works through the Nudge Security browser extension, which passively observes and automatically adds AI agents to the organization's inventory when an employee interacts with them. Each discovered agent is mapped to its human creator and enriched with governance and risk insights, enabling teams to prioritize review and response. This feature complements Nudge Security's existing API-based AI agent discovery, providing two discovery channels that offer comprehensive coverage.

The expanded browser-based AI agent discovery capability is now available as part of Nudge Security's AI agent discovery research preview. Customers can enable it by toggling the feature on in the browser extension settings, while prospective customers can request access as part of a free trial. This development is designed to support emerging agentic AI governance programs by helping organizations build an auditable inventory of agents, understand who created them, and identify potential risks such as publicly accessible agents or hardcoded credentials.
