Ketch has introduced the Ketch Agent Network, a multi-agent AI orchestration layer designed to support enterprise privacy programs. This platform aims to continuously analyze and reason across various sources, systems, and obligations, including legal requirements, internal policies, and operational realities. The goal is to provide a unified AI-driven system that helps privacy teams manage their responsibilities more efficiently.

The Ketch Agent Network is designed to address the limitations of existing single-domain tools, such as chatbots and single-agent platforms, which often require significant human intervention. In contrast, the Ketch Agent Network can reason across multiple domains simultaneously, identifying gaps and deploying purpose-built agents to close them with minimal human input. This approach enables the platform to surface gaps with rigorous prioritization, reducing the workload for privacy teams.

The Ketch Agent Network offers several key capabilities, including agentic data mapping, risk management, and assessments. These features enable the platform to automatically discover and classify data assets, compare configurations against regulations and policies, and auto-populate assessments such as DPIAs and AI impact assessments. According to Tom Chavez, CEO of Ketch, the Ketch Agent Network is designed to sense and make sense of everything at once, finding gaps and taking action before risks become liabilities, and early production results are already demonstrating the effectiveness of this approach.
