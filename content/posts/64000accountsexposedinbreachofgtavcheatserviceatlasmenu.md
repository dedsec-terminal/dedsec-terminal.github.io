A data breach has occurred at Atlas Menu, a cheat service for Grand Theft Auto V and Counter-Strike 2, exposing approximately 64,000 user accounts. The breach included sensitive information such as email addresses, usernames, IP addresses, support tickets, and passwords hashed with bcrypt. The attacker claimed to have compromised all Atlas systems and leaked the database through a public GitHub repository as retaliation against an alleged scammer.

The breach is ironic given that Atlas Menu promotes secure authentication, enhanced privacy, and advanced encryption techniques on its website. Some Reddit users have reported that this is not the first security incident to affect Atlas Menu, although there is no public documentation or independent verification of earlier breaches. The reaction to the breach on Reddit has been largely unsympathetic, with many commenters arguing that users of game cheats do not deserve sympathy.

The incident follows a recent data breach at Rockstar Games, the developer behind Grand Theft Auto, which was claimed by the ShinyHunters hacking group. The breach highlights the risks associated with using cheat services, which are widely opposed by gamers due to the unfair advantage they provide and their potential to affect the gaming experience. The Atlas Menu breach serves as a reminder of the importance of robust security measures and the potential consequences of using cheat services.
