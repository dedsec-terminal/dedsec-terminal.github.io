Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

The use of period trackers has raised concerns about data privacy, with many apps sharing sensitive information with third-party companies. A Mozilla Foundation audit found that the astrology-themed period tracker Stardust scored the worst, sending users' reproductive health details to a data firm without their knowledge. In contrast, the nonprofit-run tracker Euki earned a perfect score for its robust privacy features, including the ability to set a PIN and schedule automatic deletion of data.

Russia's FSB has been sanctioned for a cyberattack on Poland's electric grid, which nearly caused outages in the country's electric and water utilities. The attack was initially attributed to the GRU, but Western governments have now confirmed that the FSB was responsible. This incident suggests that the FSB may be taking on more aggressive tendencies, similar to those of the GRU. Additionally, it has been reported that an alleged Russian state-sponsored hacker worked for the cybersecurity firm Kaspersky, which has been accused of having ties to the Russian government.

Other security and privacy concerns have been reported, including a real breach of the Department of Homeland Security's (DHS) data-sharing platform, which was twice ruled a false positive. The breach, which occurred two months ago, exposed highly sensitive information, and the incident highlights the challenges of detecting "living off the land" hacking techniques. Furthermore, a hack has exposed an AI music generator's scraping secrets, revealing that the company scraped millions of songs, lyrics, and podcasts from various sources to train its models. The breach also exposed account information for hundreds of thousands of customers, including emails, phone numbers, and payment records.
