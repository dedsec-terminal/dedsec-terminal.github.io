Nudge Security has introduced new automated capabilities to detect and remediate risky OAuth grants and browser extensions, which are increasingly exploited by attackers. The new agents continuously analyze these potential threats, flag what's risky, and automate remediation with human oversight. This development aims to address the growing attack surfaces in enterprises, where traditional security programs struggle to keep up with the sheer number of apps, extensions, and third-party connections.

The new agents, including OAuth Grant Risk Analyst and Browser Extension Risk Analyst, join Nudge Security's existing Vendor Risk Analyst agent. These agents help security teams identify and prioritize remediation of potential threats, such as unvetted OAuth grants and malicious browser extensions. By automating the detection and remediation process, Nudge Security's agents enable teams to respond at "machine speed" and keep up with the pace of modern work. This is particularly important, as manual reviews can be time-consuming and may not be able to keep up with the rapidly evolving attack surfaces.

Nudge Security's approach combines multiple discovery signals across various surfaces, including the browser, inbox, identity provider, and connected apps. This provides a comprehensive view of potential risks and enables the agents to make higher-confidence recommendations. By delivering targeted, policy-driven remediation guidance, Nudge Security's agents help reduce noise and increase throughput for overburdened IT and security teams. According to Jaime Blasco, CTO at Nudge Security, these new agents help teams "immediately surface what's hidden, prioritize what matters, and take action" to protect corporate data from potential threats.
