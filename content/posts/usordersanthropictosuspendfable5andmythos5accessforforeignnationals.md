The US government has ordered Anthropic to suspend access to its most advanced AI models, Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5, for foreign nationals due to national security concerns. As a result, Anthropic will "abruptly disable" these models for all users, citing a misunderstanding and working to restore access as soon as possible. The company claims that the government's concerns are based on a narrow, non-universal jailbreak method that is not unique to its models and can be found in other publicly available models.

The unexpected move comes after the launch of Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5, which have strong cybersecurity capabilities. Anthropic has implemented safety classifiers to prevent the misuse of its models for cybersecurity-related tasks, including detecting potential jailbreak attempts. The company argues that its safeguards are substantially more effective than those of previously deployed models and that the perfect jailbreak resistance is not possible for any model provider. Anthropic also points out that the discovery of a narrow potential jailbreak should not be the reason for recalling a commercial model that is widely deployed.

The incident highlights the ongoing debate about the use of AI models and their potential risks. Anthropic has been at odds with the US government over the military use of its technology, with the Department of Defense labeling the company a "supply chain risk" earlier this year. Anthropic has filed lawsuits to block the designation and is advocating for a transparent and fair process for regulating AI deployments. The company's statement emphasizes the need for a statutory process that is grounded in technical facts, rather than relying on verbal evidence of potential jailbreaks.
