A cybersecurity breach has occurred at the US Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) after a contractor intentionally published sensitive information, including AWS GovCloud keys, on a public GitHub account. The leak, which was reported by KrebsOnSecurity, has raised concerns among lawmakers, with Senators and Representatives demanding answers from CISA.

The breach is believed to have occurred in November 2025, and the exposed information includes plaintext credentials to dozens of internal CISA systems. Experts have warned that the leak could provide a roadmap for adversaries to gain access to federal networks.

CISA has acknowledged the breach but claims that there is no indication that any sensitive data was compromised. However, lawmakers are skeptical, and Sen. Maggie Hassan (D-NH) has written to CISA's Acting Director, Nick Andersen, expressing concerns about the agency's internal policies and procedures.

Rep. Bennie Thompson (D-MS) has also written to Andersen, stating that the incident reflects a diminished security culture and/or an inability for CISA to adequately manage its contract support.

The breach is still being contained, and CISA is working to invalidate and replace the exposed keys and secrets. However, experts have warned that cybercrime groups or foreign adversaries may have already noticed the publication of the sensitive information and could be using it to gain access to federal networks.

The incident highlights the challenges of preventing insider threats and the importance of implementing robust security controls to prevent sensitive information from being leaked. Experts have noted that while technical controls can help prevent breaches, human error and intentional actions by employees or contractors can still pose a significant risk.

Key takeaways from the breach include:

* The importance of implementing robust security controls to prevent sensitive information from being leaked
* The need for agencies to have clear policies and procedures in place to prevent insider threats
* The challenges of preventing intentional actions by employees or contractors
* The importance of monitoring public code platforms, such as GitHub, for exposed keys and credentials
* The potential for cybercrime groups or foreign adversaries to exploit leaked sensitive information to gain access to federal networks.
