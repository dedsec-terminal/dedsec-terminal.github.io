ThreatModeler has introduced ThreatModeler Nexus, a platform that automates threat modeling with AI governance. This platform is designed to bring governed, architecture-aware security to modern software development, addressing the growing need for threat modeling as AI-generated code becomes more prevalent. ThreatModeler Nexus pairs a multi-agent system with a deterministic framework, allowing AI to accelerate the threat modeling process while governing the outcome.

The platform features a Secure Design Graph, a connected representation of components, threats, controls, and compliance mappings that serves as a system of record. This graph is the result of the merger between ThreatModeler and IriusRisk, combining over a decade of curated threat, control, and compliance intelligence. ThreatModeler Nexus operates on this graph, using agents to build system maps, ground the work in each customer's environment, and produce audit-ready evidence. This approach enables the platform to provide a comprehensive and governed framework for threat modeling.

ThreatModeler Nexus is designed to support enterprises and federal agencies in their threat modeling efforts. The company is working with Knox Systems to achieve FedRAMP authorization, which will enable federal agencies to adopt the platform while maintaining audibility and control. Industry research highlights the need for threat modeling, with AI-generated code requiring threat modeling before, during, or after code is written. ThreatModeler Nexus is positioned to address this need, with a platform that can scale to support large enterprises and provide a governed framework for threat modeling.
