The Dutch National Police have arrested a 35-year-old man from the municipality of Buren on suspicion of hacking into the computer systems of AFC Ajax, a football club from Amsterdam. The arrest was made on May 26, following an investigation that began after the club discovered unauthorized access to its systems earlier this year. The police seized various data carriers during the search for further investigation.

The hack exposed vulnerabilities in the club's app and website, including exposed APIs and shared access keys, which put the private data of over 300,000 registered Ajax fans at risk. The vulnerabilities could have also allowed attackers to steal or disable more than 42,000 season tickets, as well as modify stadium bans. The suspected hacker had initially contacted a journalist from RTL and shared details about the vulnerabilities, which were then demonstrated to show the potential impact of the attack.

AFC Ajax has since taken steps to patch the vulnerabilities exploited in the attack and has reported the incident to the Dutch police and the country's data protection authority. The club disclosed the intrusion on March 25, 2026, and has taken measures to protect the data of its fans. The investigation is ongoing, and the police are continuing to examine the seized data carriers to determine the full extent of the hack and the suspect's involvement.
