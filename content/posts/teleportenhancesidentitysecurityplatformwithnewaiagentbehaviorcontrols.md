Teleport has enhanced its Identity Security platform with three new capabilities to ensure AI agent behavior remains within defined boundaries. The new features, Beams Session Summaries, Agentic Classifiers, and Risk Scoring, provide enterprises with a foundational harness for identifying and preventing agent misalignment as autonomous agents take on greater responsibility inside production infrastructure. This expansion follows Teleport's recent white paper, "From Zero Trust to Agent Trust," which argues that zero trust is necessary but insufficient to govern agents operating at scale.

The three new capabilities work in concert with Teleport's trusted runtime for agents, Beams, and its Identity Security platform to address agentic behavior. Beams Session Summaries provide a human-readable summary of an AI agent's actions, establishing a behavioral baseline for evaluating agent activity. Agentic Classifiers enable policy evaluation of humans, agents, or groups of agents against company-specific criteria, flagging behavior inconsistent with an agent's declared objective. Risk Scoring automatically summarizes and classifies sessions by risk level, mapping actions to the MITRE ATT&CK framework.

These capabilities lay the foundation for the agent trust principles, giving agents a cryptographic, continuously monitored identity, making collective and individual risk visible, and providing enterprises with the tooling to detect and respond to misalignment as it happens. According to Ben Arent, Director of Product at Teleport, the new capabilities provide an operational harness for agent trust, allowing enterprises to see what an agent did, classify whether that behavior is expected, and score the risk of what it might do next. This is particularly important as agents break the assumption of zero trust, which assumes actors are human, bounded, and verifiable at the point of access.
