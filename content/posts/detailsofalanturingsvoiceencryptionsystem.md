A significant cache of Alan Turing's wartime papers, known as the "Bayley papers," was auctioned in London in November 2023 for nearly half a million US dollars. The collection includes handwritten notes by Turing, detailing his top-secret "Delilah" project, a portable voice-encryption system developed from 1943 to 1945. The project was named after the biblical figure Delilah, known for deceiving men.

The Bayley papers also contain notes written by Bayley, who worked with Turing, in the form of notes taken during Turing's conversations. The papers were preserved by Bayley until his death in 2020, 66 years after Turing's passing. The discovery of these papers has shed new light on Turing's work on voice encryption during World War II. The Delilah system was a groundbreaking project that showcased Turing's innovative approach to cryptography.

It appears that the Bayley papers may have been acquired by King's College, Cambridge, as part of an effort to "save them for the nation." The college announced that it has acquired the Delilah papers, which are now part of their collection. This acquisition ensures that Turing's important work on voice encryption is preserved and made available for future research and study, providing a valuable insight into the history of cryptography and Turing's contributions to the field.
