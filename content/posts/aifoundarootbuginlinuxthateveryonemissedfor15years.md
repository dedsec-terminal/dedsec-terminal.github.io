Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

A significant vulnerability in the Linux kernel, known as GhostLock (CVE-2026-43499), was recently discovered by AI-powered bug-hunting tool VEGA. The use-after-free bug allows any logged-in user to gain root access on an unpatched machine and has been present in the Linux kernel for 15 years. The flaw was fixed in April, but patch availability is uneven, and defenders are advised to confirm the fixed package rather than assuming one is waiting.

In other security news, Flock's license plate cameras mistakenly flagged a reporter's loaned Range Rover as stolen due to a data-entry error, resulting in a dramatic police response. Meanwhile, consulting giant Accenture confirmed a security breach after a threat actor claimed to have stolen 35 GB of data, including source code and Azure access tokens. The breach is particularly concerning given Accenture's contract with ICE for cyber defense and intelligence support services.

The Pentagon has launched a paid apprenticeship program, Cyber RAP, to recruit amateur hackers and train them to guard the department's networks. However, the program has been criticized for its low pay and potential risks. Additionally, a provision in the Senate Armed Services Committee's FY2027 defense bill would allow the defense secretary to establish a pilot program for contractor-owned and operated cyber operations, which has raised concerns about contributing to global cyber instability. Critics argue that this approach could blur the lines between public and private sector cyber operations and create unintended consequences.
