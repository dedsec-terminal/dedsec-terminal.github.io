The FBI has seized 13 websites allegedly linked to a Chinese intelligence-gathering effort targeting current and former US government employees and military personnel with access to classified information. The websites, posing as consulting firms, advertised job opportunities on various online platforms, aiming to recruit individuals with government backgrounds and security clearances. The operation is believed to have started in November 2023, with recruiters using fake personas, stolen identities, and encrypted messaging apps to reach potential candidates.

The recruiters offered large payments for research reports and pressured recruits to provide information from insider sources, using contracts and confidentiality agreements to conceal their true intentions. Funds were transferred from overseas into the US, and cryptocurrency was used to hide the source of payments. The FBI alleges that the operation involved bribery, identity theft, and international money laundering. The individuals behind the operation denied any involvement with a foreign government, despite evidence suggesting ties to the Chinese government.

Following the seizures, the FBI placed takeover notices on the domains, warning visitors that the sites had been disabled as part of an ongoing investigation. The FBI is urging individuals to remain vigilant and report suspicious job opportunities or recruitment efforts. Special Agent in Charge Dominique Evans of the FBI's Norfolk Field Office emphasized the importance of recognizing warning signs and reporting suspicious activity to the authorities. The seizure of these websites highlights the ongoing efforts of federal authorities to counter alleged Chinese intelligence-gathering operations targeting US government employees and sensitive information.
