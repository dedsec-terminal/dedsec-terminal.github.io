Google has filed a lawsuit against Outsider Enterprise, a China-based cybercrime network, for using AI tools, including Gemini, to build phishing websites and scam infrastructure. The operation has affected hundreds of thousands of victims, with estimated losses in the millions of dollars, and is linked to over 9,000 fake websites and 1 million fraudulent URLs. The scammers used AI to make their fraud more convincing and harder to detect, according to the FBI.

The cybercrime network operated through Telegram, distributing phishing kits used in fake package delivery alerts, banking notifications, and account security warnings. These messages directed victims to websites designed to collect credentials and payment information. The group also supplied phishing infrastructure to other criminals, supporting campaigns that impersonated technology companies, mobile carriers, financial institutions, and package delivery services. During a two-week period in May, Android users flagged 55,000 spam text messages tied to the operation, and Google connected 2.5 million messages to websites generated through Outsider Enterprise infrastructure.

Google is taking a multi-faceted approach to combat the scam, including filing a lawsuit to dismantle the infrastructure, coordinating with the FBI for law enforcement actions, and working with mobile carriers to block suspicious texts. The company is also advocating for federal legislation to make these protections permanent. Additionally, Google has introduced new AI-powered scam detection features for Android, designed to identify suspicious calls and text conversations, and intercepts over 10 billion malicious messages each month. This is Google's first lawsuit involving abuse of its Gemini AI tools, and the company is committed to protecting its users from such scams.
