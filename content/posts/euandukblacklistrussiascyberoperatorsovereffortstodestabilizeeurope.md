The European Union (EU) and the United Kingdom (UK) have jointly imposed sanctions on dozens of Russian individuals and entities accused of coordinating a malicious cyber ecosystem targeting Europe. The EU sanctioned nine individuals and four entities, while the UK sanctioned 24 individuals and entities. The sanctions aim to condemn Russia's behavior and misuse of its cyber ecosystem, which has targeted public services and critical infrastructure, causing disruptions and financial losses.

The EU attributed multiple cyber espionage and sabotage campaigns to the 16th Centre of Russia's Federal Security Service (FSB), accusing the unit of directing threat groups and targeting government networks and critical infrastructure across Europe. Recent attacks include a failed operation to disrupt electricity generation and heat supply in Poland using previously unseen data-wiping malware. The 16th Centre has also targeted government networks and critical infrastructure in several European countries, including France, Germany, and Finland.

The UK sanctions also target individuals linked to Lumma Stealer, an information-stealing malware used to steal credentials and sensitive data from compromised devices. The UK government estimates that at least 2,100 UK victims have been affected by Lumma Stealer over the past six months. The latest sanctions are part of a larger effort to counter Russia's cyber threats, with the EU and UK working together to impose restrictive measures on those responsible for malicious cyber activities. The sanctions follow another round of EU cyber sanctions imposed in March, and the National Security Agency (NSA) has also released guidance on protecting routers against cyber activity attributed to Russia's FSB Center 16.
