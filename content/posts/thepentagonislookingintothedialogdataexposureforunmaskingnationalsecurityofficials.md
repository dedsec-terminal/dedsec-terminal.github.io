The Pentagon is investigating a data exposure at Dialog, a private events group co-founded by Peter Thiel, which has compromised the personal information of multiple US national security personnel. The exposed data includes details of an intelligence official on the National Security Council (NSC) and an active-duty intelligence officer supporting sensitive military operations. This exposure poses significant operational risks, as foreign intelligence services often seek to identify and surveil US operatives.

The data exposure, which appears to have been caused by a misconfigured website, includes the private information and login tokens of 222 Dialog event registrants, including current and former senior military and national security officials. The exposed data includes sensitive details such as date of birth, home address, mobile number, and private authentication tokens. Dialog has characterized the incident as a "cyberattack," but evidence suggests that the files were exposed due to a misconfiguration in the group's website, allowing anyone to access the files by creating an account and loading a landing page.

The Pentagon's operations security team is examining the matter, and the White House has declined to comment on the exposure. The incident has raised concerns about the potential risks to national security personnel and the need for enhanced protections for their personal information. The exposed data also includes information on spouses and family members listed as emergency contacts, as well as personal thoughts and predictions from the registrants on topics such as future espionage and political violence. The incident highlights the importance of robust cybersecurity measures to protect sensitive information and prevent similar exposures in the future.
