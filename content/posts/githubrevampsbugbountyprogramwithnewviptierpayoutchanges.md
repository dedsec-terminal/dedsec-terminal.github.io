GitHub is revamping its bug bounty program to prioritize high-quality vulnerability reports and reduce low-effort submissions. The changes, which take effect on July 27, 2026, aim to address the increasing number of submissions that lack real security impact, such as reports without proof of concept or theoretical attack scenarios. This move is intended to improve the overall effectiveness of the program and reward researchers who provide valuable and actionable findings.

As part of the revamp, GitHub is introducing a new VIP tier for researchers who consistently deliver high-quality, high-impact findings. The VIP program is invite-only and offers higher payouts, faster response times, and closer collaboration with GitHub's engineering team. To qualify for the VIP program, researchers must meet specific thresholds, such as discovering one critical finding or multiple high-severity findings. The company is also restructuring its public bug bounty payouts to simplify expectations for researchers and allow for discretionary bonuses for exceptional submissions.

The changes also include the introduction of HackerOne's signal requirement, which aims to reduce low-effort and AI-generated reports. New researchers can make up to four initial submissions while establishing their signal in the public program. Those who do not meet the signal threshold will have limited submissions until they establish a track record. This requirement is designed to keep the program accessible to new researchers while maintaining the quality of submissions. The updated program is expected to provide more tailored attention and higher rewards to top researchers while still encouraging new participants to join and contribute to the program.
