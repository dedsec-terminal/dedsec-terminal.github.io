Security researchers at Norton have discovered a spear-phishing scam targeting travelers, where scammers use real hotel reservation information to trick victims into handing over sensitive details such as credit card information. The scammers have obtained booking details from at least 350 hotels, vacation rentals, and guesthouses in 50 countries, and are using this information to create highly targeted phishing messages. These messages include legitimate booking information, such as hotel names, check-in and check-out dates, and prices, making them more convincing and increasing the likelihood of victims clicking on fraudulent links.

The scam, known as "reservation hijacking," involves cybercriminals using stolen booking information to create fake websites and emails that appear to be from the hotel or a booking website. The messages often ask victims to click on a link to confirm their reservation or provide payment details, which are then stolen by the scammers. The researchers believe that the scammers may have obtained the booking information through various means, including hacking into hotel systems, third-party booking services, or by sending phishing messages to hotel staff. The use of real reservation context makes the phishing messages more effective, as victims are more likely to trust messages that include their actual booking details.

To protect themselves, travelers are advised to be cautious when receiving messages that appear to be from their hotel or booking website, even if they include real booking information. If in doubt, victims should contact the hotel or vacation rental directly through another means of contact to verify the authenticity of the message. The hospitality industry is also being urged to improve its security practices, such as implementing multifactor authentication for staff members and tightening controls on how guest data can be accessed and exported. By being aware of these scams and taking precautions, travelers can reduce the risk of falling victim to reservation hijacking and other types of phishing attacks.
