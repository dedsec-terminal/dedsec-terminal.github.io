Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the threat landscape for industrial automation systems in Q1 2026:

The percentage of ICS computers on which malicious objects were blocked decreased to 19.6% in Q1 2026, the lowest value in three years. Regionally, the percentages ranged from 9.1% in Northern Europe to 27.4% in Africa. However, the percentage of ICS computers on which malicious objects were blocked increased in five regions, including Southern Europe, Northern Europe, and Russia. Southern Europe led the way in growth for internet and email threats, as well as spyware, malicious scripts, and phishing pages.

The threat categories with the greatest increases were denylisted internet resources and spyware. Malicious scripts and phishing pages remained the top threat category, with a global average of 6.56%. Spyware decreased to 3.73%, but remained the second-most common threat category. The percentage of ICS computers on which ransomware was blocked decreased to 0.14%, the lowest value among all categories. Biometric systems were the most affected industry, with 26.4% of ICS computers on which malicious objects were blocked, and the highest percentage of email threats.

The main threat sources showed a decrease in average percentages, except for threats from the internet, which increased to 7.88%. The percentage of ICS computers on which malicious objects were blocked increased in various industries, including manufacturing, biometric systems, and building automation. Regionally, the highest percentages for certain threat categories were recorded in Southeast Asia, Africa, and Russia. The construction industry in Southeast Asia had the highest percentage of viruses, while biometric systems in Russia had the highest percentage of web miners. Overall, the threat landscape for industrial automation systems remains complex and dynamic, with various regions and industries facing unique challenges.
