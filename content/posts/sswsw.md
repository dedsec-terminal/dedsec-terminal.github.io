A recent cyberattack on the digital learning platform Canvas has caused widespread disruption to thousands of schools across the United States. The attack, which was carried out by a group of hackers known as ShinyHunters, resulted in a data breach and an extortion attempt against Canvas's maker, Instructure. The breach is believed to have affected over 8,800 schools, with universities such as Harvard, Columbia, and Georgetown being impacted. The hackers have been advertising the breach and attempting to extract a ransom payment from Instructure since May 1.

The situation took a turn for the worse on Thursday when Canvas was put into "maintenance mode" due to the attack, causing chaos for students and faculty in the midst of finals and end-of-year assignments. The hackers also launched a secondary wave of attacks, defacing some schools' Canvas portals and displaying a message that included a list of schools that were impacted by the breach. The message urged schools to consult with a cyber advisory firm and contact the group privately to negotiate a settlement before the end of the day on May 12, or risk having their data leaked.

The attack has highlighted the growing problem of data extortion and ransomware attacks in the education sector. The ShinyHunters group has been linked to several high-profile breaches in the past, and their tactics have become increasingly aggressive. According to cybersecurity experts, the group's activity appears to be related to recent activity from a group of hackers known as ScatteredLapsus$Hunters. The attack on Canvas is a significant escalation from this particular ransomware gang, and it has raised concerns about the need for governments to cooperate to stop cybercrime.

The incident has also raised questions about the effectiveness of Instructure's security measures and its response to the breach. The company has not commented on the specifics of the attack, but it has said that it is working to resolve the issue and prevent similar breaches in the future. The attack has also highlighted the need for schools and universities to have robust cybersecurity measures in place to protect against these types of attacks. As the use of digital learning platforms continues to grow, the risk of cyberattacks will only increase, making it essential for educational institutions to prioritize cybersecurity and protect sensitive student data.
