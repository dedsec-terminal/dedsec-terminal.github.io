Progress Software has disabled access to ShareFile accounts using Storage Zone Controllers (SZC) due to a "credible external security threat". The company has urged customers to shut down the servers hosting their SZC to ensure the safety of their data. This precautionary measure was taken after the threat was identified, and Progress Software is working to investigate and resolve the issue.

The initial email alert was sent to customers on July 10, and the company has promised to provide regular updates on the situation. A Knowledge Base article published on July 11 stated that there is no indication of unauthorized access to any Progress ShareFile accounts or data. However, customers are still required to keep their Storage Zone Controllers disabled for the time being. Progress Software is gradually restoring access to the affected ShareFile accounts.

The cause of the disruption is still unknown, but theories suggest that attackers may have exploited two vulnerabilities (CVE-2026-2699 and CVE-2026-2701) to achieve pre-authentication remote code execution on unpatched on-premises SZC deployments. Progress Software is working with internal and external cybersecurity experts to assess the potential threat and provide a solution. The company is expected to provide further updates on the situation, and customers are advised to keep their servers disabled until the issue is fully resolved.
