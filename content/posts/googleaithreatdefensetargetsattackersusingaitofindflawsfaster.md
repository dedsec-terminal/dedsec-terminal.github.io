Google Cloud has introduced AI Threat Defense, a cybersecurity platform that leverages artificial intelligence to identify and patch software vulnerabilities at machine speed. The platform combines several of Google's security assets, including the Gemini family of models, Wiz, CodeMender, and Mandiant, to provide a comprehensive security solution. This move is in response to attackers who use AI to discover and exploit flaws in hours or days, compressing the window for enterprises to respond.

The AI Threat Defense platform operates across a four-stage framework: Prepare, Scan and Prioritize, Remediate, and Monitor. In the Prepare stage, the platform maps exposed applications and infrastructure, while the scanning stage uses multiple AI models to identify vulnerabilities. Once a vulnerability is identified, the platform provides guidance on response and remediation, including generating fixes inside a developer's integrated development environment. The remediation stage centers on CodeMender, a Google DeepMind agent that generates patches and coordinates with Wiz and Antigravity to replace vulnerable code.

The introduction of AI Threat Defense is part of Google's broader security efforts, which include zero trust architecture, the Titan security chip, and Google Security Operations. The company is positioning AI Threat Defense as a response to the increasing use of AI by attackers to automate reconnaissance and exploitation. By combining vulnerability discovery with prioritized, automatically generated patches, Google aims to provide enterprises with a more effective way to manage risk and stay ahead of threats. The platform enters a market where most security vendors are layering AI features onto existing tools, and Google's pitch centers on its ability to provide a comprehensive and automated security solution.
