Cybersecurity leaders face the challenge of translating cyber risk into financial terms that boards and executives can understand. Instead of using technical jargon and counting Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs), they need to express cyber exposure in dollars. This is crucial for making informed business decisions and allocating resources effectively.

To achieve this, Ziv Levi, SVP of Technology at CYE, proposes a three-step financial translation framework. The first step involves identifying business exposure by mapping attack paths to critical assets such as intellectual property and customer data. The second step focuses on exploitability, assessing the skills required for an attack and the potential business impact. The third step quantifies potential damage in dollars, using data from ransomware payouts, outages, fines, and breach settlements.

The importance of translating cyber risk into financial terms is heightened by the increasing speed of vulnerability exploitation, driven by AI. The time between vulnerability discovery and exploitation is now measured in minutes, rather than weeks. Companies that can understand their exposure, quantify it precisely, and make decisions quickly are more likely to succeed in cybersecurity. By adopting this approach, organizations can stay ahead of attackers and make informed decisions to protect their assets and minimize financial losses.
