IBM and Red Hat have announced Project Lightwell, a $5 billion commitment to help enterprises secure open source software. The project aims to establish a trusted enterprise clearinghouse and a global force of over 20,000 engineers to identify and fix vulnerabilities at scale. This will provide a new model for enterprise use of open source software, from development to production environments, using advanced AI capabilities to validate and test fixes.

The need for Project Lightwell is driven by the fact that open source software underpins modern enterprise infrastructure, with over 90% of Fortune 500 companies relying on it. However, advances in AI are accelerating vulnerability discovery and exploitation, making it essential to have a robust security system in place. IBM and Red Hat have already begun collaborating with several major companies, including Bank of America and Visa, to test and refine the project. The goal is to utilize new AI-powered security methods to protect the foundational open source layers that underpin modern enterprise and AI systems.

Project Lightwell builds on IBM and Red Hat's leadership in open source, enterprise AI, and security, and incorporates learnings from other initiatives. The project will provide a trusted open source security clearinghouse, allowing enterprises to report and resolve vulnerabilities, deploy validated patches, and coordinate upstream disclosures. This approach will enable enterprises to engage IBM and Red Hat to resolve critical security issues while strengthening open source overall through responsible upstream disclosure. According to Arvind Krishna, Chairman and CEO of IBM, Project Lightwell is about defining a new industry model that brings together AI, engineering expertise, and trusted collaboration to secure open source software at its source and across the entire supply chain.
