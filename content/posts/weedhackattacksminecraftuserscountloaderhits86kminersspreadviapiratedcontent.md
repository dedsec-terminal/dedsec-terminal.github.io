**Weedhack Malware Targets Minecraft Players**
A new malware campaign, codenamed Weedhack, has been targeting Minecraft players via YouTube to spread malware capable of gaining control of victims' systems. The campaign, active since January 2026, impersonates Minecraft clients and mods to infect users, with 3820 unique malicious JAR files and over 240 URLs identified. The malware allows criminals to create custom payloads, inject malware into legitimate Minecraft mods, and steal credentials and system information.

**CountLoader and Cryptocurrency Miners**
In a separate campaign, CountLoader, a JavaScript loader, has compromised an estimated 86,000 unique machines, with approximately 9,000 infections resulting from the malware spreading via USB drives and removable media. The malware deploys various payloads, including a cryptocurrency clipper that hijacks clipboard content to redirect cryptocurrency transactions. Additionally, a years-long campaign has used illegal movie and TV show streaming sites to distribute a cryptocurrency miner, which uses DLL side-loading to drop a fork of SilentCryptoMiner and provides remote control capabilities.

**Cybersecurity Risks and Consequences**
The Weedhack and CountLoader campaigns highlight the risks of cybersecurity threats, particularly for younger audiences who may be attracted to free or pirated content. The malware can lead to cyberbullying, with customers using remote access capabilities to threaten, harass, and monitor victims. The campaigns also demonstrate the importance of being cautious when downloading software or clicking on links, as well as the need for robust cybersecurity measures to prevent the spread of malware and protect sensitive information.
