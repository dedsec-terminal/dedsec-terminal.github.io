Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the European Commission's order to Google:

The European Commission has ordered Google to open up its Android operating system to rival AI assistants, giving them access to the camera, microphone, and screen, as well as the ability to drive other apps in the background. This order is part of the Digital Markets Act and must be implemented in the next major release of Android, Android 18, by August 1, 2027. The Commission has identified 11 operating system features, of which five are considered "restricted features" that require certification before an app can access them.

The five restricted features include centralized access to on-device data, context-aware intelligence, structured on-device integration, screen automation, and system integration. Google must set up a Qualified AI Assistant Programme to certify third-party assistants, which will be free of charge and accepted without conditions. The programme will have a set of terms and conditions that must be reasonable and non-discriminatory, and Google must clear any changes with the Commission two months in advance. The other six features, including ambient data, always-on hotword detection, and background execution, will be open to all third-party apps without certification requirements.

The Commission has also ordered Google to provide anonymized search query, click, and ranking data to rival search engines and AI chatbots for a cost-based fee. The data will be anonymized using a three-pass method, and recipients will be required to have at least 50,000 monthly average EU users and meet certain eligibility criteria. Google has objected to the order, citing concerns about device security and the potential for data breaches. However, the Commission has stated that the measures are necessary to promote competition and innovation in the digital market, and Google must comply with the order by the specified deadlines.
