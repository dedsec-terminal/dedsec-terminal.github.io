Here is a summary of the article in three concise paragraphs:

Within hours of the theatrical debut of Christopher Nolan's "The Odyssey", scammers began targeting people searching for pirated copies of the film. According to Malwarebytes, researchers found two separate scams running on cloned piracy sites. The first scam involved fake browser warnings that pushed visitors into ad networks, while the second scam involved Windows executables disguised as movie downloads.

The fake browser warnings appeared as pop-ups claiming a missing component was blocking access to the site, with a "Fix It Now" button that sent visitors through a malvertising network. This could lead to fake browser extensions, scareware, or additional malware redirects. The second scam involved a downloadable file named "The Odyssey 2026 1080p WEBRip-LAMA.exe" that was actually a program, not a video. The file was designed to look like a legitimate movie download, but was actually a trojan, infostealer, or loader for additional malware.

Neither scam used a software vulnerability, instead relying on social engineering to convince users to take a malicious action. This makes the scams harder to prevent through security software alone. Malwarebytes warns that if a movie download asks you to install something, fix your browser, or run an .exe, it's likely a scam. The high-profile release of "The Odyssey" and its large budget may have contributed to the swift appearance of these scams, highlighting the importance of being cautious when searching for pirated content online.
