Financial crime investigators in the Netherlands have arrested two men and seized 800 servers linked to a web hosting company that enabled cyberattacks, interference operations, and disinformation campaigns. The company, Stark Industries, was founded in February 2022, shortly before Russia's invasion of Ukraine, and was added to the EU's list of sanctioned entities last year. The investigation found that the company provided support to actions by the Russian Federation that undermine democracy and security, including through information manipulation and disruption of public and economic systems.

The web hosting infrastructure was transferred to a newly created Dutch company, believed to be WorkTitans B.V., which acted as a front for the sanctioned entities. WorkTitans provided hosting services under the brand THE.Hosting and was linked to attacks by the pro-Russian hacktivist group NoName057(16), which has targeted key organizations with distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks. The company's infrastructure was supported by Mirhosting, which provided physical servers, colocation, and high-capacity connectivity to major internet exchanges in Amsterdam and Frankfurt.

The seizure of 800 servers, laptops, phones, and administrative records was the result of multiple raids conducted by the FIOD in data centers in Dronten and Schiphol-Rijk, as well as searches in Enschede and Almere. The investigation found that the suspects indirectly provided economic resources to Russian and Belarusian entities sanctioned by the EU. Mirhosting denied knowingly supporting illegal operations, claiming they quickly intervened upon receipt of abuse complaints, while WorkTitans did not respond to requests for a statement.

The action taken by the FIOD is a significant step in disrupting the activities of malicious actors who use web hosting companies to enable cyberattacks and disinformation campaigns. The investigation highlights the importance of monitoring and regulating web hosting companies to prevent them from being used for malicious purposes. The case also underscores the need for international cooperation in combating cybercrime and disrupting the activities of sanctioned entities.
