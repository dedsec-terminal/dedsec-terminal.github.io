Chinese-language phishing-as-a-service (PhaaS) communities are expanding and becoming a significant force in the cybercrime landscape, traditionally dominated by Russian-speaking groups. According to the Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG), these communities offer mature services, with some linked to broader criminal activity in the region. The phishing services primarily target non-Chinese entities, with nearly all legitimate organizations mimicked being from outside China.

The GTIG analysis revealed a shift in phishing tactics, with attackers moving from static password harvesting to real-time interception and tokenization. They use live administration panels to interact with victims during phishing activity, capturing one-time passcodes and bypassing multi-factor authentication (MFA) protections. Additionally, AI tools are being adopted by PhaaS operators to increase scale and reduce reliance on static infrastructure. This enables the creation of unique phishing pages that can evade signature-based detection methods.

The phishing services are becoming increasingly localized, with operators tapping into daily consumer habits and targeting users in various countries, including Japan. The YY Lai Yu platform, for example, offers over 400 phishing templates covering Japanese brands and services, and has extended its campaigns to include themes tied to loyalty points, rewards programs, and electricity subsidy offers. The use of automated infrastructure and AI-powered tools has made these phishing operations more sophisticated, allowing threat actors with limited technical skills to conduct phishing operations and enabling the China-based criminal ecosystem to continue evolving.
