A recent video posted online demonstrated a method for hacking into Instagram accounts using Meta's AI support chatbot. The hacker used a VPN to disguise their location and then interacted with the chatbot to add a new email address to the target's account. The chatbot sent a verification code to the provided email address, which the hacker then used to reset the account password and gain access.

The issue was reportedly fixed by Instagram on Monday, according to spokesperson Andy Stone. However, the incident highlights the limitations and potential vulnerabilities of using large language model (LLM) chatbots for sensitive applications like account support. The problem is not just with this specific tactic, but with the inherent trustworthiness of LLM chatbots, which can be exploited in various ways.

The incident has raised concerns about the security and privacy of online accounts, particularly with the knowledge that every keystroke is being recorded. A commenter, "First post kitty," expressed sarcasm and concern about the recording of keystrokes, implying that it is delightful but actually highlighting the potential risks and invasions of privacy. The incident serves as a reminder of the ongoing challenges in balancing convenience and security in online interactions.
