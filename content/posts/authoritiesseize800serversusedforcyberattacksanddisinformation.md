Dutch authorities have seized 800 servers linked to a hosting provider that allegedly supported Russian activities aimed at undermining democracy and security. The servers were used for cyberattacks, disinformation, and disruption of public and economic systems, primarily targeting the European Union. The investigation began with a web hosting company established in February 2022, just before Russia's invasion of Ukraine.

The web hosting company was placed on the European Union's sanctions list in May 2025, after it was found to be involved in destabilizing activities against the EU. However, a significant part of its technical infrastructure was transferred to a newly established Dutch company, which is believed to have served as a front for sanctioned entities. Two men, aged 57 and 39, were arrested on suspicion of violating Dutch sanctions laws and making economic resources available to entities under EU sanctions.

The arrests and seizures were the result of a search of three business locations and two data centers in the Netherlands, where authorities found administrative records, laptops, phones, and other equipment. The 57-year-old suspect is believed to have been the director and indirect sole shareholder of the newly established company, while the 39-year-old suspect was the director and sole shareholder of a second company that provided internet connectivity for the servers. The investigation highlights the ongoing efforts to combat cyberattacks and disinformation campaigns supported by Russian entities.
