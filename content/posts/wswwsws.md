The most significant security risk to organizations is no longer malware, but rather the abuse of legitimate tools and utilities that are already trusted and present within the environment. This is often referred to as "living off the land" and is a tactic used by modern threat actors. According to Bitdefender's analysis of 700,000 high-severity incidents, legitimate-tool abuse was found in 84% of them. The use of trusted utilities such as PowerShell, WMIC, and netsh can be exploited by attackers to carry out malicious activities, making it a major concern for organizations.

The issue is further complicated by the fact that a clean Windows 11 install comes with 133 unique living-off-the-land binaries, and PowerShell is active on 73% of endpoints, often invoked silently by third-party applications. This over-entitlement problem cannot be solved by patching alone, and organizations need to take a proactive approach to reduce their attack surface. Gartner predicts that preemptive cybersecurity will account for 50% of IT security spending by 2030, and the adoption of dynamic attack surface reduction technologies will become more widespread.

To address this issue, Bitdefender offers a complimentary Internal Attack Surface Assessment, a 45-day engagement that provides organizations with a prioritized list of users, endpoints, and tools that can be safely taken away from attackers without disrupting the business. The assessment uses Bitdefender's GravityZone PHASR technology to build behavioral profiles, identify potential risks, and provide a quantifiable exposure score. Early-access customers have reported a reduction in their attack surface by 30% or more in the first 30 days, without investigation overhead or end-user disruption.

The assessment provides benefits for different stakeholders, including CISOs, SOC and IT admins, and business decision-makers. It provides a defensible, board-ready exposure number, reduces investigation and response workload, and documents ongoing surface reduction. By taking a proactive approach to reducing the attack surface, organizations can minimize the risk of breaches and improve their overall cybersecurity posture. The Internal Attack Surface Assessment is available to organizations with 250 or more employees and can be requested at no cost, without changing the existing security stack.

By starting where the attackers already are, organizations can gain a precise and prioritized map of their risks within 45 days. This approach allows organizations to shorten the list of potential vulnerabilities that an attacker can exploit once they are inside the environment.
