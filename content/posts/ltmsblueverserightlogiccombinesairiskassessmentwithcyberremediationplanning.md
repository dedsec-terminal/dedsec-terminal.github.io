LTM has introduced BlueVerse RightLogic, a cybersecurity framework that combines AI risk assessment with cyber remediation planning. This solution aims to help enterprises identify, assess, and remediate cyber exposure as they adopt AI technologies. With AI now capable of autonomously exploiting vulnerabilities, cyber risk has become a board-level priority, and enterprises are struggling to keep up with emerging threats.

BlueVerse RightLogic addresses this challenge by providing a unified view of enterprise exposure and enabling continuous, evidence-led risk management. The framework combines an AI and cyber exposure assessment engine with a structured execution model that spans from assessment to remediation. It assesses various aspects of cybersecurity, including software supply chain risk, legacy systems, network exposure, and AI-specific risks. This comprehensive approach allows organizations to quantify exposure, prioritize action, and build a defensible security posture.

The BlueVerse RightLogic framework is delivered through a 4-6 week engagement, providing diagnostics, domain assessments, and a board-ready risk summary, followed by a prioritized remediation roadmap. The model extends into execution through a partner-led ecosystem that drives fixes across infrastructure, applications, and open-source dependencies. This enables faster response cycles, improved risk visibility, and a governed pathway for secure AI adoption at scale. According to Krishnan Iyer, Chief Growth Officer at LTM, BlueVerse RightLogic enables organizations to adopt AI safely and securely by providing a fundamentally different approach to cybersecurity.
