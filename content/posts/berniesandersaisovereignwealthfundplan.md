Bernie Sanders has proposed a plan to create a US sovereign wealth fund by taking 50% stock in AI companies, aiming to establish democratic control over these companies and return a portion of their economic rewards to the public. The goal is to prevent a handful of billionaires from determining the future of humanity with their AI developments. Sanders' plan is motivated by the concern that the concentration of power and wealth among tech oligarchs poses a significant risk to global democracy.

However, the authors of the article disagree with Sanders' approach, arguing that public ownership of AI companies can entangle corporate profit with the public interest, leading to unintended consequences. They suggest that this could incentivize the government to prioritize corporate interests over the public good, and that the Norwegian sovereign wealth fund's ownership of oil companies has not led to pro-environmental policies. Instead, the authors propose alternative solutions, such as taxation and the creation of a public AI option, which would allow governments to develop and operate their own AI models under democratic control.

The authors propose an AI token tax or an excise tax on data centers' energy use as a way to share private rewards with the broader society. They also suggest establishing publicly developed and operated AI models, like the Swiss large language model Apertus, which can provide a competitive baseline for private AI offerings and promote transparency, sustainability, and compliance with regulations. The authors urge Sanders and other leaders to consider these alternatives, which they believe have a greater chance of influencing corporate behavior in the public interest.
