An unknown threat actor has been observed using various tactics to promote a cryptocurrency clipboard hijacker, including paid posts on legitimate news websites, fake reviews, and AI-generated narrators. The threat actor has a dedicated WordPress phishing page, GitHub and SourceForge projects, a YouTube channel, and coordinated activity on VirusTotal to misclassify malicious files as safe. The goal is to target cryptocurrency asset holders and online gamblers with a Rust-based clipper that substitutes wallet addresses with attacker-controlled ones.

The threat actor is using a range of techniques to build a fake reputation economy, including inflated download counts, coordinated five-star reviews, and promotion on trusted platforms. They have also used Ghost Networks to poison reputation-driven systems like VirusTotal, aiming to reduce suspicion and increase trust in the malicious files. On GitHub, the threat actor operates multiple accounts to cross-promote and distribute their malware, while on SourceForge, they have artificially inflated the download count using an Android farm.

The campaign's tactics are notable for their sophistication and use of legitimate platforms to build trust. The threat actor has even used a press release distribution service to market their tool's capabilities, resulting in syndication across partner news websites. Check Point Research warns that this playbook of fake reputation and aggressive cross-platform promotion can easily be used to distribute more severe threats, such as information stealers or ransomware, to higher-value targets in the future. The use of AI-generated narrators and coordinated activity across multiple platforms marks a significant shift in how attackers build trust and promote their malicious activities.
