Aikido Security has acquired Root, a company that provides backported fixes for open source vulnerabilities, in a bid to tackle the growing threat of supply chain attacks. The acquisition unites the two companies behind a shared mission to make it easy for developers to build secure open source applications. Open source is the foundation of almost every application, and it has become a primary entry point for attackers, with vulnerabilities often sitting unpatched in production for years.

The acquisition aims to address the challenges faced by organizations in securing their open source dependencies. Currently, companies have two options: upgrade to a newer version, which can introduce malware or break working code, or migrate to a vendor's locked-down replacement. Aikido Libraries, powered by Root's technology, allows companies to apply patches to vulnerabilities without introducing breaking changes. This approach enables companies to fix vulnerabilities in place, without having to upgrade or migrate to a new version.

The acquisition is part of Aikido's efforts to expand its offerings in the cybersecurity space. Aikido is announcing backported fixes for critical, actively exploited open source vulnerabilities to the community, returning patches to the projects that need them rather than keeping them behind a paywall. This move is seen as a significant step towards supporting open source maintainers, who are often overwhelmed with security work. The acquisition is also the latest in a string of acquisitions for Aikido, which has become the fastest-ever European cybersecurity company to reach unicorn status with a $1 billion valuation.
