Dutch authorities have dismantled a massive botnet that had infected at least 17 million devices, including computers, tablets, smartphones, and IoT devices. The botnet, which was linked to over 200 servers in the Netherlands, was used to carry out malicious attacks. The National Cyber Security Center (NCSC) and the Dutch police worked together to take down the botnet, seizing a subset of the servers from a hosting provider.

The botnet is believed to be linked to Asocks, a company that offers residential proxies, which can be used for legitimate purposes such as accessing geographically-restricted web resources. However, the ecosystem is also used by bad actors to purchase access to compromised devices and route malicious traffic. The NCSC warned that devices can become part of a botnet when they are accessible to malicious actors, who can install malware to control the device remotely.

To protect against botnet malware, users are advised to keep their operating systems up-to-date, maintain visibility of edge devices, use strong passwords, and enable two-factor authentication. Additionally, users should install apps from trusted sources, change default passwords, and secure Wi-Fi networks with WPA2 or WPA3. By taking these precautions, users can reduce the risk of their devices becoming part of a botnet and being used for cybercriminal activities.
