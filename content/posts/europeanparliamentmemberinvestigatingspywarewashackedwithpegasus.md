A former Member of the European Parliament, Stelios Kouloglou, was hacked with the Pegasus spyware while serving on a committee investigating the abuse of commercial surveillance tools in the EU. The hacking occurred in October 2022 and March 2023, with the attackers potentially gaining access to confidential documents and committee deliberations. The infections have not been attributed to a particular government, but the Citizen Lab researchers believe that a Pegasus customer with authorization to spy in multiple European countries is likely responsible.

The hacking of Kouloglou's device is significant, as he was a member of the European Parliament's Committee of Inquiry to investigate the use of Pegasus and equivalent surveillance spyware. The committee was set up to probe alleged misuses of commercial spyware offerings under EU law. The Citizen Lab's forensic analysis of Kouloglou's iPhone found that it was compromised with Pegasus spyware on two occasions, using a zero-click exploit in Apple's smart home software. The issue was addressed by Apple in iOS 16.3.1, but Kouloglou's device was running iOS 15.5 at the time of the hacking.

The incident raises concerns about the use of spyware by governments to spy on journalists, lawmakers, dissidents, and critics. The Citizen Lab's findings also highlight the need for greater transparency and accountability in the use of commercial surveillance tools. The report comes amid a series of revelations about the use of spyware and other surveillance tools by governments and commercial vendors. The Citizen Lab has previously uncovered cases of spyware being used to target journalists, activists, and opposition figures, and has raised concerns about the potential for abuse of these tools.
