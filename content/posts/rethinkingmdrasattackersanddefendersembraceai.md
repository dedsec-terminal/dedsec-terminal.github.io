The Managed Detection and Response (MDR) model has been a solution to the problem of security teams being unable to staff around the clock and handle the alert queue. However, the threat landscape has changed faster than the MDR model can adapt. Attackers are now using AI to move faster, generate more convincing phishing campaigns, and create malware variants that evade signature-based detection. As a result, MDR is no longer enough, and security leaders may need to consider whether they have outgrown their MDR. The traditional MDR model is struggling to keep up with the volume of alerts, with approximately 60% of alerts going unreviewed, which is where attackers often hide.

The investigation quality of MDR also varies by who is on shift, with the experience of the analyst, queue depth, and time of day affecting the quality of the investigation. This can lead to shallow investigations, threats being classified as noise, and early-stage lateral movement going undetected. Furthermore, detection engineering is not a closed loop, with MDR investigation and detection engineering operating in separate silos. This results in a detection posture that degrades faster than it improves. Additionally, MDR services are often a black box, with customers not being able to see the investigation logic, inspect the evidence trail, or audit what the analyst actually reviewed before closing a case.

The solution to these problems is an AI-powered Security Operations Center (SOC) that can execute investigations at speed and scale, with humans supervising and making decisions. An AI SOC can investigate 100% of alerts, including endpoint, identity, cloud, network, phishing, and SIEM, automatically and without filtering by severity. This approach has been shown to be effective, with Intezer's platform data showing that less than 2% of alerts required human escalation, and the over 98% that resolved autonomously did so with sub-minute median triage time and 98% verdict accuracy.
