Researchers are utilizing machine learning algorithms to decrypt historical ciphers from the medieval period. The DESCRYPT project aims to decode the estimated 1% of archival material that remains fully or partially encrypted. By leveraging online platforms and developing their own AI tools, researchers have made progress in revealing the messages hidden in these ciphers.

One notable example of the project's success is the use of the Transkribus platform, which hosts over 300 community AI models. This platform was used to decrypt a partially encrypted document, demonstrating the potential of AI in deciphering historical ciphers. The researchers have also developed their own AI tool to work through the complexities of their materials, showcasing the effectiveness of machine learning in this field.

The application of AI in decrypting historical ciphers has sparked interest in tackling other infamous encrypted texts, such as the Voynich manuscript. Experts like Clive Robinson note that codes are essentially about hiding or flattening the statistics of plaintext in a reversible way, and that AI's statistical capabilities make it well-suited for inverting these statistics to reveal the original text. While the task is still challenging, the use of AI and machine learning algorithms holds promise for uncovering the secrets hidden in these medieval ciphers.
