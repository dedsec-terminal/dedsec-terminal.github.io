Italy's data protection authority, the Garante per la Protezione dei Dati Personali, has fined WINDTRE, a major Italian telecom operator, €1.7 million for "serious data security shortcomings" that led to two data breaches. The breaches, which occurred in February 2025, resulted in the theft of personal data belonging to over 365,000 customers, including names, contact details, and payment information for 41,359 customers. The attackers used social engineering tactics, posing as support technicians to gain access to company systems.

The regulator's investigation found that WINDTRE had deficiencies in managing login credentials and digital certificates, and that its security audits had missed vulnerabilities that could have been caught with more thorough checks. The company argued that it had solid security measures in place, including three-factor authentication and firewalls, and that the incidents were due to human error. However, the regulator rejected this defense, pointing to two technical failures: inadequate certificate handling and insufficient API protection.

The regulator ruled that WINDTRE had broken GDPR rules on data integrity, confidentiality, and security, and ordered the company to strengthen its protection of login credentials and digital certificates, introduce secure password management tools, and improve its cybersecurity procedures. The €1.7 million fine was determined based on factors such as WINDTRE's prompt reporting of the breaches, its cooperation during the investigation, and its lack of prior privacy violations. The company must now take steps to prevent similar incidents and ensure the security of its customers' personal data.
