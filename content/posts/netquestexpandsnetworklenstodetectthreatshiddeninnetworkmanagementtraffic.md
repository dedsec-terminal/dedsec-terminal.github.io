NetQuest has expanded its NetworkLens enriched dataset portfolio to detect threats hidden in network management traffic. The new network telemetry datasets provide detailed traffic characteristics of network management transactions, giving security teams the intelligence needed to detect threats within critical network infrastructure. This expansion is aimed at maximizing detection effectiveness at hyperscale, particularly for AI-driven threat detection tools.

Legacy network management protocols, such as SNMP and TFTP, have become soft targets for threat actors due to their age and ubiquity. These protocols have vulnerabilities, including plaintext authentication, that enable credential theft, unauthorized device reconfiguration, and network reconnaissance. Additionally, they can be used for insider and supply-chain abuse, as well as configuration and firmware exposure. Despite these risks, these protocols have historically been under-monitored, creating a blind spot in network security.

The NetworkLens expansion addresses this blind spot by using deep packet inspection to discover targeted management protocols and correlate request-response pairs into bidirectional transaction records. This provides AI-ready telemetry to downstream security pipelines, enabling security teams to detect threats hidden within network management traffic. According to NetQuest CEO Jesse Price, the promise of AI-driven cyber threat detection can only be realized with access to rich, contextual network data, and NetworkLens is designed to close that gap and provide the necessary intelligence to detect threats effectively.
