Meta has introduced stricter guardrails for teen feeds on Instagram, Facebook, and Messenger, aiming to provide a safer online experience for young users. The company has expanded its Teen Accounts 13+ content settings globally, which hide inappropriate content and limit interactions with profiles, pages, groups, and events that share such content. This setting is enabled by default for teen accounts, and parents can opt for an even stricter "Limited Content" setting on Instagram, with plans to roll it out on Facebook and Messenger later this year.

The 13+ content setting has been evaluated by online safety experts at Alice (formerly ActiveFence), who performed stress-testing to assess its effectiveness. The assessment found that Instagram Teen Accounts using the default 13+ setting were exposed to 68% less mature content than teens on a competitor's platform. The stricter Limited Content setting resulted in 96% less mature content exposure. Additionally, Instagram blocked mature search terms more often than the competitor's platform, and the assessment confirmed that Instagram's safeguards are working as intended.

The evaluation also identified areas for improvement, which Meta has addressed. The company updated its detection systems to limit interactions with accounts that regularly share age-inappropriate content and restricted "car surfing" content, a newer trend that was not previously covered by its policies. Meta plans to continue exploring new ways to reduce repeated exposure to certain types of content and present a broader mix of content in Explore, Feed, and Reels. The company's efforts aim to provide a positive and age-appropriate experience for teen users on its platforms.
