Here are three concise paragraphs summarizing the article:

This week's ThreatsDay Bulletin highlights various cyber threats that have been discovered, including Android spyware, PLC attacks, and AI image prompt injection. Many of these threats are disguised as useful tools or apps, making them difficult to detect. For example, a package called @copilot-mcp/apex was found to be a postinstall dropper that installs a macOS infostealer, while a fake Microsoft Visual Studio Code extension called "Markdown All Pro" opens a backdoor on the user's machine.

Other threats include a phishing campaign targeting Portuguese users with the Lampion banking malware, and a malvertising scheme that displays intrusive ads on Android devices after a phone call is ended. Additionally, a new attack technique called GhostCommit has been discovered, which hides malicious instructions inside a PNG image that can steal a repository's secrets. Iranian-linked actors have also been targeting internet-connected operational technology devices, including PLCs, with the goal of disrupting critical infrastructure sectors.

The threats also include fake apps that enable surveillance, such as a Bahraini Civil Defense "BH Alert" siren app that embeds a malware called OctagonPanel to hoover sensitive data from a compromised device. Another threat actor, tracked as TAG-182, has been observed disseminating MarkiRAT malware in support of Iranian government surveillance operations. To stay ahead of these threats, it's essential to stay informed and take proactive measures to protect against them. The ThreatsDay Bulletin provides a comprehensive list of the latest threats, and subscribers can receive alerts when new bulletins are released.
