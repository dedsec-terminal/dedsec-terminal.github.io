The traditional approach to cybersecurity, which relies on patching vulnerabilities and assuming a secure network, is no longer effective. Zero-day exploits and AI-generated attacks have made it impossible to keep up with patching, and the assumption that critical systems are isolated behind firewalls or separate segments is often incorrect. This "segmentation illusion" can be shattered when an attacker maps the network, revealing hidden devices, connections, and pathways that can be exploited.

HD Moore, creator of Metasploit and CEO of runZero, will lead a webinar to show how attackers view a network, highlighting the differences between an organization's static inventory list and the dynamic map of connections and pathways that an attacker uses. The webinar will focus on finding unknown assets, identifying bridges that break segmentation, and mapping attack paths to prioritize remediation efforts. By understanding the network from an attacker's perspective, organizations can identify and fix the most critical vulnerabilities and reduce the risk of a breach.

The webinar promises to provide a new perspective on network security, moving beyond traditional inventory management and focusing on live attack-path mapping. Attendees will learn how to find unsanctioned IT, shadow IoT, and hidden assets, as well as identify the bridges that connect isolated zones. By seeing the network through the eyes of an attacker, organizations can take proactive steps to secure their environment, whether it's a corporate network, factory floor, or a combination of both. The goal is to fix the most critical vulnerabilities and shorten an attacker's route to impact, reducing the risk of a breach and improving overall cybersecurity.
