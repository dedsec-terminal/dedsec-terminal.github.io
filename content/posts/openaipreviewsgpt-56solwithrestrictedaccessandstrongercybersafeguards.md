OpenAI has released a limited preview of its GPT-5.6 models, including Sol, Terra, and Luna, to a small number of companies as part of its engagement with the US government. The models are designed to strike a balance between power, efficiency, and safety, with Sol being the most powerful and Luna being fine-tuned for speed and affordability. The GPT-5.6 models come with a robust safety stack, including strengthened protections for higher-risk activity, sensitive cyber requests, and repeated misuse.

The GPT-5.6 Sol model is touted as the "most capable model yet" for cybersecurity, making it suitable for vulnerability research and exploitation. It has been tested on ExploitBench and has shown competitive results with Anthropic's Mythos Preview using only about one-third of the output tokens. The goal of the model is to enable access to legitimate work such as code review, vulnerability research, and patch development, while enforcing strong guardrails to block offensive activity. OpenAI is working to ensure that the capabilities of the model reach and benefit defenders, who can use the tools to find weaknesses and strengthen systems.

The release of the GPT-5.6 models comes as part of a broader effort by OpenAI to work with the US government on AI and cybersecurity. The company has been testing the models with a small group of trusted partners and plans to make them generally available in the coming weeks. The release follows an executive order by US President Donald Trump calling for the creation of a framework to evaluate AI models' capabilities and determine which qualify as "covered frontier models." OpenAI's release is also part of a larger trend of AI companies working with the government to develop and deploy AI models for cybersecurity and other applications.
