ThreatDown has expanded its security capabilities to include AI visibility and machine identity security, addressing emerging risks in organizations. The company's AI visibility feature provides a full inventory of AI tools running across environments, while its ITDR capabilities have been extended to secure non-human identities (NHI) such as service accounts, API tokens, and machine identities. This helps close critical exposure points that lack oversight and can outnumber human users.

The expansion addresses two key issues: the lack of visibility into AI activity and the inability to govern machine identities. ThreatDown's platform provides both capabilities without requiring a separate console, agent, or additional dedicated security staff. According to recent surveys, 90% of professionals report using AI at work, but only 38% of organizations have a formal AI policy, leading to an exponential rise in non-human identities and a critical security gap. ThreatDown's General Manager, Kendra Krause, notes that "shadow AI" is a major blind spot for security teams, and most organizations have not started thinking about the identities behind AI activity.

ThreatDown's new capabilities include AI Detection and Response, which provides visibility into AI application use across the organization, and Identity Threat Detection and Response, which tracks non-human identities and reveals their ownership, age, and privilege level. The company also offers a 24/7 MDR service with expert human analysts and a new intelligent assistant, ThreatDown AI, which turns complex security data into plain-language guidance and recommended actions. These capabilities address the threats tracked in ThreatDown's report, "Cybercrime in the age of AI," which found over 6,000 AI models published openly and downloaded millions of times, highlighting the need for visibility into AI activity and machine identities.
