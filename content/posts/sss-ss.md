The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has been criticized for its attempt to obtain a Canadian man's location information, activity logs, and other identifying data from Google. The request was made after the man posted online criticism of the Trump administration's immigration policies, specifically in response to the killings of two individuals by federal immigration agents. The man's lawyers argue that the DHS has overstepped its authority, as the man has not entered the US in over a decade and the request was made under a customs summons, which is typically used to investigate issues related to importing goods and collecting customs duties.

The customs summons, a type of administrative subpoena, was issued without judicial review, and Google was asked not to disclose the request to the man. However, Google alerted the man to the request, and he has since filed a lawsuit against the DHS, alleging that the agency violated customs law. The man's lawyers contend that the request was made in response to his online activities, which included posts condemning immigration enforcement agents, and that the DHS is using the fact that big tech companies are based in the US to request information that would otherwise be outside of its jurisdiction.

The use of administrative subpoenas by the DHS has raised concerns among tech companies and civil liberties advocates, who argue that the agency is abusing its authority to unmask users who are critical of its policies. The Electronic Frontier Foundation has sued the DHS to obtain records about the number of subpoenas issued, and a group of US congressmembers has asked tech leaders for data on how many requests their companies have received. The incident has also sparked criticism from former law enforcement officials, who argue that the abuse of authority undermines the legitimacy of the DHS's activities.

The case highlights the tension between the government's need to investigate and enforce laws, and the need to protect individuals' privacy and free speech rights. The use of administrative subpoenas to target individuals who are critical of government policies raises concerns about the potential for abuse of power and the erosion of civil liberties. As the lawsuit against the DHS proceeds, it is likely to shed more light on the agency's use of administrative subpoenas and the impact on individuals' rights.
