Keepnet, an Extended Human Risk Management (xHRM) platform, has contributed voice and SMS phishing simulation data to the 2026 Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report (DBIR). This is the first time the DBIR has included voice and SMS phishing simulation data at this scale, revealing a 40% increase in the median click rate between phone-centric and email-based simulations. The report shows that phone-centric phishing simulations have a median click rate of 2%, compared to 1.4% for email simulations, highlighting the growing threat of voice and SMS phishing attacks.

The rise of voice and SMS phishing can be attributed to several factors, including the increasing accessibility of voice cloning technology, the shift from personal to corporate targets, and the mainstream adoption of pretexting tactics. Attackers are now using a combination of voice, SMS, and email in coordinated campaigns, making it difficult for traditional email-only awareness programs to detect. The DBIR data suggests that phone-centric phishing simulations are more effective than email-based simulations, with attackers exploiting the synchronous nature of voice and SMS attacks to bypass email-only defenses.

The implications of this trend are significant for security teams, who must adapt their awareness programs to include voice and SMS phishing simulations. The cost of not doing so can be substantial, with recent high-profile incidents resulting in significant financial losses. Keepnet's contribution to the DBIR highlights the importance of measuring and benchmarking voice and SMS phishing exposure, and the company is offering personalized assessments to help security teams evaluate their resilience to these types of attacks. As AI-powered attacks become more prevalent, security teams must prioritize verification procedures, repetition under realistic conditions, and comprehensive simulations that include voice and SMS phishing scenarios.
