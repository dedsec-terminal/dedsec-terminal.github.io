Dashlane, a password manager, has disclosed a brute-force attack by an unknown party that targeted certain user accounts. The attack aimed to break two-factor authentication (2FA) protections, allowing the attackers to register new devices on existing accounts. As a result of the high volume of attempts, Dashlane's security controls triggered temporary account suspensions and authentication issues.

The attack was partially successful, with the attackers able to download encrypted vaults belonging to fewer than 20 users on the personal subscription plan. Dashlane has directly notified these users, and those who have not received a notification are not affected. It's worth noting that the vault data is still encrypted and cannot be accessed without the Master Password, making it unlikely that the attackers can access the data unless the password is weak.

As a precautionary measure, Dashlane advises all users to review their account security. This includes checking registered devices and removing any unrecognized ones, enabling 2FA, and using a strong Master Password that is long, unique, and difficult to guess. Dashlane's internal systems were not impacted by the incident, and the company has taken steps to notify and protect affected users.
