Humanix has expanded its detection capabilities to identify live violations of security procedures, specifically those governing IT support workflows. This is in response to the growing threat of social engineering attacks, which target help desk and service desk agents to gain unauthorized access to sensitive information. According to Humanix CEO Keith Stewart, these agents are often placed in an impossible position, being expected to be helpful while also avoiding aiding potential attackers.

The threat of social engineering attacks is significant, with 76% of modern breaches targeting humans. Attackers use tactics such as vishing to exploit support and service personnel, creating high-stakes, artificial crises that can cause established procedures to break down. Humanix's detection and response platform aims to close this gap by identifying impersonation attempts, detecting manipulation tactics, and flagging when an agent is coerced into violating their organization's procedures. This allows for intervention before procedural failures become breaches.

Humanix's platform provides visibility into critical human and AI agent interactions, continuous detection and response coverage, and control of the human attack surface. In a common scenario, an attacker may pose as a new contractor and pressure the help desk agent into skipping required security steps. Humanix flags this moment, live, allowing for intervention before access is granted. By providing this capability, Humanix helps organizations protect themselves against social engineering attacks and prevent breaches. The platform covers voice, chat, email, and ticket interactions, providing comprehensive protection against human-centered attacks.
