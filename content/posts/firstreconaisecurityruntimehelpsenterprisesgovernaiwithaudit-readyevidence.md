First Recon AI has launched its AI Security Runtime, a platform designed to help enterprises govern and secure their use of artificial intelligence (AI) across the organization. The platform inspects every AI interaction, applies policy inline, and records every decision as audit-ready evidence, enabling companies to adopt AI quickly and confidently. This is particularly important as AI is increasingly used in various aspects of business, from employee interactions to autonomous agents, and sensitive data is often involved.

The current security tools used by enterprises are inadequate for governing AI, as they were built for files, email, and networks, and cannot read AI prompts or judge intent. First Recon's AI Security Runtime fills this gap with four key functions: observing activity, detecting sensitive data and threats, enforcing decisions inline, and tracing every decision as sealed evidence. The platform's proprietary Semantic Security Engine reads the meaning, intent, and context of every interaction, providing more accurate detection and control. This allows companies to put AI to work while staying in control and meeting compliance requirements.

First Recon's AI Security Runtime is delivered in two ways: as an endpoint agent for organizations requiring high levels of control, and as a secure AI workspace for chat, agents, and company knowledge. The platform provides coverage from device to model, with a single policy surface across all major AI providers. First Recon has already partnered with Conscia Group, a pan-European leader in cybersecurity and managed IT services, to provide AI control and security to its customers. The platform's ability to produce an audit trail is particularly valuable, as it enables companies to prove their AI control to regulators and meet compliance requirements.
