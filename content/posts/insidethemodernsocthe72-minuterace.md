**The Speed Gap in Modern Security Operations**
The modern Security Operations Center (SOC) faces a significant challenge: the speed gap. Attackers are using AI to automate and accelerate their attacks, with some moving from initial access to data exfiltration in as little as 72 minutes. This compressed timeline outpaces traditional security operations, which often rely on manual triage and fragmented workflows. The speed gap is a process problem, not a personnel problem, and requires a fundamental shift in how security operations function.

**Anatomy of a Modern Identity-Driven Attack**
Modern attacks often involve compromised credentials, identity manipulation, and rapid lateral movement. Threat actors use tactics such as MFA manipulation, help-desk impersonation, and privilege escalation to gain access and move quickly through an organization's systems. The attack timeline has compressed, with attackers often achieving their objectives in hours or minutes. The Unit 42 Global Incident Response Report found that 65% of initial access is driven by identity-based techniques, and attackers are increasingly moving across multiple environments, including identity, endpoint, cloud, and Software as a Service (SaaS).

**Closing the Speed Gap**
To close the speed gap, SOC leaders must re-engineer their security operations to match the velocity of modern threats. This requires shifting from sequential workflows to automated correlation and response, correlating related signals across multiple sources, and operationalizing response actions for common attack scenarios. By prioritizing behavior over indicators and using AI-driven automation, organizations can accelerate detection, investigation, and containment.
