The increasing use of agentic AI, where agents carry out tasks on behalf of humans, has introduced new digital security risks. To address these risks, the FIDO Alliance, with contributions from Google and Mastercard, is launching two working groups to develop industry standards for validating and protecting transactions carried out by AI agents. The goal is to create a protective baseline that can be adopted across industries, allowing users to authorize agent actions using secure mechanisms that can't be easily phished or taken over by bad actors.

The standards will include cryptographic tools to confirm that agents are accurately carrying out authenticated instructions, as well as privacy-preserving frameworks to give users, merchants, and service providers the ability to validate transactions initiated by agents. This will help prevent agent hijacking and provide transparency and accountability in the event of a dispute. The FIDO Alliance's CEO, Andrew Shikiar, notes that existing security models weren't designed for agentic interactions, and it's essential to establish foundational principles for trusted interactions.

Google and Mastercard are contributing open-source tools to the initiative, including Google's Agent Payments Protocol (AP2) and Mastercard's Verifiable Intent framework. These tools provide mechanisms for cryptographically verifying user intent and authorizing agent actions while keeping sensitive information private. The goal is to provide a secure and transparent way for users to interact with AI agents, such as when purchasing products online. For example, if a user instructs an AI agent to buy a pair of sneakers when they become available at a certain price, the standards will ensure that the transaction is authenticated and transparent.

The development of these standards is urgent, given the rapid advancement and adoption of agentic AI. The FIDO Alliance, Google, and Mastercard emphasize that the process must move quickly to establish a foundation for trusted interactions. While the contributed tools will give the working groups a head start, they will still need to build out practical examples and use cases to ensure the tech works in real-life scenarios. The ultimate goal is to promote trust in agentic AI and ensure that users, merchants, and service providers can adopt and support the protocols at scale.

The success of this effort is crucial, as the cost of supporting exploited AI agents can be very high.
