A 21-year-old hacker known as "Snoopy" has been sentenced to 18 months in prison for his role in a scheme that compromised 60,000 betting accounts on a fantasy sports and betting website. Nathan Austad of Minnesota pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy to commit computer intrusion in December 2025. The scheme, which occurred in November 2022, involved a credential stuffing attack using stolen usernames and passwords to gain unauthorized access to user accounts.

The attack resulted in the theft of approximately $600,000 from around 1,600 accounts. Austad and his co-conspirators not only stole funds but also sold access to the compromised accounts on websites that traffic in stolen accounts, known as "Shops." Austad controlled his own shop, named after the Peanuts character Snoopy, and exchanged messages with co-conspirators discussing the investigation and acknowledging the fraudulent nature of their activities.

Austad is the third defendant to be sentenced in the case, following Joseph Garrison and Kamerin Stokes, who received 18 months and 30 months in prison, respectively. Authorities reported that Austad controlled cryptocurrency accounts that received around $465,000 worth of cryptocurrency, including proceeds from the scheme. The affected betting website was not named by the Justice Department, but the details of the incident match a November 2022 incident disclosed by DraftKings involving the compromise of about 68,000 customer accounts.
