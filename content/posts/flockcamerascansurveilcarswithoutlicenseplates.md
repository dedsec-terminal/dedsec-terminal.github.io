Flock Cameras, a surveillance technology, can track cars without license plates by creating a "Vehicle Fingerprint" based on unique features such as decals, bumper stickers, and roof racks. This allows law enforcement officials to gather information about a vehicle even when the license plate is not visible. The company's system also enables officers to search for and locate multiple vehicles that are believed to be traveling together, using a feature called "multi geo search".

This type of surveillance is not new, as similar techniques have been used in the past, such as tracking cell phone location data to identify phones that are frequently near each other. In fact, Edward Snowden revealed in 2014 that the NSA was using this method to track phones. Moreover, anyone with access to cell phone location data can potentially use this technique to gather information about individuals or groups.

The scope of surveillance extends beyond cell phones, as other devices such as laptops, tablets, wearables, and even payment cards can be tracked and cross-linked using software. This has been possible since the turn of the century, and companies have been using this data to compile reports on user behavior. As one expert notes, this type of data can be valuable not only for investigators but also for prosecutors, and raises concerns about privacy and the potential for misuse of this information.
