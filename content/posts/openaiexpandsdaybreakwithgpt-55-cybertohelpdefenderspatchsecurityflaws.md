OpenAI has announced the expansion of its Daybreak initiative with the release of an improved version of its GPT-5.5-Cyber model. This model is designed to help defenders patch security flaws by identifying vulnerabilities, validating them, and developing patches. The model can analyze large codebases, identify security issues, and generate reports with severity, affected code locations, and remediation guidance. Additionally, OpenAI is releasing an update to the Codex Security plugin to speed up the process of discovering and patching vulnerabilities.

The release of GPT-5.5-Cyber and the updated Codex Security plugin is part of OpenAI's effort to help secure open-source projects through its new initiative, Patch the Planet. This initiative, launched in partnership with Trail of Bits, aims to reduce the burden on maintainers by providing security engineers with the tools and resources needed to review and validate findings, develop patches, and build reusable vulnerability discovery workflows. Initial participants in Patch the Planet include several high-profile open-source projects, such as cURL, Python, and Mozilla Firefox.

The launch of Patch the Planet and the updated GPT-5.5-Cyber model comes as the cybersecurity landscape is rapidly evolving, with advanced AI models accelerating vulnerability discovery and exploitation. Cyber experts have raised concerns that AI models can be used by bad actors to take advantage of security vulnerabilities, forcing the industry to plug holes almost as soon as they are discovered. OpenAI's initiatives aim to provide defenders with the tools and resources needed to stay ahead of these threats and improve the overall security of open-source projects. The company has already seen success with its Daybreak initiative, which has helped surface numerous vulnerabilities across various operating systems and web browsers.
