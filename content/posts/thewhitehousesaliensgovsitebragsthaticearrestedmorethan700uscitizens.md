The White House has launched a website called Aliens.gov, which claims to provide information about Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) arrests. However, the site has been criticized for dehumanizing immigrants and comparing them to extraterrestrials. According to the site, ICE has arrested almost half a million people in nearly 12,000 cities and towns in the US. But in 715 of these locations, at least one of the people arrested is identified as a US-born citizen, and in 83 locations, every single arrestee is reported to be an American.

The website includes information about alleged criminal offenses for each location, but in over one-fifth of the locations, no criminal charges are recorded. The site also maps Puerto Rico, a US territory, as a separate jurisdiction and lists it as a foreign country. The White House has updated the data on the site, which initially included non-immigration arrests, but the updated data still shows that ICE has arrested many US citizens. This contradicts the Trump administration's claim that ICE is targeting the "worst of the worst" immigrants.

The website has been widely criticized for its misleading and inaccurate information. The "encounters" counter on the site is fake, and the starting number is hand-typed into the website. The site also uses a copyrighted music track from the X-Files theme song without permission. The use of this track has raised questions about copyright infringement, and the White House has not responded to questions about how it obtained the files. The website has been seen as a piece of political theater aimed at dehumanizing immigrants, and its accuracy and credibility have been widely questioned.
