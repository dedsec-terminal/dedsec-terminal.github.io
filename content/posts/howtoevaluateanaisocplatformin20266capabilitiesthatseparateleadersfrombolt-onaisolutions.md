Evaluating an AI SOC (Security Operations Center) platform can be challenging due to the numerous vendors offering similar solutions. However, it's essential to look beyond the labels and focus on the capabilities that separate leaders from bolt-on AI solutions. An AI SOC platform is a security operations platform where AI agents perform the core work of the SOC, including detection, triage, investigation, and response, under human oversight. This differs from bolt-on AI, which only summarizes alerts within an existing SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) system.

To evaluate an AI SOC platform, it's crucial to assess six key capabilities: a real-time, correlated data foundation; full-lifecycle agents; evidence-backed, auditable verdicts; detection coverage beyond the SIEM; staged autonomy with human oversight; and measurable outcomes. These capabilities can be tested during a proof of concept or demo, and they should be able to demonstrate predictable and trustworthy automation. A platform with a real-time knowledge graph, layered model architecture, and continuous threat hunting can provide consistent and evidence-backed verdicts, unlike bolt-on AI solutions that often fail to hold up under scrutiny.

Exaforce's Agentic SOC Platform is an example of a solution that incorporates these capabilities. Its four Exabots cover the full SOC lifecycle, from detection to response, and reason over a unified real-time data platform. The platform has delivered measurable outcomes for customers, such as reducing investigation time by 95% and achieving a 14-minute mean time to respond on P0 incidents. While no platform can make the modern SOC a solved problem, Exaforce's approach demonstrates the potential of AI SOC platforms to revolutionize security operations. By focusing on the six key capabilities and evaluating vendors based on these criteria, organizations can make informed decisions when selecting an AI SOC platform.
