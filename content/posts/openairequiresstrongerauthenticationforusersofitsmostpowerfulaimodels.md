OpenAI, a leading AI research and development company, is now requiring stronger authentication for users of its most powerful AI models. As of June 1, 2026, individuals in the Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC) program will be required to enable Advanced Account Security (AAS) using passkeys, such as hardware security keys. This mandate is a significant step towards enhancing security posture and setting a new industry precedent for secure authentication.

The use of passkeys, particularly hardware-backed passkeys like YubiKeys, provides a higher level of protection against phishing and other security threats. Yubico, a partner of OpenAI, is playing a crucial role in securing the AI frontier by providing phishing-resistant authentication solutions. The company's hardware security keys, such as YubiKeys, offer a physical "tap" that acts as a vital circuit breaker, ensuring that high-consequence AI actions are authorized by a verified human.

The partnership between Yubico and OpenAI aims to deliver hardware-backed security to the builders of the AI future. The mandate is a move away from "probabilistic" security, where passwords are relied upon, to a more secure approach that utilizes cryptographic certainty provided by hardware. With features like zero-knowledge recovery and enterprise attestation, Yubico's solutions are designed to provide a robust security framework for OpenAI's TAC program, ensuring that users can maintain mission-critical access while minimizing the risk of security breaches.
