XM Cyber has introduced platform enhancements to help organizations reduce identity risk, particularly in the face of AI-enabled attackers. The new capabilities focus on continuous exposure management, enabling teams to identify and right-size access permissions in accordance with least privilege access policies. This is crucial as excessive permissions are a common technique used in breaches and lateral movement across hybrid-cloud environments.

The enhancements provide granular visibility into access permissions and their actual usage, allowing teams to quickly identify unused elevated permissions across Active Directory, Entra, and cloud platforms. This information enables teams to remove unnecessary permissions, reducing the attack surface and improving risk posture without disrupting operations. The new capabilities include assessing Active Directory excessive permissions and Cloud Infrastructure Entitlement Management (CIEM), which evaluates entitlements for cloud entities to provide a comprehensive view of usage patterns.

By integrating permissions usage into XM Cyber's platform, security and identity teams can surface excessive permissions, validate them as part of an attack path, and revoke unused permissions to reduce attack surface risk. This provides critical context into how permissions are used across the enterprise, enabling teams to prioritize exposures and make informed decisions about remediation. The additional insight connects identity risk to the full range of exposures, giving organizations a clearer understanding of how identity-related issues contribute to real attack paths and where remediation will have the greatest impact.
