Conifers has launched its AI-powered Security Operations Center (SOC) platform, designed to unify security operations and automate response to cyber threats. The platform, built on Conifers' CognitiveSOC, connects threat intelligence, threat hunting, detection engineering, investigation, and remediation into a single framework. This launch comes as the cybersecurity industry faces a shift in how attacks are discovered and deployed, with AI accelerating the pace of vulnerability discovery and exploitation.

The traditional human-paced security operations model is no longer effective against the increasing volume and speed of attacks. Conifers' CEO, Tom Findling, notes that sophisticated AI models are already being used by attackers, enabling them to discover and weaponize vulnerabilities faster than ever before. The company's agentic SOC platform is designed to respond to this challenge by allowing every function to intercommunicate in real-time, collapsing fragmented silos and enabling automated response. The platform's agentic fabric enables various functions, including threat intelligence, threat hunting, detection engineering, investigation, and remediation, to work together seamlessly.

The agentic SOC platform operates on top of a customer's existing security stack, with over 60 integrations across various platforms, and can be onboarded in as little as two to four hours. The system provides transparent reasoning chains and defensible evidence trails for every action taken, allowing customers to define scope, authority, and operational guardrails. As confidence is established, the platform enables the transition from human-in-the-loop to human-on-the-loop security operations, allowing for more efficient and effective response to cyber threats. With its unified AI-driven approach, Conifers' agentic SOC aims to help security teams combat the increasingly sophisticated threats they face.
