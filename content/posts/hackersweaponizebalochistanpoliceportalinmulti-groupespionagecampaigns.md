Here is a summary of the article in three concise paragraphs:

Cybersecurity researchers have uncovered a sustained cyber espionage campaign targeting Pakistani law enforcement organizations, including the Balochistan Police, between February 2024 and April 2026. The campaign, attributed to suspected China- and India-aligned threat actors, compromised servers hosting web applications that manage police and citizen data, such as biometric records, criminal case files, and personnel records. The attackers targeted network appliances and servers hosting web applications, including the Complaint Management System (CMS), which serves police staff and citizens.

The campaign involved four different threat clusters, each deploying a unique malware family: PlugX, ShadowPad, Cobalt Strike, and Remcos RAT. The use of PlugX and ShadowPad is traditionally associated with Chinese nation-state hacking groups, while the Remcos RAT has been linked to an India-nexus threat actor. The attackers used lures related to Pakistani law enforcement, displaying a decoy document that purports to contain an operational plan for the repatriation of illegal foreigners. The compromised infrastructure included servers hosting web applications, network appliances, and a Fortinet FortiMail appliance that served as the agency's primary inbound email gateway.

The compromise of the Complaint Management System web application allowed the attackers to extend their reach beyond the initially compromised environment, turning a tool built to make policing in Pakistan more accessible and accountable to the public into a malware delivery mechanism. The campaign is notable for drawing both a "partner and an adversary of Pakistan" to the same victim for intelligence gathering, likely fueled by geopolitical motives. The researchers believe that the convergence of multiple cyberespionage actors operating against law enforcement institutions of a single state is a signal of target value, highlighting the importance of these institutions in holding the government's internal security picture.
