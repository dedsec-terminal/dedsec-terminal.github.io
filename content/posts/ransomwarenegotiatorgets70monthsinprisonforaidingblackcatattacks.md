A 41-year-old former ransomware negotiator, Angelo Martino, has been sentenced to 70 months in prison for conspiring with the BlackCat ransomware operators to extort multiple victims. Martino pleaded guilty to one count of conspiring to interfere with interstate commerce through extortion and worked as a negotiator for five different ransomware victims. However, he secretly provided the BlackCat attackers with confidential information about the victims' negotiating positions and insurance policy limits, allowing the operators to maximize the ransom amounts.

Martino's actions were described by federal prosecutors as those of a "double agent" who worked to maximize harm to his clients and financial gain for the cybercriminals. He was accused of colluding with two other cybersecurity professionals, Ryan Goldberg and Kevin Martin, to deploy BlackCat ransomware against multiple victims in the US. Both Goldberg and Martin were sentenced to four years in prison in May 2026 for their roles in the attacks. Martino's betrayal of his clients' trust had devastating consequences, with many businesses nearly destroyed by the ransomware attacks.

The Justice Department has seized $10 million in assets from Martino, including digital currency, vehicles, and luxury items purchased with illicit proceeds. Martino is expected to appear in court in September 2026 to determine the exact amount of restitution to be ordered against him. The case highlights the importance of holding individuals accountable for their roles in facilitating ransomware attacks and the need for vigilance in protecting against insider threats. The FBI and Justice Department have sent a clear message that they will pursue not only the hackers who deploy ransomware but also the insiders who enable them and profit from their crimes.
