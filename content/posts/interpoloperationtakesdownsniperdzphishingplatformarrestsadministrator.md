An INTERPOL-led operation, codenamed Operation Ramz, has successfully disrupted the Sniper Dz phishing-as-a-service (PhaaS) platform, which had been active since at least 2015. The operation, which took place between October 2025 and February 2026, involved authorities from 13 countries in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region, resulting in 201 arrests. The primary developer and administrator of Sniper Dz, known as Guedz, was among those arrested by the Algerian National Police.

Sniper Dz, which had rebranded itself as Joker Dz, Storm Dz, and Spam Dz over the years, offered a sophisticated PhaaS service that provided ready-made phishing kits, hosting infrastructure, and operational support to cybercriminals. The platform had collected over 45,000 victim records and had targeted 30 major global organizations, including PayPal, Facebook, and Netflix, using 80 phishing templates in five languages. The phishing campaigns used convincing imitation websites to harvest credentials, personal information, and other sensitive data from users of technology, social media, and streaming platforms.

The takedown of Sniper Dz is significant, as it was a major player in the PhaaS market, offering its infrastructure for free and making it easier for aspiring cybercriminals to launch phishing campaigns at scale. The platform's monetization avenues relied on credential theft and victim traffic, with stolen credentials being harvested through phishing campaigns and users being redirected to carrier billing fraud, premium SMS subscriptions, and other affiliate-driven scam campaigns. The disruption of Sniper Dz is a major blow to cybercriminals and a significant victory for law enforcement agencies in the fight against phishing and cybercrime.
