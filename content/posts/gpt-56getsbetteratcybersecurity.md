OpenAI has introduced the GPT-5.6 series models, which include Sol, Terra, and Luna, with improved capabilities in coding, biology, and cybersecurity. The rollout is currently limited to a small group of trusted partners through the API and Codex, with plans to expand to ChatGPT, Codex, and API users in the coming weeks. The GPT-5.6 series features a robust safety stack, with strengthened protections for higher-risk activity, sensitive cyber requests, and repeated misuse.

The GPT-5.6 Sol model introduces improved agentic capabilities, including max reasoning effort and ultra mode, which uses subagents to speed up complex tasks. In cybersecurity, the model advances the performance-efficiency frontier on long-horizon security tasks, including vulnerability research and exploitation. OpenAI has also developed safeguards tailored to each model's capabilities, aiming to make prohibited offensive activity more difficult, uncertain, and detectable while preserving legitimate uses.

To test the models' safeguards, OpenAI conducted automated and human red teaming, exploring attack patterns and identifying failure patterns earlier. The company worked with third-party experts, including AI security lab Irregular, to evaluate GPT-5.6 Sol on real-world offensive security benchmarks. The results showed that the model performs slightly better than its predecessor, GPT-5.5, particularly on longer, more complex hacking tasks, and discovered previously unknown vulnerabilities in widely used software and mobile devices. However, it continues to struggle with well-defended targets and complete end-to-end attacks.
