An Algerian national, Abdellah Belmili, has been extradited to the US from Spain to face bank fraud conspiracy charges. Belmili is accused of running online marketplaces, including Market0Day and Spoxy.us, which sold phishing kits, stolen financial data, and other cybercrime-related services. He allegedly accepted payment exclusively in Bitcoin and promoted his platforms through a Telegram channel.

The investigation into Belmili's activities began in September 2020, when FBI agents discovered Market0Day. They made undercover purchases from the platform, obtaining a phishing kit designed to impersonate JPMorgan Chase and accessing a compromised cPanel account. However, when customers began complaining about undelivered products, Belmili directed them to a new platform, Spoxy.us. The Justice Department alleges that Belmili defrauded multiple financial institutions, including American Express, Bank of America, and Wells Fargo, resulting in approximately $900,000 in deposits to an account controlled by him.

Belmili faces up to 30 years in prison if convicted. The case highlights the FBI's efforts to combat cybercrime, with Acting Special Agent-in-Charge Brendan Dunford stating that the arrest proves that cybercriminals cannot hide behind usernames and encrypted platforms. In a separate case, the US government seized a cloud computing account linked to the Huione Group, a Cambodia-based conglomerate accused of providing money laundering services to cybercriminals and scam operations. The seizure is part of a broader effort to disrupt money laundering and cybercrime activities.
