The ShinyHunters hacking group had gone quiet after a significant ransom payment, specifically the Instructure ransom, two weeks prior. This silence was anticipated, as groups like ShinyHunters often lay low after a major heist to avoid heat, only to resurface later. The group's inactivity was short-lived, as they have now claimed new victims.

ShinyHunters has announced three new targets: a US-based dental benefits administrator and oral health company, Charter Communications, Inc., a US telecommunications and cable company, and another unidentified company. One of the affected companies, DentaQuest, had its website taken down and is currently displaying an "Access Denied" message. Although DentaQuest's website has been removed from the list, the incident raises concerns about the company's potential ransom payment and the resulting optics.

The recent activities of ShinyHunters suggest that ransom payments may be becoming a common outcome in such incidents. The group's resurgence and claims of new victims indicate that the hacking community remains active, and companies must be vigilant to protect themselves from cyber threats. The broken website and potential ransom payment of DentaQuest serve as a reminder of the importance of robust cybersecurity measures to prevent and respond to such incidents.
