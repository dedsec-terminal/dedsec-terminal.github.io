The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has introduced a new nomination form that allows researchers, vendors, and industry partners to report known exploited vulnerabilities. This form provides a direct way for outside contributors to submit vulnerabilities for possible inclusion in CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog. Additionally, email submissions are still accepted at vulnerability@cisa.dhs.gov for those who prefer this method.

The new reporting capability aims to enhance CISA's ability to identify, validate, and share critical threat information. According to Chris Butera, CISA's Acting Executive Assistant Director for Cybersecurity, early detection and coordinated vulnerability disclosure are crucial in reducing risk at scale. CISA encourages researchers and organizations to share vulnerability threats and help secure systems that Americans rely on daily.

The introduction of the nomination form is a response to criticism that CISA has been slow to add actively exploited bugs to the KEV catalog, which was launched in November 2021. The new form is expected to help the agency add vulnerabilities more quickly and keep the list current. Submissions must still meet the existing criteria, including an assigned CVE, confirmed exploitation, and remediation guidance, to be considered for inclusion in the KEV catalog.
