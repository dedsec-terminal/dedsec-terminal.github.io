The United Arab Emirates has strict cybercrime laws that can lead to severe penalties, including imprisonment and fines, for spreading false information or engaging in other forms of online misconduct. These laws are particularly stringent during times of crisis, emergency, or disaster, with penalties doubling to a minimum of two years in prison and 200,000 UAE dirhams. The laws are not new, but rather a pre-existing framework that is triggered during times of crisis, and they cover a wide range of online activities, including the spread of misinformation, online defamation, and privacy violations.

The recent conflict in the region has highlighted the importance of these laws, with authorities announcing arrests linked to misleading videos, AI-generated clips, and the spread of misinformation. Legal consultant Ahmed Elnaggar notes that the rationale for these arrests is consistent with the existing framework, which assesses content not only for its accuracy but also for its potential impact on stability, security, and public perception. This means that even seemingly harmless online activities, such as sharing a screenshot or forwarding a message, can have serious legal implications.

The law does not distinguish between formal publication and informal sharing, and intent is not always the deciding factor. A screenshot can become legally problematic if it exposes private communications without consent, distorts the context of what was said, or contributes to reputational harm. Similarly, forwarding a message is not a neutral act and can be subject to the same scrutiny as original posting. The legal definition of media activity is broad enough to capture not only original creators but also anyone who participates in the circulation of content.

The consequences of violating these laws can be severe, with penalties ranging from fines to imprisonment. In some cases, foreign nationals can face deportation. It is essential for residents, tourists, and anyone using a smartphone in the UAE to be aware of these laws and to exercise caution when engaging in online activities. This includes being mindful of the sources of information, avoiding the spread of misinformation, and being careful when sharing or forwarding content. By understanding the laws and taking necessary precautions, individuals can minimize their risk of facing legal consequences.
