**Introduction to Machine Identities**
Machine identities, also known as non-human identities (NHIs), are becoming the majority of "things with access" in modern infrastructure. These identities, including service accounts, service principals, and workload roles, authenticate using credentials like access keys, secrets, and tokens. Many of these identities hold privileges equal to or greater than human admins, making them a hidden layer of privileged access that often goes unmanaged.

**The Risks of Unmanaged Machine Identities**
Unmanaged machine identities create significant risks, including outdated credentials, unclear ownership, and privilege creep. The sprawl of machine identities across cloud stores and vaults makes it difficult to manage them, leading to familiar risks. Delinea is focused on turning machine identity risk into something manageable through visibility, posture, and control. A practical machine identity program involves visibility into machine identities, posture assessment of risk, and control with context by vaulting and governing credentials, rotating them as needed, and enforcing least privilege.

**Managing Machine Identities**
To manage machine identities effectively, it's essential to have a simple and repeatable approach: Visibility → Posture → Control. This involves discovering machine identities, assessing their risk, and controlling their access. Delinea helps teams move from finding risks to fixing them using this practical flow. The company provides visibility into machine identities, credentials, and operational context, helping teams prioritize issues that move risk the fastest. By managing machine identities with the same discipline as human identities, organizations can close one of the biggest remaining gaps in identity security and reduce the risk of compromise and data breaches.
