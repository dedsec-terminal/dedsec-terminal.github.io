OpenAI has launched a full-scale effort to patch open-source bugs, dubbed "Patch the Planet", in collaboration with Trail of Bits and other security firms. The project aims to provide free security consulting services to open-source maintainers to help them find and patch vulnerabilities, strengthen their code bases, and incorporate AI security tools into their development process. This effort comes as the AI industry advances, leaving critical open-source projects at risk of falling behind and vulnerable to hacking.

The Patch the Planet project has already begun working with over 30 open-source projects, with more in the pipeline, and has uncovered hundreds of bugs and produced dozens of patches in its first week. The project's goal is to provide individualized support to as many open-source projects as possible, improving both their current security and long-term resilience. OpenAI's cyber tech lead, Fouad Matin, notes that the project aims to reduce the burden on maintainers, who are often volunteers struggling to keep up with bug reports, by making it more efficient to validate potential reports, create patches, and land them.

The launch of Patch the Planet comes as OpenAI competes with Anthropic in the AI cybersecurity space, with both companies preparing for IPOs. OpenAI's announcements, including the improved GPT-5.5-Cyber model, are part of its limited Trusted Access for Cyber program and do not involve a public release. The company's efforts are also timely, given the growing concerns about AI hacking capabilities and the recent warning from the Five Eyes intelligence alliance that "frontier AI models" will fundamentally transform both offensive and defensive cyber capabilities in a matter of months.
