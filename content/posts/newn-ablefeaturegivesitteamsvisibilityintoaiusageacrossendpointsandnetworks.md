N-able has introduced a new feature called Shadow AI Visibility, which provides IT teams with visibility into AI usage across endpoints and networks. This capability is available across N-able's Unified Endpoint Management (UEM) solutions, N-central and N-sight, and its Security Operations platform, Adlumin. It helps organizations identify, classify, and monitor AI tool usage, addressing a growing operational and security blind spot as AI adoption accelerates.

The Shadow AI Visibility feature is designed to help organizations address the challenge of "shadow AI" activity, where employees use AI-powered applications and tools outside traditional governance processes. This can introduce security, compliance, and operational challenges when organizations lack visibility into which tools are being used, by whom, and where. According to a Gartner survey, 69% of organizations suspect or have evidence that employees are using prohibited public GenAI. Shadow AI Visibility extends N-able's AI-powered cybersecurity solutions, providing customers with a clearer inventory of AI tools in use and enabling them to take a more informed approach to AI governance.

The Shadow AI Visibility feature delivers several key benefits, including visibility across endpoints and networks, classification and governance insights, identity and device attribution, and integrated workflows within N-central, N-sight, and Adlumin. This capability creates opportunities for managed service providers (MSPs) to support customers with AI governance initiatives, including usage assessments, risk reviews, compliance reporting, and policy recommendations. By providing a starting point for developing AI governance strategies, Shadow AI Visibility helps customers establish an inventory of AI tools and usage patterns without adding operational complexity.
