A data breach at Paidwork, a platform that pays users for completing online microtasks, has exposed the sensitive data of over 23 million users. The breach, which occurred in March 2026, was discovered when a leaked database surfaced on a cybercrime forum in April. The database, which is approximately 11GB in size, contains a wide range of user data, including full names, email addresses, phone numbers, and home addresses.

The exposed data also includes more sensitive information such as dates of birth, gender, education levels, bank account numbers, and transaction records. Additionally, device and IP information, profile photos, personal interests, and passwords stored as bcrypt hashes were also compromised. Although bcrypt is a strong hashing algorithm, weak or commonly used passwords may still be vulnerable to cracking. The breach has significant implications for users, who may face financial and personal consequences as a result of the exposed data.

Paidwork has not publicly acknowledged the breach, and users are advised to take immediate action to protect themselves. This includes changing passwords, not just on Paidwork but also on any other accounts where the same password was used. Enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) and using a password manager to generate and store strong, unique passwords are also recommended. Users can check if their data has been compromised by using the Have I Been Pwned database, which added the breach to its records on July 19.
