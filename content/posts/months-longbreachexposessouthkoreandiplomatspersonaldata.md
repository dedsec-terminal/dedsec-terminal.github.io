The South Korean Foreign Ministry has disclosed a months-long breach of the Korea National Diplomatic Academy's online education system, which compromised personal data of current and former ministry staff and diplomats. The online platform, launched in 2022, was used for remote learning and job training for diplomatic personnel. The breach, which started in April 2025 and continued until February 2026, exposed usernames, names, email addresses, and encrypted passwords.

The leaked data did not include sensitive information such as resident registration numbers, phone numbers, home addresses, and photos. However, it is estimated that around 10,000 current and former diplomats and officials may have been affected. The ministry has taken the affected system offline and is implementing additional security measures to prevent further damage. The government has warned individuals to exercise caution when receiving emails from unknown sources and has advised those with privacy concerns to contact the ministry's security department.

The breach is believed to have been carried out using a zero-day vulnerability, which is consistent with tactics previously linked to North Korean state-backed hacking groups. However, South Korean officials have not attributed the attack and are still conducting a technical analysis. The ministry delayed announcing the breach for five months due to the sensitivity of the matter and the need for careful review and analysis. Officials have dismissed speculation that the delay was tied to unrelated diplomatic developments, citing the technical complexity of the investigation and the need to coordinate with other government agencies.
