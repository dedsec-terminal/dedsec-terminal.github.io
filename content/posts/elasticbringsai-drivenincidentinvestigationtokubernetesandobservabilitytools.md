Elastic has introduced a new feature that brings AI-driven incident investigation to Kubernetes and observability tools. This feature automatically starts an investigation when an alert is triggered, identifying the root cause, gathering evidence, and providing recommended next steps before an engineer even opens the alert. This aims to close the gap between alert and answer, saving time and reducing the burden on on-call engineers.

The Elastic Observability platform builds on Kubernetes dashboards, prebuilt alert templates, and machine learning-powered anomaly detection to accelerate incident resolution. It offers two ways to investigate incidents: an agentic investigation workflow that runs diagnostics automatically when alerts fire, and a Kubernetes MCP App that brings investigation capabilities into AI tools and IDEs. This allows engineers to investigate Kubernetes environments conversationally, with AI agents querying live data and surfacing interactive views directly in the tool.

The Elastic Observability MCP App provides engineers with access to a range of features, including cluster health rollups, service dependency graphs, and anomaly detail. Elasticsearch stores all Kubernetes logs and metrics at scale, ensuring engineers have the full operational context needed to investigate incidents. According to Bahaaldine Azarmi, GM of Observability at Elastic, this feature enables engineers to reach resolution faster and with more confidence, without having to start a new investigation from scratch. The goal is to provide answers, not just alerts, and to do so in a way that is seamless and intuitive for engineers.
